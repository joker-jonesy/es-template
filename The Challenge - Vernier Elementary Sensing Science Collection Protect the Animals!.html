The Challenge - Vernier Elementary Sensing Science Collection: Protect the Animals!

The Challenge

Your challenge is to design an adaptation to help the animals by keeping them warm during the cold winter months at your local zoo. You will use the Vernier Go Direct Temperature Probe to test and measure the success of your adaptation creations. The goal is to help protect the animals!

What I'll Learn 

  • I CAN use the Vernier Go Direct Temperature Probe with Graphical Analysis 4 software to measure changing temperature and graph various temperatures.
  • I CAN make a scaled bar graph.
  • I CAN analyze a scaled bar graph by comparing temperatures from different tests.
  • I CAN determine if there is a change in temperature between the habitat with and without the adaptation created and by how many degrees.
  • I CAN make an argument supported with evidence to justify how the animal adaptation I created will help animals survive.

Stuff I'll Need

  • Vernier Go Direct Temperature Probe
  • Computer, iPad, or Tablet with Graphical Analysis 4 Software 
  • Vernier Go Direct Temperature Probe Instruction
  • Vernier Graphical Analysis Instructional Manual
  • Download Vernier Graphical Analysis App from Google Play Store Here. If you have a Chromebook, you can use this link access the Graphical Analysis program without the app.
  • Materials for Adaptation:
    • Some materials from the Vernier Elementary Accessory Collection may include precut fabric, cotton balls, freezer bags, cups, and tape.
    • Other materials may include empty paper towel rolls, boxes, or cardboard pieces.
