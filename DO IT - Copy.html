Do It! K'NEX: Design and Test a Spinning Top

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build a K’NEX spinning top that will spin for the longest time possible (15 seconds or more). Then, analyze your data using place value to determine which design allowed your top to spin the longest.

 Project Steps

  1. Build Your Model

  2. Feel the Conservation of Angular Momentum

  3. The Spinning Top Challenge

  4. Analyze Your Data

Demonstrate Conservation of Angular Momentum

Have you ever wondered what makes a spinning top stay upright? Build this K'NEX model and experience the conservation of angular momentum yourself.

Build Your Model

Build a model that shows the conservation of angular momentum:

Step #1

Use four of the longest grey rectangle rods and attach them to a center circle connector.

Step #2

Connect six red rods and six yellow half-circle connectors as shown.

Step #3

Find another red rod and two tan clip connectors.

Step #4

Slide the red rod through the center and attach the clips to hold it in place.

Feel the Conservation of Angular Momentum 

Spin the model to experience the conservation of angular momentum.

Step #1

Hold the wheel in front of you with a hand on each end of the red axle.

Step #2

Lower your right hand while raising your left hand to tilt the wheel. How does that feel?

Step #3

Pay attention to how easy it is to tilt the wheel.

Step #4

Get the wheel spinning by twisting the red axle with your fingers.

Step #5

Hold the axle loosely enough so it continues to spin. If you have trouble doing this, try slipping drinking straws over the ends of the axle.

  • How does it feel?
  • Are you able to tilt the wheel smoothly?
  • Is there any resistance to the movement?
  • What you feel is the conservation of angular momentum.

The Spinning Top Challenge

Take the Spinning Top Challenge by designing a top that spins as long as possible. Conservation of angular momentum explains why a spinning top stays upright for so long. Friction is what causes the spinning top to slow down and eventually fall over.  

Your challenge is to use these concepts to design a top that spins for 15 seconds or longer.

  1. Using your K'NEX kit, build a simple top that you think will spin well (you might want to see the K'NEX Spinner example on page 3 of this K'NEX Instruction Manual.
  2. Complete the Spinning Tops Data Table to organize your data. Keep track of how long your top spins each time, and any changes that you made to your top design.
  3. Spin your top. Use a stopwatch to time how long it spins before it falls over.
  4. Now change something in the top design to make it spin longer. (Example: height, width, and weight). Spin your top and time it again. Keep notes to show what you decided to change, and how it affected the length of the following spin.
  5. Continue making changes to your best design until you can no longer find ways to improve its spin time. Keep track of each trial on your data sheet.
  6. Take pictures of your different tops and label them with their longest spin time.

Analyze Your Data

You will now analyze the data that you collected for each spin. You will use place value to determine which designs allowed your top to spin the longest. Then, complete the questions below on the Spinning Tops Data Table.

  1. Explain which trials had the least and greatest spin times. Describe why these may have produced the best or worst results.
  2. Write the longest spin time in base-ten numerals, number name, and expanded forms.
  3. Label the place values and write your shortest spin time in the correct places on the chart. 
  4. Write three comparison statements using your tops and their spin times.
  5. Put your spin times in order from least to greatest.

Example:

Top 1/Trial 1 spun for 13 seconds < Top 4/Trial 4 spun for 15 seconds.

Top 4/Trial 4 spun for a GREATER amount of time than Top 1/Trial 1.
