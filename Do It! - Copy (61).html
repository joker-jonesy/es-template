Do It!

Build Milo the Science Rover using LEGO WeDo 2.0. Then, program it to find and collect samples of natural material (plant, animal, rock, etc.) just like NASA’s Mars rovers.

Lego WeDo 2.0

Open the WeDo 2.0 application. Watch the video at the top of the home screen to learn how to use the software.

Making Milo

Start a new project by following the directions below.

1. Click on the book to start a new project.

2. Click on the books in the toolbar and select:  A. Milo the Science Rover.

3. Move forward of back through the activity using the arrows on the top of the page.

4. Do your best to answer the question using the documentation tool or your ePortfolio. If you do not know an answer, write how you could find the answer.

5. When it is time to build, use the arrows at the bottom to go step-by-step. 

Tip: Follow these directions to get back to this activity from WeDo2.0:

When you open WeDo2.0, it will display on your whole monitor. In order to view other items on your desktop:
(1) Hold down the Alt key and press Tab on your keyboard.


(2) A pop-up window will appear with all your open applications. Then just use your mouse to click between applications.

When you open WeDo2.0, it will display on your whole monitor. In order to view other items on your desktop:

Reflect

  • What was easy about building and programming Milo? What was hard? How did you get through the hard parts?
  • A rover is one technology for studying planets. What are other ways to study planets? What can rovers do that other technologies cannot?
  • Watch the latest video covering NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory's Curiosity Report (August 5, 2016): Four Years on Mars to learn about the places that the Curiosity Rover traveled on Mars and the natural materials that it discovered. As you can see, rovers can travel, identify, collect, study, document, and more. Besides studying planets, how else could rovers help scientists?
