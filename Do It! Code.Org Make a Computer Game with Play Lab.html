Do It! Code.org: Play Lab and Perimeter

The Challenge

Your challenge is to complete ten Play Lab puzzles and then create your own computer game that explores perimeter.

 Project Steps

  1. Tour Play Lab

  2. Choose a Theme in Code.org

  3. Complete the Puzzles

  4. Build a Math Animation

Tour Play Lab

Your screen will split into three main parts.

On the left is the area where you can see your program run. 

In the middle is the gray toolbar where you will find your blocks for programming.

On the right is your workspace. This is where you will move the blocks to build your program.

There is also a video with instructions and helpful hints.

Choose a Theme in Code.org

You can choose a theme by clicking on the images below or by going to the Code.org website.

Complete the Puzzles

You will be asked to complete each puzzle before you create your own game. 

Each puzzle will take you step-by-step through different programming commands.

For example, you will start with programming your actor to say "Hello!" and to move left or right. Then, you will build upon these programming skills. After you have successfully completed a puzzle, you will see: "Congratulations! You completed Puzzle 1."

Once you have completed all of the puzzles, you will be ready to create your own computer game.

Build a Math Animation

Now it is time to build your own math animation scene to help you explore the perimeter. You will end up with five actors. Each actor will make a different rectangle with a different perimeter.

    1. Select your first actor. Which block will you need?
    2. Select a background. Which block will you need?
    3. Start the actor in the upper left corner. Which block will you need? Why?
    4. Program the actor to start to move when clicked. Which block will you need?
    5. Program the actor to make a rectangular shape as it travels. Which block will you need? How many blocks will you need to make a rectangle? Why? Which direction will you select (up, down, left, right)?
    6. Actor one should make the largest rectangular perimeter possible. What will you set the pixels to so that the actor makes the largest possible perimeter? See the image to the right to help you think it through. 
    7. Program the actor to say its perimeter in pixels after it travels in a rectangular shape. For example, “My perimeter is 300 pixels.” Which block will you need to make the actor “speak”?
    8. Repeat the above steps for actor 1, 3, 4, and 5 using the clues below for the perimeter.
    • Actor two should make the smallest rectangular perimeter possible. Remember to consider the pixel choices (25, 50, 100, 200 or 400 pixels) when determining the smallest possible perimeter.
    • Actor three should make a rectangular perimeter that is ½ of the largest possible perimeter.
    • Actor four should make a rectangular perimeter that is double the smallest possible perimeter.
    • Actor five should make a rectangular perimeter of 1,000 pixels.
