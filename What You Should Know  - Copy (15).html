What You Should Know 

Beyond Our Solar System

Our solar system is only a tiny part of the universe. Beyond the Sun and its planets, we find many other amazing things. These things are called deep-sky objects because they are extremely far from us.

Stars

Our Sun is one of many stars in our universe. Stars are glowing balls of burning gas. They release heat and light into space. Things like planets often orbit around stars.

We see many other distant stars in the night sky—each with its own size and color. For example, our Sun is a very common type of yellow star. It is a pretty small star, but it could still hold about 1.3 million (1,300,000) Earths inside of it!

Nebulae

Stars are born from big clouds of gas and space dust called nebulae (neb-you-lie). One nebula (neb-you-la) can be home to many young stars. 

Galaxies

A lot of different stars, solar systems, and nebulae are attracted to each other by gravity. The force of gravity pulls them all closer together, forming groups of space objects and material. These massive groups are called galaxies.

Our own solar system is a part of the Milky Way galaxy. In fact, almost all of the twinkling lights you can see in the night sky are stars in our own galaxy. If you have a powerful telescope, you could also see galaxies beyond the Milky Way.

What Are Constellations?

Humans have been watching the sky for thousands of years. Over time, ancient peoples started finding shapes in the sky by connecting stars with imaginary lines. These shapes, or constellations, often look like animals, objects, or people (both mythical and real). People also noticed that these constellations move across the sky in predictable ways. This makes them very useful guides for activities like navigating or farming.

Today, we have 88 standard constellations used by astronomers around the world. They represent many different things, and each has its own history and story. Some examples include:

  • Ursa Major, the bear
  • Draco, the dragon
  • Sagittarius, the archer
  • Orion, the hunter
  • Pyxis, the compass
  • And many more!
