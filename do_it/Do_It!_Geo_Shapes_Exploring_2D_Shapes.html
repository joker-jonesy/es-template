Do It! Geometric Shapes: Exploring 2D Shapes and Area

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build and explore the properties of 2D shapes. Then, build different sized rectangles and find the area of each one.

 Project Steps

  1. Exploring Shapes
  2. Build 2D Shapes

  3. Describe Your 2D Shapes

  4. Build and Measure Rectangles

Exploring Shapes

Look at the objects around you.

Work with your partner to list some of the shapes you see. Which shapes can you find?

Find a 2D shape. How do you know it is a 2D shape?

Take pictures and write about your objects. List any similarities and differences between the shapes.

Build 2D Shapes

Now that you have explored shapes in your surroundings, you are ready for your first challenge. Use Geometric Shapes Building Set to build 2D polygons.

Here are some things to try:

  • Make shapes with different numbers of sides. What is the smallest number of sides you need to make a polygon?
  • Keep all the sides the same length. Which polygons can you make with sides of all the same length?
  • Use sides of different lengths. Which shapes can you make now?

Describe Your 2D Shapes

Describe some of your 2D shapes.

  • Take a photograph of each shape you build.
  • Make a list of the parts you used for each polygon. (Another group should be able to use your lists to collect the parts needed to create the same polygons.)  
  • Which struts work well for each shape?
  • Measure and record the length and width of your shape. (Remember your units.)
  • Name your shape.

Build and Measure Rectangles

Use the Geometric Shapes Building Set to make rectangles with different sizes.

  1. Make one rectangle. 

  2. Use your ruler. Measure the length and width of the rectangle in inches. Measure to the nearest whole inch. 

  3. Use the measurements to find the area of this rectangle in square inches.

  4. Attach a second rectangle to this first rectangle. How does this affect the area of the shape?

  5. Make a second rectangle. It should be a different size than the first one.

  6. Use your ruler. Measure the length and width of the rectangle in centimeters. Measure to the nearest whole centimeter. 

  7. Use the measurements to find the area of this rectangle in square centimeters.

  8. Attach three more rectangles to this first rectangle. How does this affect the area of the shape?

  9. Take a picture of both rectangles along with the measurements and areas.

  10. Make more rectangles of different sizes. Find the area of each one in inches or centimeters.

  11. Solve these problems:

    • What is the longest rectangle you can make? What is the area?
    • What is the shortest rectangle you can make? What is the area?

  12. Take pictures to show your work.
