Do It! Padcaster: If I Could Make A Video For A Younger Me

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create a stop motion short film to teach your younger-self an important lesson. Graph and compare movie speed and frames per second to narrow down how you would like to combine and edit your video footage using Stop Motion Studio Pro.

 Getting Started

  • Gather any materials you need for your stop motion video and images.
  • Set-up Padcaster Studio Kit equipment using the guided instructions or the information found in the Information About Padcaster & Stop Motion Studio Pro.
  • Review the Stop Motion Studio Pro information page to answer any additional questions.

 Project Steps

  1. Plan

  2. Record

  3. Edit & Share

Plan

Choose an important lesson you would like to share with yourself when you were in kindergarten or first grade. Ideas include never talking to strangers, bicycle safety, keeping money safe, being kind to friends, or another topic you can come up with to share. Create a storyboard for your stop motion video.

 You can use this template or create one of your own.

Record

Using your storyboard, Padcaster Studio Kit, and materials, you can now record your clips and take pictures of your scenes. A few things to consider before you begin recording include the lighting, location of the camera, whether to use the tripod, or if you will be in motion, in addition to anything else you can think of during this process.

Edit & Share

Before editing your video, you will graph and compare the movie speed and frames per second to determine which option you want to use for your video. Refer back to the Proportional Relationship section for additional guidance.

Add annotations, or notes, to your graph to record your feedback about that movie speed. Was it too fast or too slow for the message and events in your movie?

Using the Stop Motion Studio Pro app, combine and edit your images and videos to create your stop motion short film. Part of the editing process is determining the best speed and frames per second. Are there certain parts of the film that you want to move slower than other parts? For instance, if you are creating a video about bike safety, perhaps the speed of the bike gets slowed down and then suddenly moves very fast for a more dramatic effect. Brainstorm and try out a few different speeds before you make your final decision. Add text if needed to make your message clearer to younger learners.
