Do It! Vernier Elementary Sensing Science Collection: Send It Rollin’

 The Challenge

Two vehicles are rolling downhill; one is fully loaded and the other is empty. Which one is going to get to the bottom of the hill first? Your challenge is to create a downhill ramp using the materials provided and roll a toy vehicle down the ramp while adding more weight each time. 

Using the Vernier Go Direct Motion Detector, you will measure the speed of a toy vehicle as it goes down the ramp. 

 Getting Started

Set up the Vernier Go Direct Motion Detector for data collection.

Launch Graphical Analysis.

  1. Connect the motion detector to your Chromebook, computer, or mobile device.
  2. Click Mode to open Data Collection Settings. Set End Collection to 3 s. Click Done.
  3. Click view options (top right corner, square with three boxes in it) and choose 1 Graph. This will display a graph of position vs. time.
  4. Draw a prediction of how the motion will change during data collection.
  • Click Graph Tools, and then choose Add Prediction.
  • Draw your prediction of the vehicle’s movement down the ramp. Click Save.

 Project Steps

  1. Create Your Hypothesis

  2. Build Your Ramp
  3. Test Your Hypothesis
  4. Add Weights and Collect Data
  5. Create a Line Graph
  6. Evaluate Your Hypothesis

Create Your Hypothesis

Gather all of your materials. Hold your vehicle in your hands. Now, add some weights to it. Think of the following:

  • When rolling the vehicle across a flat surface with no extra weight on it, what do you notice?
  • When rolling the vehicle across a flat surface with the extra weight on it, do you notice anything different?
  • Picture them rolling downhill side by side. Which do you think will get to the bottom sooner.

Using all of that information, formulate your hypothesis on the Send It Rollin’ Data Sheet. In your hypothesis, be sure to include:

  • If the vehicle will go faster or slower when weight is added
  • Which vehicle will take less time to roll down the ramp

Most hypothesis sentences have a cause and effect: If ___________ (cause), then _____________(effect).

Complete this sentence with your hypothesis. Write it on your Send It Rollin’ Data Sheet.

If ___________ (more or less) weight is added, the vehicle will move ___________ (faster or slower). 

For example:

If I feed my plants energy drinks, then they will grow faster.

Build Your Ramp

Using the materials provided, create your ramp. Make sure to include:

  • A wide enough path for your vehicle to travel down
  • A mark at the top of the ramp where you will release the vehicle each time and space behind it for the motion detector

 

Test Your Hypothesis

You are ready to start testing to see if your hypothesis is correct. 

  1. Place the Go Direct Motion Detector at the top of the ramp with the gold circle pointing down the hill.
  2. Place the rear wheels of the vehicle on the mark that you created for the starting point.
  3. Click Collect to start data collection and release the car.
  4. If the truck runs off the ramp or goes past the book (stopping object), put the vehicles at the starting point and start data collection again.
  5. Click the graph to examine the position and time values. Find the fastest speed the vehicle traveled while moving down the hill. Record the data in the Send It Rollin’ Data Sheet
  6. Repeat the run 2-3 times to find an average speed.

Add Weights and Collect Data

After you have a baseline (or initial) run, you should know how long it took for a vehicle with no extra weight added to its load to get down the ramp.

  1. Clear your data in Graphical Analysis only after you recorded the value of the time it takes for the vehicle to get down the ramp. If you delete the data too early, you will have to do the run again.
  2. Add one of the extra weights to the vehicle and make sure it is secure. 
  3. Repeat steps 2-5 with one weight added to the car.
  4. Continue this process until all of the weights have been added to the vehicle.

The highest point on the graph is the fastest speed. To find out what the actual speed was, click the highest point and then follow the steps for viewing the stats. That is where you will see the actual speed.

Create a Line Graph

Once all of your data is on the Send It Rollin’ Data Sheet, use the information to create a scaled line graph.

Be sure to include:

  • Graph title
  • Labels for your x-axis and y-axis
  • Scale

Use the maximum speed on each run to make the line graph.

Evaluate Your Hypothesis

Use your line graph, graphs from Graphical Analysis, and Send It Rollin’ Data Sheet to answer the questions.

  • Are there any patterns that you notice about the speed of the vehicle as it traveled down the ramp when weight was increased?
  • What did you find out about weight and speed?
  • How would knowing this information help you out? Does it apply to just vehicles and ramps?
  • Go back to your hypothesis. Is your hypothesis correct? Explain, using the data you collected.
