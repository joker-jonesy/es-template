Your challenge is to use the website Vectr to crop and edit photos. You will first identify how a scale factor makes a product larger or smaller than one of its factors, just as cropping can make an image larger or smaller. Scale Factors and Photo Editing

  1. How do scale factors relate to photo editing? Online images and photos are measured with pixels. More pixels in an image means more detail, but it also takes more memory. This can cause an image to either take a long time to download or even ‘freeze’ the program. You can fix this problem by cropping an image, which is when you cut off some of the picture, and by doing this you also cut out some of the pixels. Another way you can fix this problem is by scaling an image down by resizing; this shrinks the picture and the number of pixels. . Scaling down an image is similar to multiplying the pixels by a fraction.
    1. Examples
      10×4=40
      10×3=30
      10×2=20
      10×1=10
      10 x ½ = 5
      10 x ⅓ = 3.33
      10 x ¼ = 2.5
      What pattern do you notice about these examples? What happens when you multiply by smaller numbers? Why? What happens when you multiply by larger numbers? Why?
      Enlarging an image is similar to multiplying the pixels by a number greater than 1.
  2. Another way to think of scaling an image up or down is by looking at the length and width. Think of an image that is 6 inches by 4 inches.
  3. When resizing images, the scale factor is often written as a percentage.  When there is no change in size, the image is 100% of its original size.  The image was scaled up to 125% of the original size and scaled down to 75% in the example above.
  4. Now think of scaling an image up or down. For each image described, scale each up by the given factor, and then scale it down.

Open an Image for Editing

You may want to take your own photos or use online images for your project. There are two ways that photos can be added to the Vectr workspace.

TO OPEN:

  1. Open a new file within Vectr. 
  2. After opening a new file, look at the toolbar to the left of the workspace and locate the insert tool.
  3. Click the insert tool. Select an image file from your computer, and it will be added to your workspace.

Images can also be added to the workspace by dragging an image from your computer into the workspace. Be sure to save a copy of the original image to compare the ‘before and after’ images. Give the edited image a slightly different name. One strategy is to add “_new” or “v2” after the name.

To Edit: Double click the image to select and edit the background image.

  • To Resize, click and drag the control points on your image. Hold the point as you resize the image.
    • Look at the bottom left of the screen to check the image size before you start resizing. Record the width or the length—you only need one.
    • Check the image size after resizing the image. Record the new width or length. Make sure that you record the same dimension before and after cropping.
    • Identify the scale factor. To do this, write a fraction with the new size in pixels divided by the original size in pixels. Use a calculator to find the decimal equivalent. The difference between 100% and the percent is the scale factor.
    • On the next image, scale the image by a factor of 0.1 and by a factor of 10. How much larger or smaller is each new image than the original one?
  • To Crop
    • Double click the path/image you would like to edit.
    • After double-clicking, the flip buttons and box/edit points surrounding the image will appear. This will allow you to crop the image without actually having to cut it.
    • Move the image in the direction that you would like to cut it.
    • You can also crop the image by altering the container path. This allows the image to be edited into different shapes without cutting the image.
    • Simply drag the edit points in the direction and shape that you would like the image to resemble.
                 
  • To Flip the image horizontally or vertically, hover over the buttons above the image once the image is clicked.
  • To Rotate, click the image. Once the control points appear, hold the mouse just outside of the control points until the rotate handle appears.
  • To undo any changes, select Ctrl + Z on your keyboard. You can also delete the path and start over.
  • When you are Done with your changes, click the export icon. You can save your design by either right-clicking the preview and selecting “save as” or by dragging the preview onto your computer desktop.

NOTE: If you want to type text on your image after editing, save it first. Then, go back to the workspace and select the text icon. After selecting the text icon, click on the image so the text box appears. Add text, and edit as necessary.

Using Filters

To change how the image looks, use the filters located in the editing panel on the right side of the workspace. Filters include backgrounds, borders, shadows, inset shadows, and opacity. Filters can be added, disabled, deleted, and sorted. 

TO APPLY A BACKGROUND FILTER:

Click the drop-down menu under backgrounds. Choose Background.

TO APPLY A BACKGROUND FILTER:

  • Color Backgrounds: sets a solid color background with the option to change colors. Use the color picker to select the color of your choice.
  • Image Backgrounds: sets an image background.
  • Linear Gradient Backgrounds: sets a background of a range of colors instead of just one solid color. Each gradient background has two color points known as color stops.
  • Radial Gradient Backgrounds: sets a background that starts from a single central point and radiates outward.

To learn more about applying and editing background filters, visit backgrounds.

TO APPLY A BORDER:

  • Click the drop-down menu under the border. Choose border alignment.
    • Outside aligns the border outside the image's boundary.
    • Inside aligns the border inside the image's boundary.
    • Center aligns the border to the center of the image's boundary.
  • Select a color for the border.
  • Adjust the size of the border. As you adjust the border, figure out how the border has changed the total size of the image. What is the scale factor?

Borders can be added to the text. The border will display around each letter and not the boundary of a word.

To learn more about applying borders, visit borders.

TO APPLY A SHADOW FILTER:

  • Select a shadow color from the dropdown menu and colorpicker.
  • Select the offset for both the x and y- axis.
  • If desired, add a blur for your shadow. The higher the number, the blurrier the shadow.
  • Adjust the size of the shadow spread by changing the shadow's spread number.

To learn more about the types of filters you can use and adjust, visit the Vectr user guide.
