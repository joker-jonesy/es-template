Do It! UKIT Beginner: Ace in the Hole

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build and code a Golf Club Robot using the UKIT Beginner kit. You will practice your measuring skills using a protractor and determine what angle the golf club needs to be pulled back to achieve the greatest distance of a ball.

 Project Steps

  1. Build Your Golf Club Robot

  2. Code Your Golf Club Robot

  3. Measure Your Angles

  4. Create a CER

Build Your Golf Club Robot

Build your golf club robot using the UKIT Beginner kit. Open the app on your phone or tablet. Select the Golf Club model as shown below and follow the directions to build. There are 30 steps to follow in the UKIT EDU app. 

Code Your Golf Club Robot

It is now time to code the golf club robot to hit a ping-pong ball or other ball. If you do not have a ball, a crumpled piece of paper will work. When you have completed the build, click on the check mark in the upper right hand corner. You will then see the building platform for your coding. Click on the Example code.

Measure Your Angles

Now that you have programmed your golf club robot. you will experiment and compare the distance the ping pong ball travels to the angle of the golf club. Note that you must not change the duration of the swings (300 ms) during the experiment, the duration is the control. Controls must stay the same through the whole experiment to ensure the data is reliable and accurate. 

  1. First, make sure that your golf club robot is connected to the app. Then run the program to make sure it is working well.
  2. Next, place a ping pong ball or another similar sized ball on the pad as shown. Mark where your ball starts on the ground with a piece of masking tape.
  3. Then, the program will pull the golf club robot back and wait for 5 seconds before release. This will give you time to measure the angle using your protractor prior to its release. Record the angle you measured and, when the ball is hit, the distance it traveled in the table below. Conduct three trials at this angle, then change the angle and repeat three more trials. Be sure to re-measure with the protractor each time.

After you collect the data from the three trials, add all of the distances together and divide by 3 (the number of trails) to get an average distance. Record it on your table. 

Now, change the program in the app to a different angle. Conduct three measurements of the angles and the distances as you did before. Remember, do not change the duration of the swing.

Change the angle a minimum of four times. Take a screenshot of one of the programs you altered. This, and your Data Table will be uploaded in your project submission.

Create a CER

Your CER must answer the question: Does the angle of the robotic golf club swing make a difference as to how far the ping pong ball travels?

Fill in the table below and use the questions in the far right column to check yourself.  You will upload your completed CER in your project submission.

Question:

Does the angle of the robotic golf club swing make a difference as to how far the ping pong ball travels?
