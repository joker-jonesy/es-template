Do It! Snap Circuits Green Energy: Hand Crank Data Analysis

 The Challenge

Have you ever heard people say that we need to conserve energy because we are running out of it? They were probably talking about non-renewable energy sources like fossil fuels. We are quickly using up these resources, so it is becoming more important to find and use renewable energy sources.

One resource we will always have is human power. Your challenge is to produce and store energy using human power. Then, test to see if the energy you produce can power a fan, motor, lights, and more. You will use Snap Circuits with the hand crank to design, build, and test your ideas. As you test your circuits, you will analyze the data to find factor pairs and decide whether a number is prime or composite.

Can you use a hand crank to make enough energy to operate a motor? Lights? A fan?

  • How should you design your circuits to give these devices enough power to run?
  • Can you get these devices to run even when you are not turning the hand crank?

To succeed in your challenge, explore the hand crank and complete some introductory projects. These experiences will give you the skills needed to build and teach others about your final challenge projects.

Explore the Hand Crank

To succeed in your challenge, you should understand how your hand crank works. 

  • Gently turn the handle. What happens?
  • Look inside the hand crank.
    • What is happening to the gear that is touching the handle?
    • How many complete turns does this first gear make for every full turn of the handle?
    • How many complete turns does the last gear make for every turn of the hand crank?
  • Place the hand crank on the base. Connect it to the motor and add the fan.
    • What happens to the fan when you turn the hand crank clockwise?
    • What happens when you turn the crank counter-clockwise?
    • What do you think is happening when you turn the crank in different directions?

Record what you see.

Introductory Projects

These introductory projects will give you the experience and knowledge needed to succeed in your challenge. You will experience stored versus direct energy, how different circuits generate different amounts of voltage, and what this means for the operation of devices. Download the Snap Circuits Green Energy Instruction Manual.

For project 2, count the number of times you turn the hand crank in 30 seconds. You need to turn it fast enough that it provides electricity to your circuit. Use a stopwatch to time yourself, or have your partner count to 30 for you. Create a table to analyze your data. See the example below.

If you turn the hand crank 28 times in thirty seconds, you could analyze the data as follows:

Number of Turns Factor Pairs Multiples Prime or composite?
28 1×28, 2×14, 4×7 56, 84, 112 28 is a composite number because it has more than one factor pair.

Create your own chart. Answer the following questions:

  • Name all of the factor pairs of your number.
  • Name 3 multiples of your number.
  • Is your number prime or composite? How do you know?

Final Challenge Projects

Complete as many of these final challenge projects as you can in the time that you have. Keep exploring and experimenting until you can explain what you need to do to make these circuits work. Were you able to get these devices to run even when you are not turning the hand crank?
