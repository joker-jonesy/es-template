Do It! Google Maps: National Parks Brochure

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create a brochure for a family trip to five national parks in the United States. Your brochure will include distance and time between locations, cost of gas, and any additional information you would like to include.

 Getting Started

  • To get started, go to Google My Maps
  • Sign in with your Google account or create an account if you have permission from your parent or guardian.

  • On paper (or in a program like Google Sheets or Excel), create a table to collect the time and distance date. See the samples below.

 Project Steps

  1. Make the Map

  2. Time and Distance Data

  3. Calculate the Cost of Gas

  4. Make a Brochure

Make the Map

Refer to the Google My Maps section for step-by-step instructions on how to complete each task if necessary.

  1. Log into Google My Maps and create a new map. Remember to name your map so that you can easily find it next time.
  2. Add the locations of the parks you will visit to your map. Some things to consider when planning the trip:

What places may you want to stop for food? What hotels or campgrounds do you plan to stop at for the night? Are there any sightseeing areas or theme parks you would like to stop at?

Where are the parks located in relation to each other? In what order will you visit the parks?

     3.  Change the color and style of icons.

Consider the colors you plan to use in your brochure or poster when choosing a color and style icon. This can help your family better understand your plans for the trip. It can also help you find specific types of locations, like hotels or sightseeing attractions.

Time and Distance Data

  1. Click on the 3 vertical dots by the Driving Direction.
  2. Click on Step-by-Step Directions from the pop-up menu.
  3. Near the letter A, you will find the distance in miles and the time in hours and minutes that it takes to get from the starting point to the first National Park.
  4. Record the time and distance in the chart. (Hint: Round the distance to the nearest hundred and the time to the nearest hour.)

Calculate the Cost of the Gas

  1. Select your vehicle.


  2. Calculate how much it will cost to fill up the gas tank of the vehicle. (Hint: Multiply the gas price times the fuel capacity.)
  3. Calculate how many times you will need to refuel the vehicle to complete the trip. (Hint: Make a number line.)
  4. Calculate the total amount of gas for the national park trip. (Hint: Multiply the total cost to fill up the tank times the number of times that your family will refuel.)

Create a Brochure

  1. Observe Earth’s features:
    • Click a national park in the list of locations.
    • Click View on Google Maps.
    • Select the Street View icon (yellow person).
    • Click one of the blue lines (that will take you into the image).
    • Navigate the image to make observations of the landforms.
    • X in upper right: close and return to the street view options
    • Compass rose: rotate
    • Zoom in + Zoom Out: take a closer or wider view
    • Image in the lower left: walk around
  2. Record 1-3 Earth features that you observed. You can use words and pictures.
  3. Record 1-3 other words that describe what you observed. You can use words and pictures.
  4. You can use paper, poster board, or another program like Pixie or Google Drawings to make your brochure.
  5. Make a brochure or a poster that includes the following information:
    • National parks that you plan to visit
    • The order that you will visit the park
    • The distance between each park
    • The time it will take to get to each park
    • The total driving time
    • Earth features that the family will see at each park
    • The total cost of gas
