Do It!  Google Maps: Mapping My Area

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to use Google My Maps to find your house, school, and other places that you visit often. You will find the area and perimeter of each place and create a presentation to share with your class.

 Getting Started

  • To get started, go to Google My Maps.
  • Sign in with your Google account or create an account if you have permission from your parent or guardian.

 Project Steps

  1. Google Map Locations

  2. Perimeter & Area

  3. Create Your Presentation

Google Map Locations

  1. Using Google My Map (using the line, shape, and measurement tools) find one place you visit often in the shape of a rectangle. 
  2. Using Google My Map tools, find the length and width of the shape. Put this information into a chart similar to the one found below.
  3. Repeat these steps until you have found the length and width of 5 places you visit often.

Perimeter & Area

Find the perimeter and area of each location. Refer back to What You Should Know for instructions on how to find the area and perimeter. Think about why you would want to find the perimeter and area.

Things to consider:

  • If you calculate the perimeter, how can you use that information to cut down on the feet walked?
  • If you calculate the area, how can you use that information to avoid walking around the entire store?
  • Perhaps you could challenge your family to walk the highest amount of feet or square feet when you visit each place in one day.
  • Or, you could challenge your family to walk the least amount of feet or square feet when you visit each place.
  • Plan you and/or your family’s day out. Where should you go first, second, third…? Why? How does the perimeter and area help you plan your day?

Check your work (answers) using a calculator. If you find any errors, create a new table with the checked work.

Create Your Presentation

Now that you have gathered all of your information, you can create a digital or poster board presentation showing the location, measurements, area, perimeter, and calculations.

Things to include:

  • The 5 places you visit the most
  • The length and width of each place
  • The perimeter of each place
  • The area of each place
  • Why this information is important when planning which place you will visit
  • A challenge you created for your family (if this is something you chose to do in Step 2).
