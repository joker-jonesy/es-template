Create a Fraction Model for Project #3

Create a Fraction Model for the IC used in this circuit.

  1. Sketch a rectangle to represent the circuit board.
  2. In your sketch, divide the rectangle into 9 equal columns to match the Snap Circuits circuit board.
  3. Select an IC. Temporarily move it to the top left corner of the circuit board.
  4. Count how many columns are taken up by the IC. Is it 2 columns wide? 4 columns? This is the numerator of your first fraction.
  5. On your grid, color in however many columns are occupied by the IC.
  6. Count the number of total columns. This is the denominator of your first fraction.
  7. Write down your numerator and denominator in a single fraction.
  8. In the same sketch, divide the rectangle into 6 equal rows to match the Snap Circuits circuit board.
  9. Count how many rows are taken up by the IC. Is it 1 row high? 2 rows? This is the numerator of your second fraction.
  10. On your grid, use a different color to shade in the number of rows occupied by the IC.
  11. Count the number of total rows. This is the denominator of your second fraction.
  12. Write down the numerator and denominator in a single fraction.
  13. Multiply the two fractions. This is the total area of the circuit board used for this IC.
  14. Check your multiplication by counting the boxes on your Fraction Model.
    1. Count the number of boxes shaded in by both colors. This is the numerator.
    2. Count the total number of boxes in the sketch of the circuit board. This is the denominator. Does it match what you calculated when you multiply the fractions?

Design Your Own Circuit

Now that you have experience constructing circuits that use sensors and IC's, design your own. Be sure to take photos and videos to include in your project submission. Explain what you wanted it to do and whether or not you were able to make it work the way you wanted. What methods did you try?

Label photos to identify sensors, chips, and any other important parts of your circuit.
