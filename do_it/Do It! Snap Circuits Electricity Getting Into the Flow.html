Do It! Snap Circuits Electricity: Electrical Circuits and Number Patterns

 The Challenge

Think about how you use electricity in a single day. Your challenge is to learn the basics of electricity. You will also learn how to control it in different ways by building electrical circuits. For your math challenge, you will identify a pattern using the values of the Snap Circuit connectors and explain the pattern using properties of addition and multiplication. 

 Project Steps

  1. Complete Project #1 – Electric Light and Switch.

  2. Complete Project #2 – DC Motor and Switch.

  3. Complete Project #13 – Two-Speed Fan.

 Math Connection

  2. Identify the number pattern and eve-odd pattern.

Project #2 – DC Motor and Switch

Use the instructions manual to figure out how to turn your circuit into a motor circuit. 

Did you notice the “+” signs on the battery and motor blocks? Make sure you build your circuit with the motor's “+” facing the “+” of the power source.

Flip the Motor

  • Flip the motor block around in your circuit as shown.
  • Be careful not to lean over your circuit when you turn it on.
  • Now test your circuit and see what it does. What do you think happened here?
  • Take a picture of your circuit and label its parts.

Reflect

When you flipped the motor, it changed the way the current flowed through it.

Don't forget about the current direction when you build your Snap Circuits projects. If a circuit does not work correctly, check to see if a part is backward.

If you want, make a video showing and explaining this idea to your classmates.

Project #13 – Two-Speed Fan

What do you think will happen if you build a circuit with both a light and a motor? Both loads use power from the battery.

Build the two-speed fan and make a prediction about what will happen when you push down on the press switch.

Notice where the spacer is used when building your circuit.

Now test your prediction.

  • Turn the slide switch to the “on” position. Observe the light and the fan. Are they both turned on? How fast is the fan spinning?
  • Now press and hold the press switch. What changed? Are both the light and motor still on? How fast is the fan spinning?

Was your prediction correct? Explain in your own words why pressing the press switch had the effect it did. Take a picture of your circuit. Use arrows to label the current path both when the press switch is “off” and when it is “on.”

Keep in mind that electricity will take the easiest path in a circuit. Any load, like a light, makes it harder for electricity to flow through it by creating resistance. A simple wire is much easier to move through.
