Do It! CoDrone EDU: Pilot Practice

The Challenge

Your challenge is to learn how to pilot a drone safely. Then, practice simulating flight patterns for a drone pilot career of your choice. 

 Getting Started

NOTE: Do not try to fly CoDrone until you have reviewed the CoDrone Safety Instructions:

Direct Link: CoDrone Safety Instructions

 Project Steps

  1. Complete the Getting Started Course 

  2. Choose a Drone Job 

  3. Pilot the Drone

Complete the Getting Started Course 

Complete Lessons 0.1–0.5 of the Getting Started Course, which can also be found in Information about CoDrone EDU. 

Start with Lessons 0.1–0.4, paying attention to the review in the final step of each lesson. This is your checklist of key points. 

After, complete Lesson 0.5, including the Piloting Challenges in steps 15 and 16. On a piece of paper, draw the letter(s) and shapes you attempt in these special challenges.  

  • Each partner should pilot at least two letter shapes and the waves in step 15, and two of the shapes in step 16.  
  • As seen, use an arrow to show the direction of the path you fly. Makes notes about any challenges you face trying to make these shapes, and how you meet these challenges.  

You will submit a photo of your drawings and notes to the project submission.     

Lessons 0.6: Trimming, and 0.7: Troubleshooting, Repair, and Maintenance can be reviewed on an as-needed basis. Trimming is what you can do if your drone starts to drift instead of hover.  

Choose a Drone Job

Review the different uses for drones in the What You Should Know section and think about any other uses you are aware of. Think about the flight patterns required for each of the jobs. For example, an agriculture drone may fly back and forth in rows to water a whole field systematically, while a surveillance drone may repeatedly fly around the perimeter of an area it is monitoring.  

NOTE:

When you choose a drone job to simulate, consider how much time you have for this activity, which skills you are comfortable with, and which skills will be necessary for the flight simulation. 

Choose one drone job to simulate.  

  • On a piece of paper, write the drone's task or job. 
  • Describe what the drone would need to do to fulfill its job. 
  • Draw the flight pattern it might take.  
  • Create any props or drawings to help with the simulation, for example, set up paper targets to represent houses so your drone can deliver packages. Or, set up a pretend wilderness to fly the drone on a search and rescue mission. 

You will submit a photo or screenshot of this document for your project submission.  

Pilot the Drone

You chose a purpose and created a simulated environment. Now, fly the drone in the flight pattern. Each partner should get a chance to pilot the drone.  

You will submit a photo of your simulated environment for your project submission. 

If you have extra time, you can choose another drone career or purpose and fly the flight pattern; you can create a simple simulated environment, like basic paper squares to serve as takeoff/landing zones.
