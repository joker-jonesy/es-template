Do It! Comic Life: Illustrating Problem and Solution

 The Challenge

Create a comic strip with Comic Life where you illustrate the problem and solution for a made-up multi-step word problem. 

 Project Steps

  1. Choose a Problem You Will Solve

  2. Plan Your Comic Story

  3. Create Your Comic Strip

  4. Submit Your Comic Strip

Choose a Problem You Will Solve

Think about a math problem for your comic strip that can be solved with an equation. Consider the following math problems:

  • The total number of muffins needed for a bake sale.
  • The number of crayons needed for the class.
  • The number of cars in a car lot.
  • The number of flowers needed in a garden.

Create an equation you will write your story around.

Plan Your Comic Story

Create a comic strip that illustrates a made up multi-step mathematical word problem. Remember, this would require someone to use at least two operations to solve the problem. 

For example:

Neal has 4 friends over. They order pizza. Each friend gets 3 pieces of pizza, and there are 2 pieces left over. How many slices of pizza total were there?

2 + 3(4) =  P 

P represents the number of total slices.

Use the steps below to help you plan your comic story:

  1. Write an equation.
  2. Test your equation.
  3. Choose a character(s).
  4. Come up with ideas for your word problem.
  5. Think about how the word problem could fit in the storyline of the comic.
  6. Begin work on your comic strip.

Create Your Comic Strip

Make up a story about your problem and how you will tell the story of solving it. Then, open your Comic Life software to get started.

Use Your Equation To Plan Your Story

As you build a multi-step problem as a story, determine the characters, the setting, the problem and the solution. Write down your equation and make sure you have a plan for each step. Make sure you include the equation written out and answer in the comic strip. Don’t forget your units!

After you have planned your story, build it using Comic Life.

Choose a Template

Once you click on the Comic Life icon to launch the software, click on a template. There are many different categories, so be sure to look through a few. Which template has the right spaces in the right shape and size for your story? Is there a color or style that will help make your multi-step word problem story more interesting?

Add Panels

Click on a panel layout. There are several different layouts for each template. Think about your story and the sequence of the story. If a big, important event happens right away, you may want to choose the layout with the largest panel at the top. Drag and drop the layout onto the workspace.

Add Images and Drawings

After selecting a template and a layout for your comic, you will add images, text, and speech bubbles.

You can:

  • Take photos with a camera and save them to your student folder
  • Scan artwork and save it to your student folder
  • Use the Internet to find and save images to your student folder

Improve it with the Toolbar

The toolbar is where you will change the text, style, colors, and font. When you are done creating your comic, take a screenshot of your comic strip.

Submit Your Comic Strip 

You can save and export your comic as a PDF or image file or capture your comic with a screen shot and save it to upload for your Project Submission.
