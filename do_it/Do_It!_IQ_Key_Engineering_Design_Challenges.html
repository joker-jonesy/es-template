Do It! IQ Key: Engineering Design Challenges

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build, test, and evaluate a crane machine. Then you will see if you can improve the design. You will start by building the basic crane and calculating the work done when it lifts a load. Then you will see if you can improve the crane design and calculate the work done by your modified crane for comparison.

 Project Steps

  1. Build a Basic Machine

  2. Test the Basic Machine

  3. Design and Build a Better Machine

  4. Perform Another Set of Trials

Test the Basic Machine

Physicists and designers need to understand the energy transfer in their machines. Calculating work helps them understand the energy being transferred from one object to another. Larger machines (like a larger crane) with a larger work value tells you that more work or more energy has been transferred. Physicists also use the work value to calculate things such as power of an object. This challenge will help you practice calculating work and making observations about what can increase or decrease the work done by your machine.

Before you design a better crane, test the basic version that you just built.

Complete the Crane Lift Test: How much weight can the basic crane lift?

  1. Build a container for the crane to lift. You can use a small sandwich bag or make an origami cup and poke a hole to fit around the crane hook. However you do it, make it light and easy to hang.
  2. Add weight to the bucket (pennies make good weights).
  3. Record the total mass, in kilograms, of the bucket and its contents. Example: 50 pennies has a mass of 125 grams or 1/8 of a kilogram.
  4. Turn the crane on to lift the bucket.
  5. Set your crane to where it can lift the weight in the bucket 1/5 of a meter (or 20 centimeters) when measuring from the ground surface to the raised hook.
  6. Repeat steps 2, 3, and 4 until the crane can no longer lift the weight.
  7. Record the maximum mass the crane lifts to 1/5 of a meter.
  8. Make a chart like the one here to record your data.

  9. Calculate the work done by the crane for your heaviest lift. Don’t forget to account for the acceleration due to gravity.  
  10. Make sure to show your work when calculating the work done by your crane. Write an equation and draw a fraction model to represent the work done.
  11. Modify your design to increase the work done by the crane.

Design and Build a Better Machine

Now that you know how well your basic model works, you can make changes to improve it.

How will you know if your design works better than the basic design? Improve the basic crane design to increase the work value of your machine.

Use the Engineering Design Process to guide you. Document your work for each step of the process.

Calculate the work done by the crane for your new design by following the same steps as before. Compare your results to the basic machine.
