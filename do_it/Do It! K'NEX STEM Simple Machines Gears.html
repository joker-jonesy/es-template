Do It! K'NEX STEM Simple Machines: Build Machines with Gears

 The Challenge

Gears are found everywhere, including in bicycles, cars, toys, vacuums, and clocks. If you have ever ridden a bicycle up a hill, you know that gears help you get to the top. But how do gears make the task easier?

Your challenge is to use K'NEX STEM to explore gears and build your own gear machine. First, you will review attributes of shapes as you sort K’NEX pieces. Then, you will apply this concept to identify similar and congruent gears as you explore how the gears work—or won’t work—together.

 Project Steps

  1. Attributes of Shapes

  2. Build and Explore the Gears in a Machine

Attributes of Shapes

To get ready to work with K’NEX Gears, review how attributes define shapes. This is important as you figure out how pieces—especially gears—fit together to do work. Gears cannot work without a support system. The gears must connect to other shapes for a machine to work. As you complete this first part, think about how the attributes of shapes create the support system that allows the machine to do work.

  1. Sort the K’NEX pieces into groups of congruent shapes. How many distinctly different pieces do you have?
  2. Next, combine pieces into similar groups. Are all of the gears similar? In what ways are the gears different?
  3. To help you figure out how the pieces fit together, build some quadrilaterals. How many different 4-sided shapes can you build? What pieces did you have to use to make different shapes? Which pieces resulted in similar shapes?
  4. Copy this Venn Diagram. Then, build at least 2 shapes that fit into each circle. Hint: Start with squares, and work out to the largest circle. When finished, take a picture or sketch the results.
  5. Answer these questions:
    • Are all rectangles in the ‘Squares’ circle?
    • Are all squares in the ‘Rectangles’ circle?
    • Are all rectangles and squares inside the quadrilateral circle?
    • Are all quadrilaterals in the rectangle circle?
  6. Write an explanation that describes how you decided where to put each shape.
  7. THINK: Which shapes would provide the best support for a machine? What is the attribute that allows these shapes to support a machine? HINT: Try combining shapes to create a “frame” that will stand on its own.
  8. Now make a Venn diagram for the gears. How are the gears different?  How many circles do you need?  Does size put a gear into a new circle?
  9. Save your images or sketches of the Venn diagrams and your answers to questions in steps 4, 5, and 6.

Build and Explore the Gears in a Machine

  1. Download the K'NEX STEM, Gears Instruction Manual here.
  2. Examine the gears. Identify which are congruent and which are similar. Describe the attributes of each gear, including the number of teeth and the diameter.
  3. What is the shape of the ‘teeth’? What are the attributes of the teeth? How do the teeth fit together to do work?
  4. Build one of the gear machines. (see below) Identify the shapes used. Which shapes are connected by the gears? Which parts of the machine move to do work?
  5. Which parts of the machine do not move? What are the attributes of the parts that do not move? THINK: Why is it important that these parts do not move? How do the attributes of these shapes help the gears in the machine do work?
  6. Take a picture of your finished model. Add labels to your picture. You may also want to take a video to show the machine in action.
  7. Describe how your gear system works. What type of gear does your machine use? How do you know?
  8. Are the gears in your design similar or congruent? What happens when a gear with more teeth moves a gear with fewer teeth?

Create Your Own

Use your imagination and your knowledge about simple machines and gears to build your own gear machine. What could you use it for? 

Share your design with your class and explain how gears help your machine do work.
