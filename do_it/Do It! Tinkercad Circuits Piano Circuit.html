Do It! Tinkercad Circuits: Piano Circuit

The Challenge

Your challenge is to use Tinkercad Circuits to design and test circuits. Explore how different circuit parts affect how a circuit works. Then, create a piano circuit that audibly simulates and incorporate specific components including breadboard, pushbuttons, resistors, wires, piezo, timer and capacitor.

Explore Components

Start by completing one or more circuit tutorials, then design and build your own circuits. Use the "Start Simulation" button to test your circuits. If the resistors in your circuit such as the light or motor do not work during the simulation, try moving components around.

Gather Required Components 

You will need to gather all of the correct components before you place them onto your breadboard. You can use the search bar at the right hand side to easily find components.  You will need to give each component a name and a number for the specific resistance for each of the resistors. 

Items you will need: 

  • 6 pushbuttons ( Add Name: P1, P2, P3, P4, P5 and P6)
  • 2 - 2kµ resistors (Name: R1 and R4)
  • 1 - 3kµ resistor (Name: R5)
  • 2 - 1kµ resistor (Name: R2 and R7)
  • 1 - 1.5kµ resistor (Name: R3)
  • 1 - 6kµ resistor (Name: R6)
  • 1 - Piezo Buzzer (Name: B1)
  • 1 - 250nF capacitor (Name: C1)
  • 1 - Timer 555 (Name: T1)
  • 1 - Regular breadboard

Design a Piano Circuit

Once you have gathered all of your components you will start to design your piano. First you will need to connect the power source to the breadboard. You will use an alligator wire to connect to the red positive terminal on the power supply and the positive terminal on the breadboard. Next, you will do the same thing with another alligator clip but this time connect to both negative terminals. 

Be sure to change your power supply to voltage value 12 and current value 0.2. 

Select each item to learn more.

Pushbuttons

A pushbutton is a type of switch that is activated by pressing a button. Next connect your pushbuttons to the breadboard. Start by placing them in column 5 and the first button between row E and F. Make sure they have a one hole gap in between them. 

Piezo Buzzer

A Piezo buzzer is a electronic component that produces sound when a current is applied to it. It is currently used for devices that require a sound outpug. Select the piezo component and rotate it three times by pressing "R" on your keyboard. Connect the piezo to column 60, making sure the ‘-’ part of the piezo is connected to the negative row of the breadboard towards the bottom. There should be 1 hole separating the ‘+’ terminal of the piezo and the 3rd ‘leg’ of the timer.

Resistors

A resistor is an electronic component that limits the flow of current in a circuit. Now you will add your resistors. To begin connecting the resistors to the breadboard, rotate the first six resistors so they are horizontal with the breadboard, do this by selecting each one and pressing "R" on your keyboard three times.

  1. Starting at row A; column 7, place the first resistor on the breadboard. 
  2. The next resistor should be at row B column 11.
  3. The third at row A column 15.
  4. The fourth at row B column 19.
  5. The fifth at row A column 23.
  6. The sixth resistor at row B column 27.
  7. The seventh and last resistor should be vertical to the breadboard. Place it at row B column 59 and the ‘+’ column of the breadboard.

Wires

In a circuit wires are used to connect different components together to let electricity flow through. Placement of wires is important otherwise the circuit may not work correctly. 

  1. Starting from row G column 5, connect a wire to row G column 9.
  2. Connect another wire from row H column 9 to row H column 13.  
  3. Connect another wire from row G column 13 to row G column 17.  
  4. Connect another wire from row H column 17 to row H column 21.  
  5. Connect another wire from row G column 21 to row G column 25.
  6. Connect another wire from row H column 25 to row H column 59.
  7. Connect another wire from row D column 31 to row D column 59.
  8. Connect another wire from row J column 58 to the ‘-’ row of the breadboard.
  9. Connect another wire from row A column 58 to the ‘+’ row of the breadboard directly above it.
  10. Connect another wire from row G column 61 to the ‘+’ row of the breadboard at the top.
  11. Connect the final wire which is from row D column 60 to row G column 59.

Capacitor

A capacitor is an electric component that stores electric charge. Connect the capacitor at row i column 58 on the breadboard.

Timer

A 555 timer is a type of circuit that can be used to create a bunch of electronic signals. This includes oscillating and pulse-width modulated signals. Connect the 555-timer between rows E and F at column 58 on the breadboard.

Simulate and Troubleshoot

Click start simulation on your design. This can be found in the upper right hand corner of Tinkercad Circuits. You can then click on your pushbuttons and play your piano. If your design doesn’t work, make sure the volume is turned up on your computer. If your design still doesn’t work, go back through steps to troubleshoot and make sure the pieces are attached in the correct location.
