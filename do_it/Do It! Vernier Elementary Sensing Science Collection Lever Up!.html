Do It! Vernier Elementary Sensing Science Collection: Lever Up! 

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to find materials and create a lever that you can run tests on. You will use the results of the tests and collected data, to determine how to build the ultimate lever to lift objects.

 Getting Started

Getting started using Bluetooth connection: 

  1. Charge your sensor for at least 2 hours before first use. 
  2. Turn on your sensor by pressing the Power button once. The LED will blink red.
  3. Launch Graphical Analysis. 
  4. Click or tap Sensor Data Collection. 
  5. Select your Go Direct sensor from the sensor choices. 

Getting started using USB connection: 

  1. Connect the sensor to the USB port. 
  2. Launch Graphical Analysis. 
  3. You are now ready to collect data.

Setting Up Data Collection Mode

Set up the data collection mode.

  1. Click or tap Mode to open Data Collection Settings. 
  2. Change Mode to Event Based. 
  3. Enter Length as the Event Name and cm as the Units. 
  4. Click or tap Done.

Create Your Lever

Create your lever with the materials of your choice.

Think about these questions as you get started;

  • What kind of support does the meter stick need under it?
  • Can the meter stick just be placed on the fulcrum, or does it need to be secured?
  • Will something flexible or hollow work? How can I make it work?

Setting Up the Book and Sensor

  1. Tape your fulcrum near the edge so that it cannot move.

  2. Place the lever so that the 20 cm mark is on the fulcrum, leaving the longer end sticking out over the side of the table.
  3. Put the book you are going to lift at the 5 cm mark so that just the edge of the book rests on the beam.
  4. Hook the force sensor on the loop of string, about 30 cm up the beam.

Zero the sensor: Hold the force sensor so that the hook on the end points upward. Click the force meter on Graphical Analysis and choose Zero. Note: You have to hold it very still.

Collecting and Recording Data

Each time you collect data, you want to make sure that you are pulling the sensor until it makes the lever horizontal. Making the beam or lever horizontal or straight allows you to see the amount of force needed to lift the load.

  1. Click Collect to start
  2. Holding the force sensor, slowly pull down until the lever is straight across.
  3. While holding the lever steady, have someone in the group click Keep. This will record the force you applied to lift the book.
  4. Enter 10 to indicate the 10 cm effort arm, and click Keep Point.

Collect more data.

  1. Always reposition the book so the edge is at the 5 cm mark and make sure the fulcrum is in the right place.
  2. Move the string out to 40 cm and reattach the sensor.
  3. Pull down on the force sensor to lift the book. When the lever is straight, click Keep Point.
  4. Continue collecting data until the data table is filled. When you finish all of the data collection, click Stop to stop data collection. Click the graph to examine the length and force values. Record the applied force for each of the effort arm distances on Data Table #1 on the Lever Up! Data Sheet 

Moving Sensor and Book [Additional Testing]

  1. Move the beam so the fulcrum is at the 30 cm point. In this position, the resistance arm is 30 cm. Keep the book in the same position as before, at 5 cm. 
  2. Using the same steps from the previous set of data, move the sensor and effort arm length as described in the table until all of Data Table # 2 of the Lever Up! Data Sheet is filled.

Analyzing Data

By looking at your two data tables and your observations throughout the challenge, answer these questions:

  • What did you notice about how hard you had to pull as the effort arm became longer and longer?
  • What would happen if the effort arm became shorter?
  • What do you think would happen to the data if the book sat at 10 cm on the resistance arm? 15 cm?
  • What happened to the applied force when the effort arm became longer?

Example:

I think by moving the book farther up on the resistance arm to 10 cm, that it would be ______________ to pull the sensor and make the lever straight. As the effort arm became longer, the applied force _________________. 

The, using your two data tables, create a double bar graph comparing the applied force and effort arm length between data table #1 and data table #2.  Be sure to:

  • Include a title.
  • Label the x-axis.
  • Label the y-axis.
  • Set a scale based on the effort arm length.
  • Label or color code the different bars.
  • ***Round the applied force number (N) to the nearest whole number. Example: if the applied force is 6.3N, I would round it to 6N because .3 is closer to 6 than 7

The Ultimate Task

Based on what you have learned about the amount of force required to lift a book and the position of the fulcrum, design a lever to launch something of your own choice. How heavy of an object could you lift?
