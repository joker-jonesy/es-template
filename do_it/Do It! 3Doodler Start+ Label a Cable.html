Do It! 3Doodler Start+: Label a Cable 

The Challenge

Your challenge is to design a system for organizing cables to keep them tidy and free from tangles. Use the 3Doodler pen to create a prototype that organizes at least one cable. Include a label or symbol to identify each cable.   

 Project Steps

  1. Choose a Cable 

  2. Sketch a Design 

  3. Create Your Cable System 

Choose a Cable

Find a cable to be organized and labeled. Choose one used frequently, like a charging cable for a kit in the SmartLab. 

Measure the cable to know how big your cable organizer system should be. Be sure to measure the length of the cable and the end of cable. This will help you create a design that fits onto the cable. 

Sketch a Design 

Sketch a design for your cable organizer system. Consider how and where the cables will be stored when not in use. Sketch at least three different designs for the symbol to label your cable. Collect feedback on your design from a neighbor and/or your facilitator to decide which symbol to use. You may get some good suggestions from others for improving your cable organizer. 

Title 3

Use the 3D pen to create your cable organizer and label. Consider creating different organizer parts by tracing your sketch with your 3D pen before connecting the parts with the pen. You can draw flat shapes on the clear mats in the 3Doodler kit before adding layers to create the 3D shapes you need. 

Use a different color to create the symbol to label your cable and organizer. Make sure the symbol is large enough to be seen easily. 

Do NOT use the 3Doodler pen directly on a cable!

The 3Doodler plastic can stick to cables permanently, causing damage to the cable.
