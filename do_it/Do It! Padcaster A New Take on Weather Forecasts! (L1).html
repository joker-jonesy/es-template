Do It! Padcaster: A New Take on Weather Forecasts!

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create a new take on a local weather report. Share with your classmates how they can prepare and respond to upcoming weather changes. Include positive and negative numbers on a thermometer to provide further evidence of upcoming temperatures. 

 Getting Started

Export Your Video in Padcaster Producer App 

To export your video when it is finished:

  1. Click the icon in the bottom left corner.
  2. On the next screen, click the video you want to export.
  3. Click the button in the top left with a rectangle and arrow combined. 
  4. Choose email or file sharing. Ask your teacher to know which option to choose

 Project Steps

  1. Create a Script & Gather Props

  2. Add Effects & Record Weather Broadcast

  3. Export Your Weather Broadcast

Create a Script & Gather Props

Now is your chance to create your script and gather props for your weather report.

Things to Consider:

  • How will you make your weather report unique and engaging?
  • Will you do a report about how to dress for the weather, eco-friendly preservation, or something else you can think of?

For example, for a report on how to dress for the weather, if it is going to rain, suggest to your peers that they should wear a raincoat, rain boots, and carry an umbrella. Make it more interactive by wearing these items yourself for the weather report or even have several of your friends dress for the weather to have a weather report fashion show.

Eco-friendly preservation might include putting a bucket out to gather rainwater to use later, closing all windows to reduce the use of the air conditioner on hot days, opening the windows and turning off the air conditioner on cooler days to save energy, or anything else you can come up with.

  • What weather will you be reporting on? Will there be rain, snow, sunny weather, etc.? Consider using the National Weather Service to learn about the weather forecast in your area.
  • What extra props or graphics will you use to engage your audience?
  • Will you use the green screen or put up your own backdrop?
  • Will you film inside or outside?
  • How long will your weather report be? (Consider keeping it under 5 minutes.)

Things to Include:

  • Type of weather expected
  • 3- to 7-day forecast 
  • Thermometer and integers to display the expected temperature
  • Comparison of temperatures
  • Images or graphics of some sort
  • Ideas include using the green screen to add any backdrop you would like to use, examples of clothing if you choose to do a report on how to dress, or props of how to achieve eco-friendly preservation.

Add Effects & Record Weather Broadcast

  1. Ensure your equipment is set up properly. 
  2. Check the angle of the camera and the stance of your reporter (or yourself if you are the reporter). 
  3. Add effects within the Padcaster app that you want included. Ideas include: color effects, title, opening credit or closing credits, image overlay, lower third, and more
  4. Begin recording. 
  5. It may take a few tries to get the final weather report you would like.

Export Your Weather Broadcast

  1. Export your video clips. 
  2. If you have multiple video clips, combine the clips using one of the video production apps found on the Padcaster iPad. 
  3. Think about how you will share your weather forecast with others. Will you email it, show it in class on a projector, or send it to everyone's devices? Speak with your teacher about the best option for your classroom.
