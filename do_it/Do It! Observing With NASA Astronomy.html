Do It! Observing With NASA: Astronomy

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to use Observing with NASA telescopes to look at astronomical objects. Then, create a presentation about the objects you found.

 Project Steps

  1. Find an Image

  2. Prepare Your Presentation

Find an Image

The MicroObservatory Image directory has great photos of astronomical objects taken with NASA cameras. Note that the images are sorted by the date and time they were taken . To view and download the pictures, follow the three steps below:

  1. Search through the “Sort by Object” menu  to find  astronomical objects to view.

  2. Click the Image Filename to open the picture.

  1. Click on the image link to download and save.

  1. Choose at least 4 different types of astronomical objects .(In other words, don't choose all galaxies or all stars, but a variety of objects.

Prepare Your Presentation

Go online to learn more about the objects you discovered.  Use the name of the object to search for information about the object. Did you find a picture of a moon, star, or something else?

Find at least five interesting facts about each object and include them in your presentation along with your photographs.

Begin preparing your presentation in PowerPoint, Google Slides, or another presentation tool. Be creative! You will upload your presentation in your project submission. 

Some suggestions for your presentation:

  • When was your object discovered? Who found it?
  • Has a spaceship or probe visited your object? Scientists have sent spacecraft missions to the Moon and all of the planets in the solar system. Are there close-up pictures of your object from one of these missions?
  • Include screenshots of the Observing with NASA website or other great astronomy websites.
  • Add pictures of the robotic telescopes.
  • Find artists' drawings of your objects.
