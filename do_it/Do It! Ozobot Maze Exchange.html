Do It! Ozobot: Measure a Maze

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to challenge a team to create YOUR maze.

Use shapes to design a maze that Ozobot can follow. Use a ruler to measure the lines of the maze. Collect data about the lengths of the lines and organize the data on a line plot. Then write clear, detailed instructions so another team can recreate and guess your design. This activity uses markers and paper or an iPad.

 Getting Started

Engineers are professionals who invent, design, analyze, build, and test machines to solve problems. As an engineer in this project, you will design a maze that Ozobot can follow. Then, write clear, detailed instructions so another team can recreate your design. This activity uses markers and paper or an iPad.

Engineers write technical requirements to define what a solution must be able to do. Then, they write design specifications to identify how their solution should solve the problem.

Design and Build

Design and build your maze. Follow the design specifications listed below: 

  1. Have either markers and paper or the OzoDraw app ready so you could create your maze. If you use the OzoDraw iPad app, choose Playground or Freeform mode.
  2. Design the maze so that others can create it as well. Categorize, draw, and list the attributes of at least two shapes that are quadrilaterals.
    • Your maze design should include these two shapes.
    • What words, categories, attributes can be used to describe these shapes?
  3. Since you will need other lines to complete your maze, use a ruler to make the additional lines. Be sure that all of your lines are measured to the quarter, half, and whole inch.
  4. Include the following:
    • Two or more intersections
    • Two 90 degree turns 
    • Two quadrilateral shapes 

Measure and Graph Data

Use a ruler to measure the length of each line segment in your design.

  1. Measure each line as you draw it. Write down the length of the line for each step. Example:  "First I drew a line that was 1 and ½ inches long, then I drew a line…”
  2. Measure each line to the nearest quarter, half, or whole inch.
  3. Make a line plot with a horizontal scale that includes quarter, half, and whole numbers.
  4. Record the length of each line on your line plot.

Test and Record Steps

Use Ozobot to try out the maze you drew. You may discover that you need or want to make changes to the design. Calibrate Ozobot first. 

Once you are happy with your maze design, write clear and detailed instructions on how to recreate your design.

  1. Where does the maze start and finish?
  2. What kind of shapes did you design?
  3. What are the lengths of each of the line segments?

Trade, Try, and Redesign

Trade your instructions with another group. (Do not let them see your drawing.) Try to draw the other group's maze from their instructions. If a group is stumped and cannot draw the maze accurately, the instructions are not clear enough. The team that designed the maze should rewrite the instructions.

  1. Both teams should keep rewriting and re-drawing until the designs are right.
  2. When the designs are right, let Ozobot follow the maze!

Extra Challenges

  • Place your Ozobot anywhere in the maze. Does it still make it to the finish? What codes do you need to add to make sure your Ozobot reaches the finish?
  • Compare your line plot with another group. What is the same? What is different? Who used more lines? Who used the longest lines?
  • What else does the data tell you about the two mazes? What predictions can you make based on the information?
