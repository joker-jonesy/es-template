Do It! K'NEX STEM Simple Machines: Build and Measure Inclined Planes

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build an inclined plane machine so you can show how inclined planes make work easier. You will use a protractor to measure the angles of your inclined planes. Then, you will compare the attributes of triangles to identify attributes of inclined planes.

Basic Models

Build one or more of the basic inclined plane models from the instruction manual. Take a picture and label its parts. What type of inclined plane does it use? Explain in your own words how your model helps make work easier. 

Advanced Machines

Need more building challenges? Try building one or more of these models. Take a picture and label its parts. What type of inclined plane is it? Is there more than one type being used?

Explain to one of your classmates how it makes work easier.

     

Coordinate Planes

  1. Using the graph paper, make a coordinate plane. Draw a horizontal line along the bottom of the paper and label it as the x-axis. Draw a vertical line along the left side of the paper and label it as the y-axis. 
  2. Place your K’NEX inclined plane on the graph paper. Lay it so that the bottom of the design is on the x-axis. Have the inclined plane cross the origin at (0,0).
  3. Trace the inclined plane onto the coordinate plane.
  4. Make a right triangle from the inclined plane on your coordinate plane.
  5. Use a protractor to measure the angles of your inclined plane.
  6. Identify the attributes of your inclined plane. Classify the triangle made by the inclined plane by connecting all the sides.
