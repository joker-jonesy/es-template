Do It! PowerPoint: Teaching Preschoolers

 The Challenge

How do young children learn? Your challenge is to create a PowerPoint presentation to teach preschoolers (age 3-4) about numbers, shapes, colors, or letters.

 Project Steps

  1. Plan Your Project

  2. Getting Started With PowerPoint

  3. PowerPoint Tutorials

  4. Create Your Presentation

Plan Your Project

  1. Decide whether your project will be about numbers, letters, shapes or colors.
  2. Plan how you will use photos, illustrations, movies, or sounds (You can use the Insert Tab/Sounds menu to record your voice or other sounds).
  3. Consider ways to have the preschooler learn quietly and ways to have him or her be active in the learning process.
  4. Think about what the preschooler can DO to learn about your subject.
  5. Create a storyboard for your project that shows the flow of your lesson. You will upload this in your project submission. A sample is below:

    Goal of Lesson - To teach the letter L
    Audio Visual
    Today we are going to  learn about the letter L! The letter L
    What do you know that starts with L? L dancing


  6. Then open PowerPoint or your presentation software and begin!

Getting Started With PowerPoint

Do you know how to:

  1. Choose a theme
  2. Choose different layouts for each slide
  3. Choose different colors, fonts, and font sizes
  4. Insert pictures and audio
  5. Insert charts, graphs, tables, and shapes

If yes, you can skip ahead to the Do It! section. If not, go through the video and interactive tutorials.

PowerPoint Tutorials

Find your version of PowerPoint:

  1. Open a new PowerPoint Presentation (any theme works).
  2. Click on “File”.
  3. Click on “Accounts”. The version will be to the right.

Create Your Presentation

Now you will create your presentation. Keep in mind the attention span of a preschooler. Keep your presentation short and include things for the student to do - don’t simply talk or lecture to them. Practice your script a few times before recording it.  You will submit your presentation for your project submission.
