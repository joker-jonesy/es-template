Do It! Snap Circuits Electricity: Conductivity and Predictions

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to test different materials to see if they conduct enough electricity to make a light glow. Not all materials are the same when it comes to conducting electricity. Do you think you could send electricity from one end of your pencil to the other? What about across a penny? With a simple circuit setup, you will be able to find out for yourself. Then, write and test multiplication or addition expressions using odd and even numbers. This will be done to make predictions about whether the sums or products will be odd or even.

 Project Steps

  1. Build a Circuit

  2. Test Conductors and Insulators

  3. Make and Test a Predictions

  4. Look for a Rule

Build a Circuit

Build a Circuit- You can use the battery pack or the AC adapter for your circuit. Build the circuit shown here.

Feel free to use any color of the jumper connection wires. Use spacers under the two-snap wires so that they rest on the table.

Did you notice that the circuit you built does not provide a continuous path for the electricity to flow around the circuit?

Test Conductors and Insulators

Conductivity Test—A conductivity test is when you test different materials to see if they conduct electricity. First, make sure your circuit works by touching the two jumper connectors together: The light should turn on. 

Now, collect at least 10 different materials to test.

Before you start your experiment, download the Conductivity Test Table.

Use the first column to list test materials. Then, make a prediction about whether you think each material is a conductor or an insulator. Save your Conductivity Test Table to your student folder.

Now, test your materials.

  1. Touch the metal ends of the jumper wires to separate sides of one of your materials.  Does the light glow? If so, how bright?
  2. Conductors will conduct enough electricity to make the bulb glow while insulators do not. Is your material a conductor or an insulator?
  3. Repeat this test for each of your materials and record your observations.

Include a picture of your circuit, your test table, and your observations in your project submission.

Make and Test Predictions

Make predictions about what happens when you add or multiply certain numbers. You will test your predictions and then look for a rule that you could use to make more informed predictions about similar numbers. You will use the same skills of predicting and observing to test different materials for how well they conduct electricity. 

Before you start your investigation, download the Patterns with Numbers Test Table.

To investigate patterns with numbers, decide whether you will work with addition or multiplication. Write 10 expressions made up of two numbers (for example 2 + 5 or 2×5). Include a variety of odd and even numbers in your expressions. These expressions do not have to be in numerical order or consecutive.

Make a prediction about whether the expression will result in an odd or even value.

In the observation column, include models or diagrams that verify the answer to the expression.

Look for a Rule

Look for a pattern in your results and decide whether you have enough information to accurately predict the outcome of other expressions. Document the pattern you found and the mathematical rule that defines it.
