Do It! Sphero: Draw, Design, and Solve

The Challenge

Robots come in all shapes and sizes. You will discover that a robot can even take the form of a ball and do many amazing things.  

Your challenge is to learn how to control Sphero using the Draw Canvas. Then, design your own custom animation for Sphero 's LED matrix. Finally, use Sphero to help you solve addition and subtraction problems that include fractions with different denominators.  

 Getting Started

You will use the Sphero Edu app to control your robot. If you do not already have one, create an account on Sphero Edu.  

Getting started is simple.  

  1. Make sure your Sphero is charged. You will see a solid white light on the charger when it is fully charged. 

  1. Log into the Sphero Edu app on your device.  

  1. Tap the "Connect Robot" button and select your robot to connect to your Sphero. (Your device ID is written just below the little white Sphero logo on the internal hardware of the Sphero.) 

  1. Select the Activities icon on the bottom of your device screen.    

  1. Select the Sphero tab at the top of the screen. 

 Project Steps

  1. Challenge 1: Draw Canvas

  2. Challenge 2: Design a Path and Write an Algorithm

  3. Challenge 3: Design an LED Matrix Animation 

  4. Challenge 4: Use Sphero to Help Solve Math Problems 

Challenge 1: Draw Canvas

Choose the activity Draw 1: Shapes. You will learn how to control Sphero using Sphero's Draw Canvas.  Complete steps 1-3 presented in the Sphero Edu app. If your Facilitator says you have time, you can also do the robot drawing in step 4.

Key Skills:

  • Aim Sphero. 
  • Draw shapes using Sphero.
  • Learn how to undo a mistake.
  • Program Sphero to change colors as it moves.

Challenge 2: Design a Path and Write an Algorithm

Now that you know how to control Sphero, decide on actions you want Sphero to take, and in what order. 

Navigate back to Home and go to the Programs icon. Select the Plus sign (+) at the bottom right of your screen to start a new Draw program. Draw a path on the Draw Canvas that includes color changes as well. Use the design specifications below as a guide. 

Design Specifications:

  1. Sphero should change color at least twice. 
  2. Sphero should change direction at least four times. 
  3. Optional: Change speed. 
  4. Optional: Set up Sphero ramps for Sphero to drive over. 

Take notes about what you learn in each step of the process. Take screenshots of your designs, and videos of Sphero in action to upload for your Project Submission. 

Also, take screenshots of the sensor data. You can select Sensor Data from the menu (three vertical dots) at the top right of the screen. The sensor data shows the actual path Sphero travels. While the sensor data should look like your drawing, it may look different if Sphero runs into an obstacle, falls off a table, or drives over different surfaces. 

  1. Write an algorithm for your design on paper. 

  1. Start a new Draw program and try to recreate your design by following only the written algorithm (Do not look at previous drawings or photos.) 

  1. Test your design with Sphero: Did it repeat the same color, speed, and direction changes? If not, which part of your previous instructions did you leave out? 

  1. Make changes to your current drawing and algorithm until your Sphero is able to do the same actions as in your first program. 

Reflect 

  • What were the challenges of creating an algorithm that Sphero could follow?  
  • What changes did you make to the algorithm or path so you could get Sphero to repeat the same action(s)? 

Challenge 3: Design an LED Matrix Animation

Now that you have experience controlling Sphero and using the Draw Canvas, it is time to explore and control the LED matrix. This requires just one block of code.  

Go back to the Activities icon in the leftmost column of your device screen and select the Sphero tab immediately to its right. Scroll down or use the search at the top to find: Matrix. Follow steps 1-3 and step 5 presented in the Sphero Edu app. If you have time, you can also complete step 4, which asks you to create a longer, more complicated animation.

Challenge 3: Use Sphero to Help Solve Math Problems 

The word problems with fractions that you see in math lessons are a small sample of real- world mathematical problems you may face in your everyday life.  

For example, you may need to adjust the amount of ingredients for a recipe based on the number of people eating dinner. If a recipe makes enough servings for four people, but you only must feed two people, you can halve the ingredients. 

Fraction models help you visualize just how fractions can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided. They make you better equipped to solve real-world problems involving fractions. 

Using your Sphero to find equivalent fractions is a great way to visualize fractions that have the same value. Sphero can be used to display fraction models that represent real-world problems or can be programmed to navigate among equivalent fractions. Take your skills to new heights by creating math problems for your Sphero to solve.
