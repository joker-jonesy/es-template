Do It! Comic Life: Introduction to Comic Life

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to use the Comic Life application to tell a story in the form of a comic strip.

 Project Steps

  1. Create a Thoughtful Comic Strip

  2. Plan Your Comic

  3. Plan Your Questions
  4. Get Started with Comic Life

  5. Share Your Comic

Create a Thoughtful Comic Strip

  1. Plan Your Comic: Brainstorm ideas and then pick one to write about. Consider the planning reflection questions to help you develop a thoughtful story.
  2. Find pictures: Look for the pictures that will best illustrate your point.
  3. Write the story: Make an outline first, figure out what will happen in your story.
  4. Get Started with Comic Life: Find a template or start from a blank one, design a comic strip that will give you the look and feel you want to support your story.

Plan Your Comic

You could write a story about:

  • A famous person that made you laugh
  • A time when you scored a goal for the other team
  • A subject in class that has you puzzled
  • The fire you almost started the first time you baked
  • Your favorite joke and change the ending
  • What happens when you do not follow a safety rule
  • Favorite part of a movie and change the characters or ending to make it funny
  • What your pet would say if it could talk

Plan Your Questions

  • Comics tell a story - What will your story be about? What point do you want to get across to your audience?
  • Comics use a sequence of events to tell a story - Where will your story take place? What will your characters do in your story?
  • Comics make their point with humor - How will you use humor to get your point across?
  • Comics use words and images to tell a story - Will you use only images to convey your ideas or will you use images and words?

Get Started with Comic Life

On your computer, click on the icon to start Comic Life.

  1. Choose a template.
  2. Click on Panel Layouts. Drag and drop the panels to the workspace.
  3. Add images from your desktop or a folder. Use the camera option or search images on the Internet. Add words and caption balloons.
Explore the toolbar. This is where you will be able to change the text style, font and color, zoom in and out of the screen, change caption styles and colors, and more.

Share your Comic

Print or screenshot your comic when you have finished creating it for your project submission. Share your comic with a classmate.
