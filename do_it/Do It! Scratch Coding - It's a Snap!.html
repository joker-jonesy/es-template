Do It! Scratch: Check Your Work with Computer Programs

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create your own animation with Scratch. If you are new to Scratch, you will complete the Getting Started Tutorial. Then you can program your animation! Once you are comfortable with Scratch, use the standard algorithm to solve multi-digit multiplication and then check your answers using a simple Scratch program.

 Getting Started

If you are new to Scratch, or you want a refresher, complete this tutorial.

Do you have a Scratch account? Before you begin, ask your facilitator if you need an account. If you need to save so you can work on your project later you will need an account.

Go to the Scratch website tips page. Click on the "Try it!" button.

Click the play button to complete the first step.

When you finish a step, click the right arrow to move on. Complete each step.

Perform Multi-Digit Multiplication by Hand

On a piece of paper, start by multiplying using the standard algorithm to find the product of the multi-digit numbers. Be sure to show your work.

Solve three multi-digit multiplication problems of your choice using the standard algorithm.

  • If there are 19 students that log in to Scratch 22 times each, how many times have they logged in?
    • 19 × 22
  • If there are 111 schools with 45 students that sign up for a Scratch account, how many new accounts will there be?
    • 111 × 45
  • If a malware analyst has 13 computers that contain 2,726 errors, how many errors will the malware analyst need to resolve?
    • 2,726 × 13
  • If 900 teachers in the state teach 100 different students, how many students are there altogether?
    • 900 × 100
  • If a multimedia artist has 32 clients and must create 29 animations for each client, how many will the multimedia artist need to complete?
    • 32 × 29

Check Multi-Digit Multiplication in Scratch

Follow these step-by-step instructions to program an animation that will check your math.

Step 1

  1. Click on Events (the yellow dot on the far left-hand side).
  2. Drag the 1st option, “when the green flag is clicked”, to the middle box.

Step 2

  1. Click on Looks (the purple dot on the far left-hand side).
  2. Drag the option “say Hello! for 2 seconds” to the middle box, connecting it with the first box.
  3. Add two more of these boxes. Ensure they each connect to the other boxes.

Step 3

  1. Click on Operators (the green dot on the far left-hand side).
  2. Drag the option containing empty bubbles with a multiplication symbol * in the middle of the bubble, placing it into the Hello! section of each purple statement (see the image below for clarification).

Step 4

  1. Fill in the empty bubbles of the green Operator blocks with the  numbers from your chosen multi-digit multiplication problems. See the example pictured below.
    1. 17 * 54
    2. 12 * 15
    3. 13 * 19
  2. Press the green flag at the top left of the animation pane to run the code and find out the product for each of your multiplication problems.
  3. If a solution does not match with the one you have, stop and recalculate your work with the standard algorithm (step-by-step).
