Advanced Animation Ideas

Time Your Animation

How many drawings will you be adding to your compilation? You will need to think about how long you want your drawings to play for and adjust the timeline tool based on what you decide.

You may want to play with the timeline tool first, before setting specific times for your animations. When you do slide the arrow to adjust the length of time that animations play, watch what happens to the times shown in fractions of a second along the top of the images shown in the timeline tool. What happens as you slide the arrow?

Once you're comfortable with the timeline tool, try to set your drawings to play for a certain amount of time. For example, if you had one animation that you want to play for 70/100 of a second and another for 8/10 of a second,  what is the total length of time that the two animations would play?

To determine this, you would add the two fractions together. But, to add fractions, you first need to find common denominators. To do this, find equivalent fractions by multiplying or dividing both the numerator and denominator of one or both fractions so that they both have the same denominator. Since 100 is a multiple of 10, you can either multiply so that both have a denominator of 100, or divide so that both have a denominator of 10.

8/10 x 10/10 = 80/100

*Multiplying both the numerator and the denominator by 10 produced an equivalent fraction that has a denominator of 100.

*Both fractions now have a common denominator of 100, and they can be added together to determine the length of time that the two animations would play.

80/100 + 70/100 = 150/100

Consider what the sum represents; what does it mean if an animation plays for 150/100 seconds?

150/100 can be rewritten as 1 50/100, or 1 1/2 seconds. Thus, the two animations will play for a total of 1 1/2 seconds.

The rate of frames that Do Ink plays is three frames for every 1/10 of a second, or 30 frames every one second. Calculate how many frames would play if you wanted to play one animation for 10/10 of a second and the other for 80/100 of a second.

Remember that you may need to use equivalent fractions to find common denominators before you can add fractions!

Again, be creative! Play with the timeline tool and use math to try to set your own times!
