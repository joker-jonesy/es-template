Do It! Stellarium: Travel Through Space

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to discover the many planets in our solar system as seen from Earth. Use a protractor to determine the angles between interesting objects in our solar system, and then use that information to write directions your partner can follow to locate your solar system objects.

Taking Screenshots

To set up Stellarium for screenshots:

  1. Go to the Tools tab of the Configuration window, which is found in the left menu.
  2. Go to the "Screenshots" section shown below. Click the folder icon and choose where to save screenshots.
  3. If you want to print your images, check the "Invert colors" box to save ink.
  4. Now, whenever you see something in the sky you like, press CTRL + S. An image will save to your folder. If you are using a Mac computer, press Cmnd + S.

Planet Search

Learn more about each of our planets by exploring them directly in Stellarium. Can you find all seven of the other planets in the sky?

  • Go to the Date/time and Location settings in the left menu. Make sure they match your current time and place.
  • Go to the SSO tab of the Sky and viewing options window in the left menu.
  • Check the "Show planet markers" box and make sure that “Solar System objects" is also checked.
    This marks each planet and moon in the sky.

Search the sky on your own, or use the Search tool to go to each planet.

  • What does the planet look like?
  • Is the planet above or below your horizon (the line between the sky and the ground) right now?
  • Do you see any moons? How many?
  • Take some screenshots of the planet and its moons.

Stellarium can also show you the night sky as seen from other planets.

  • Go to the Location window and go to a different planet (like Mars) from the "Planet" dropdown menu.
  • Can you find Earth and our Moon from this new planet?
  • Take screenshots of what you see.

Measure Angles

Once you find a part of the night sky that is especially interesting to you, take a screenshot. 

  1. Open Google slides and insert the screenshot of your night sky.
  2. Choose at least three objects and mark them on your Google slide using the drawing tool. Use the line tool in Google slides to draw a horizontal line through the object that is nearest to the bottom of the page. Then, draw lines from one interesting object to the next in order to show the path you will take to get from that first object to the others. 
  3. Copy and paste the image of the protractor shown here onto the same Google slide. 
  4. Use your first object as the vertex of the first angle, which is the very center of the protractor. Your horizontal line should go through the 0° line at the bottom edge of the protractor, and the line to your second object will form the other ray for your angle. Measure the angle. Then move your protractor, this time using the second object as the vertex. The line you just travelled should go through the 0° and line up with the bottom edge of the protractor. The line to the next object will form the other ray. Measure this angle. Continue until you have measured all angles.
  5. Now, measure the distance for each of your lines to the nearest one-fourth inch.
  6. Challenge a friend or two to draw your route using your specific measurements. Have them start with the first point and a horizontal line. Give them the first angle measure and the distance of the line to the second object. Direct them to make a point for the second object and see if it matches up with the pathway you created on your printed night sky.
  7. Continue with describing your pathway to your friends. See if they can successfully follow the entire route. Then try theirs.
