Extend Yourself - Snap Circuits Electricity: Build Series and Parallel Circuits

  • Check out the Snap Circuits Kid Creations website to see what other kids have done. Design and submit your own circuit.
  • Explore holiday light strings. Holiday light strings can use series circuits or a combination of both series and parallel. If you have a holiday light string at home, remove a bulb and see what happens. Can you figure out what kind of circuit it is? How do blinking light strings work?
  • While struggling to create the long-lasting light bulb, Thomas Edison once said, "I have not failed. I’ve just found 1000 ways that won’t work!" What do you think he meant by that? Discuss it with your partner, friend, or parents.
  • Go online to learn more about Thomas Edison or Humphrey Davy. What else did they invent? Who else made important contributions to the invention of the light bulb?
  • How do compact fluorescent lamps (CFL bulbs) work? How are they different from incandescent bulbs? How do they save energy? Go online to learn more.
  • What are LED bulbs? Do they have advantages over incandescent bulbs? Find out online.
  • The total area of the Snap Circuits grid is 54 units2.  How many series circuits could be built on the grid? What would be the perimeter and area of each? How many parallel circuits could be built on the grid?  What would be the perimeter and area of each? Use your snap circuit kit to build the circuits and record your findings in a two-column table.
  • Explore Fractional Relationships with Conversions. Create fraction models for other measures: 1 yard = 3 feet; 1 cm = 10 mm.  Can you think of others?
