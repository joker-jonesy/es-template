Do It! Wixie: Exploring Polygons and Patterns 

The Challenge

Your challenge is to use Wixie to draw polygons including rectangles and calculate their perimeter and area. Then, make patterns by rotating and reflecting rectangles with polygons.

 Project Steps

  1. Find Perimeter and Area of Rectangle

  2. Find Perimeter of Other Polygons

  3. Make Patterns by Reflecting and Rotating Shapes

Find Perimeter and Area of Rectangle

  1. Draw a rectangle.
  2. Click on the arrow in the Tools.
  3. Choose the centimeter ruler.
  4. Measure the length and width of your rectangle. You can rotate the ruler by moving the dot.
  5. Use the Text Tool to show how to find the perimeter. Add a text box, then use the numbers on the keyboard. To add a plus sign, click the shift key and the plus (+) key at the same time. To add an equal sign, just click the equal (=) key. Use the spacebar to put spaces between your numbers and symbols.
  6. Also use the Text Tool to show how to find the area. Remember: area = length × width.
  7. Repeat Steps 1 to 6 on a second page. This time, make a rectangle that has the same perimeter but a different area than your first rectangle.
  8. Repeat Steps 1 to 6 on a third page. This time, make a rectangle that has the same area but a different perimeter than your first rectangle.
  9. Take screenshots of your rectangles, including the perimeters and areas.

Find Perimeter of Other Polygons

  1. Draw a polygon that is not a rectangle.
  2. Click on the arrow in the Tools.
  3. Choose the centimeter ruler.
  4. Measure the lengths of each side of your polygon.
  5. Use the Text Tool to show how to find the perimeter. Add a text box, then use the numbers and symbols on the keyboard to show how to find the perimeter.

Make Patterns by Reflecting and Rotating Shapes

  1. Choose a rectangle. 
  2. Copy and paste the shape several times.
  3. Rotate and reflect the shapes to make a pattern.
  4. Take a screenshot of your rectangle pattern.
  5. Now, do this with your polygon. Make a pattern by copying, pasting, rotating, and reflecting the shapes.
  6. Take a screenshot of your rectangle/ polygon pattern.
