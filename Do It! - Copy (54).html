Do It!

Your challenge is to build a motorized fan that runs off of renewable energy. Start with a LEGO design and then make changes to meet your challenge.

Wind Power: Build a Turbine

Build a wind turbine and use it to charge the energy meter. Record your observations using the worksheet. When you are done gathering all of your data, charge the energy meter so the joule counter reads 100 J. Ask your facilitator for a fan to simulate wind.

In a wind turbine, wind hits the blades and turns the turbine. This turns a motor and creates an electrical current. This electricity is then either stored for later use or sent to homes through the power lines.

Make a Simple Fan

Now that your turbine is built and your energy meter is charged, do the following:

1. Remove the plug from the back of the energy meter.

2. Attach it to the front of the meter.

3. Turn the dial to the right, then to the left. What happens? Record your observations.

4. If you use up all the stored energy, put the plug back into its original position (back of the energy meter) and recharge using your wind turbine and fan.

 

Fan Reflections

If you wanted to use your machine as an actual fan, consider how it could be improved.

  • Which way would you turn the orange dial? 
  • How many fan blades would you use?
  • Which direction would the fan blades face?

What is the difference between the plug on the front and the plug on the back of the energy meter?

New Fan Design: Store Energy AND Use Energy

The energy you generated with your wind turbine is stored energy in the energy meter.

The stored energy is used up when you run your fan.

How could you redesign your machine to fan you while it generates and stores energy? 

HINT: You may need some additional parts from the renewable energy kit as part of your redesign.

Record and Reflect

As you work, remember to:

1. Record your observations and reflections.

2. Take pictures or video of your work.

3. See what you can do to improve your fan.

4. When you make changes, retest your fan and compare to previous tests. Did your results change?

Share your motorized fan with other groups. Compare how you each solved the same problem in different ways. What parts of your solutions were similar? How were they different?
