Do It!

Your challenge is to light colored beacons as you get electricity to flow through a circuit. You will choose challenge cards that tell you to light certain colored beacons. See how many challenges you can solve!

Getting Started

Instruction Manual and Legend Cards

Read the Circuit Maze Instructional Manual for complete instructions. There are also two Legend cards. The legend cards review the challenge icons, game tokens, the switch token, and the token polarity (or the way the current needs to flow for the circuit to work). Be sure to review these cards or the instruction manual so you understand all the parts of the game. 

How to Play

  1. Choose a challenge card. You will want to start with beginner, but eventually, you can also try intermediate, advanced, or expert.
  2. Look under Challenge. You will see the goal of the card, which is to light up specific colored light beacons. It may say to light the yellow beacon, as seen here.
  3. Look at the big picture of the grid. Add the pieces you see to your grid. Make sure they are in the right spots and facing the right directions. 
  4. Look under Add to Grid. Figure out where to add those pieces in order to reach the goal.
  5. The Solution to the card is on the back. But try not to look! Challenge yourself to solve the card on your own.
  6. If you cannot figure out the solution, try solving a simpler card. The cards are numbered 1-60 with 1 being the easiest. 

NOTE: Except for the Finish Token and the Switch Token, every metal strip connector on every token should connect. You should not have a metal strip leading to nowhere. The Finish Token is different because it has three metal strips and for different challenges, you may have one, two, or all three strips used. The Switch Token is also different: for some challenges, one side of the switch may be meant to stop the current flow or force it to loop back.

Did you solve your challenge? Try another!

Switch Challenges 

More advanced cards may have three rows of challenge icons. These are Switch challenges. They show you what should happen when the switch is in these different positions.

For example, Challenge card 18 (seen here) shows that when the switch points left, the red and yellow beacons are on but the green is off. When the switch is pointing straight, the yellow beacon is on but the others are off. When the switch points right, the yellow and green beacons are on but the red is off.

ePortfolio Ideas

Choose two or three challenge cards that you think were particularly hard to solve. Explain how the circuits work. 

Take pictures of your circuits, even if your solutions did not work. Once you solve the challenge or review the solution, you can explain what you tried to do originally, and how you changed your circuit to make it work.

Here are some questions you can answer about your circuit:

  1. How did you make sure to light all beacons that were supposed to be lit?
  2. How did you make specific beacons stay off (if that is what they were supposed to do)?
  3. If you used a switch, how did it affect your circuit?
