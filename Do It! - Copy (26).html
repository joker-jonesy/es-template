Do It!

Your challenge is to build and explore the properties of polygons, polyhedrons, and Platonic solids.

You will first explore 2D and 3D shapes with Goobi. Then you will build as many Platonic solids as you can. ReviewWhat You Should Know: Platonic Solids if you need more information.

Build 2D Shapes 

Let’s begin our exploration by building polygons with Goobi. 

  1. Start with a bar
  2. Continue to add bars until you are ready to form your shape.
  3. Place a ball between every bar to help hold the shape together.  

Now take a few minutes to examine your shape. What do you notice about it (number of faces, angles etc)? What polygon did you make?

       

Build 3D Shapes

Now, turn your polygon into a polyhedron. Remember a 3D shape does not lay flat like a 2D shape, you can turn it around to see the edges, faces, and vertices. 

For example, a rectangle (polygon) can be transformed into a rectangular prism (polyhedron).

            

A regular prism is not a platonic solid! Why not? What are the characteristics of a platonic solid?

Make it a Platonic Solid

Using what you now know about platonic solids, take another look at the 3D shape you made. Is it a platonic solid? Why or why not? If it is not a platonic solid, how can you make it a platonic solid? 

  1. You can try using the tripod to support your shape. You can attach up to 3 bars with a tripods.

  2. Remember to connect bars with balls to create the most structurally strong structure. 

When you finish building, compare your platonic solid with your classmate's platonic solid. What is similar about the shapes? What is different? Are there things in your world that resemble your shape? Record your findings into your E-Portfolio.
