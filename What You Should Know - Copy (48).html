Simple Machines: Gears and Pulleys

If you did the Kicking It! and Gearing Up! challenges, you have already seen some types of simple machines. 

                                                 

Now look at the simple machines used in the Hungry Alligator robot. Together, the gears and pulleys open and close the mouth.

  

Spur Gears

The teeth of a spur gear are straight. Gears are a type of wheel and axle simple machine. The edge of the wheel has teeth, which fits into he teeth of other gears. If one gear is turned, the other gear turns in the opposite direction.

 

Crown Gears

While spur gears are good for making chains of gears in straight paths, sometimes we want to change the direction of the gear motion. To do this, we can use bevel gears. This type of gear has its teeth on its side instead of its outer edge. This means it can  One type of bevel gear is called a crown gear. Compare the teeth of the spur gear to the teeth on the crown gear. Did you notice that the crown gear looks like a crown?

   

Why Use a Crown Gear?

We can combine a spur and a crown gear to change the direction of spin. 

If a spur gear is spinning vertically (up and down), the crown gear will spin horizontally (sideways).

All About Pulleys

Your alligator also uses a pulley. A pulley is a combination of two wheel and axle machines connected by a rope or chain.

What can you do with a pulley?

  • A pulley changes the direction of a push or a pull like you see in the picture with the boy.
  • Pulleys can make several parts of a machine spin at the same time like you see on a bike.
  • A motor and small pulley can also turn a belt, like in a car engine.

  

Troubleshooting Robots

Sometimes a robot does not do what we want. Since robots do only what we tell them to do, we need to check our work.

If a robot does not work, answer these questions:

  • Did you build it correctly?
  • Does it have electricity?
  • Is the computer program correct?
