Do It! Comic Life: This or That?

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create a comic then research how much it will cost to print a ship the comic and how much money you will make as the creator.

A newspaper publisher comes to you and wants you to create a story about you and a friend who can’t agree on something. Maybe you can’t decide whether to eat this or that ice cream, what game to play at recess, or whose idea to use for your project. You get to come up with your own idea for the comic! 

After creating the comic, the publisher wants you to make 150 copies of the comic and send them to the office to be put in a special edition newspaper. You will need to research how much it will cost to print (in color) 150 copies of the comic and how much it will cost to ship the comics. You will then need to determine how much you will be paid for your work by taking the total amount you spent and doubling it. 

 Project Steps

  1. Create Your Outline

  2. Find, Save, and Upload Your Images

  3. Create Your Comic

  4. Start Your Invoice

  5. Print and Mail Your Comic

  6. Finish Your Invoice

Create Your Outline

On a sheet of paper, write and/or sketch an outline of the information that you want included in your comic. Be sure to include:

  • Characters
  • Settings
  • What disagreement will your characters have?
  • Who will have the final say?
  • How do you want your comic laid out?

Find, Save, and Upload Your Images

  1. Using your outline, search for images that you want to use for your comic.  
  2. Websites with free use of images include but are not limited to Pixabay, Pics4Learning, Pexels, and WikiCommons. 
  3. Once you find an image you want to use, click the Download button. The image will download straight to your Download folder unless you change the location.
  4. You will need to transfer any images you find from your Download folder to your C: drive.

Create Your Comic

Using your outline, begin to create your comic. To do so, do the following:

  1. Choose a template or create your own.
  2. Add comic panels.
  3. Add images and drawings. 
  4. Add details with the toolbar.
  5. Add your final touches.

Start Your Invoice

Complete the top of the This or That Invoice form with the following information: 

  • Your zip code
  • Date
  • Your name

The invoice needs to remain open or on hand for the remainder of the project.

Print and Mail Your Comic

For the next part of the challenge, you will gather information on how much it will cost to not only print copies of your comic in color, but how much it will cost to mail them.

Select each item to learn more.

Print Your Comic

You need to print 150 copies of your comic for the newspaper publisher. Using Staples.com, you need to determine how much it will cost to print that quantity. Knowing how to use websites like Staples can help if you need to print out documents in the future and you do not have a printer at home. 

How Much Will it Cost to Print?

  1. On the Staples website, click Start Project.

  2. The Staples website displays a pop-up window asking you to upload your document. Find your document and upload it completely. Then, click Continue.

  3. Next, you’ll enter the Printing Builder section. Select 8.5”x11” Letter size paper. Click Next.

  4. For paper type, click Standard Paper and then Standard White. Click Next.

  5. Make sure Color Ink is highlighted, and then click Next.

  6. Make sure No Finishing is selected, and then click Next.

  7. Change the quantity of copies you are required to print and click Review Summary. The Staples website recalculates the new pre-tax subtotal. For purposes of this project, we will use that price.

NOTE: If your comic is more than one page, you will need to make sure the quantity of prints includes all pages.

  1. On your invoice, there is a line for Amount for Copies. Write in the amount.

Mail Your Comic

After printing out copies, they need to be mailed to the publisher overnight. Using the USPS website, you can determine how much it will cost to send mail overnight as well as an estimate of when the package will arrive.

How Much Will it Cost to Send? 

  1. Using the USPS website; only change the following information:

    • Zip Code Mailing FROM: [Your zip code]
    • Zip Code Mailing TO: 90012
    • Date Plan on Mailing: (choose the next day)
    • Time Plan on Mailing: (leave it on the time is says)
    • DO NOT CHECK ANY BOXES
    • Choose View Flat Rate Envelopes.

  2. Look for Priority Mail Express 1-Day™ Flat Rate Envelope. It should be the first option. 

  3. Use the price for the Normal Delivery Time; Retail.

  4. On your invoice; write in the numbers for Amount for Mailing.

Finish Your Invoice

To finish your invoice sheet, follow these steps:

  1. Add the cost of printing and the cost of mailing together and write this amount in the subtotal area.

  2. Multiply the subtotal by two to get the payment amount, and fill in that number in the spot provided.
