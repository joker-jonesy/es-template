Get Started

Let’s start cranking! Your challenge is to produce energy using a hand crank. Can you produce enough energy to operate a motor? Lights? A fan?

  • How should you design your circuits to give these devices enough power to run?
  • Can you get these devices to run even when you are not turning the hand crank?

To succeed in your challenge, you probably want to explore the hand crank and complete some introductory projects first. These experiences will give you the skills and experience needed to build and teach others about your final challenge projects.

Explore the Hand Crank 

To succeed in your challenge, you should understand how your hand crank works.

  • Gently turn the handle. What happens? 
  • Look inside the hand crank.
    • What is happening to the gear that is touching the handle?
    • How many complete turns does this first gear make for every complete turn of the handle? 
    • How many complete turns does the last gear make for every turn of the hand crank?
  • Place the hand crank on the base. Connect it to the motor and add the fan. 
    • What happens to the fan when you turn the hand crank clockwise?
    • What happens when you turn the crank counter-clockwise?
    • What do you think is happening when you turn the crank in different directions?

Record your observations and analysis.
