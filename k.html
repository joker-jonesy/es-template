Do It! K'NEX STEM Simple Machines: Build and Measure with Levers

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to learn how simple machines work by building levers with the K’NEX STEM Building Solution kit. 

What do the scissors, the wheelbarrow, and the seesaw have in common? They are all simple machines that use levers to make work easier. Having a friend lift you off of the ground on a see-saw is much easier—and more fun—than trying to jump that high by yourself. Once you learn about levers, you will use K'NEX to build a pan balance model, and use the model to determine the weight of five objects.

Pan Balance Lever Challenge

A pan balance is a type of scale that is a simple lever machine. It has a beam, a fulcrum, and pans on the left side and right side of the beam. Objects are placed in both pans. When the pans are balanced, that means that the objects on one side have the same weight/mass as the objects on the other side. 

  1. Build the pan balance from the K’NEX instruction manual. 
  2. Use page 1 of the K'NEX Levers Guess the Weight Table (PPT) or K'NEX Levers Guess the Weight Table (PDF) to record. 
  3. Attach a 10-gram weight to the right side of the hanging tray. 
  4. Measure the distance from the hanging tray on the right side to the fulcrum. Record the distance in the table.
  5. Attach one of your objects to the left hanging tray.
  6. Move the hanging tray with your object toward the fulcrum until it is balanced.
  7. When the lever balances, measure the distance between the object and the fulcrum. Record the distance in the table.  
  8. Repeat the same process for your other objects. When you have recorded all the distances, look for a pattern. 
  9. Based on the pattern, try to guess the weight of the objects. Record your guesses in the “Guess the Weight” column of the table.
  10. After you guess the weights, examine the data again and look for a relationship between the distance from the fulcrum and the weight of the objects when the scale is balanced.
  11. Go to page 2 of the Guess the Weight Google table. Fill in the blanks to explain the relationship between the distance from the fulcrum and the weight of the objects when the scale is balanced.
  12. Go to page 3 of the Guess the Weight table. Fill in the blanks to write an equation that represents the relationship between the distance from the fulcrum and the weight of the objects when the scale is balanced.

If you need some help guessing the weight of your objects, try the following activities:
