Complete the Circuit

Your first challenge is to complete a basic circuit using the power pack, colored conductive dough, and an LED light. 

  1. Roll two balls of conductive dough.
  2. Connect the red cord from the power pack to one ball of dough and the black cord from the power pack to the other ball of dough.  
  3. Separate the metal probes on the LED and stick one into each of the balls of conductive dough. 
  4. Turn the switch on the power pack on. You can use the slide switch to increase and decrease the flow of electricity. Did the LED light up?
  5. If your LED did not light up try switching the probes. The long side of the metal probe must connect to the positive side of the battery. 

Experiment with Insulators

Your second challenge is to add an insulator to your circuit. Remember an insulator is not a good conductor of electricity. What do you need to do to complete the circuit with an insulator? 

  1. Roll two balls of colored conductive dough.
  2. Connect the red cord from the power pack to one ball of conductive dough and the black cord from the power pack to the other ball of conductive dough.  
  3. Place a ball of insulating white clay between the two balls of conductive dough. 
  4. Decide where to put the LED probes. Should the probes be placed in the white insulating dough, in one colored conductive dough, or in both colored conductive dough? 
  5. Turn the switch on the power pack to the "on" position. You can use the slide switch to increase and decrease the flow of electricity. Did the LED light up?
  6. If your LED did not light up, turn off the battery pack and try moving the probes. What do you already know that can help you figure out where to put the metal probes of the LED?
