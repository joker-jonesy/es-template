Information About Comic Life 

What is Comic Life?

Comic Life is an application that allows users to provide information in a comic design. When using it in the classroom, it can be used in all subjects. Comic Life allows you to add your own images, create your own designs, and add your own information and thoughts. It is a creative way to learn information and present information to others. Comics are short, which helps narrow down the most important information.

How Do You Use Comic Life?

Choose a Template

When you open the Comic Life app, it will bring up the Choose Template page. This is where you start your comic adventure. You can choose a premade template, or you can even start with a blank page and create something completely on your own. 

Saving and Using Images

Using images in Comic Life is more than a one step process. It is important that images are downloaded correctly and no violations of copyright laws are done. There are several websites that provide free images for you to use. These websites include but are not limited to Pixabay, Pics4Learning, Pexels, and WikiCommons. You will need to save any images you find to your C: drive in order to get them into Comic Life.  

Once you find an image you want to use, click the Download button. The image will download straight to your Download folder unless you change the location.

Modifying/Adding Text

To add or modify the text, there is a toolbar located at the bottom of the Comic Life screen. If there are text bubbles or information strips, you can click and type your information into it. If you want to add more text, you can click the text bubble/box you want and drag it anywhere in the comic you need it.  

To delete any text boxes/bubbles, just click on them and press the Delete button on the keyboard.

To add a title or larger words, you can click Lettering and then type the word(s) you want. You can change the colors by clicking the Fill button at the top of the page.

Additional Help

For additional help, you can go to the Help tab in the Comic Life app.

The help tab has the following:

  • A starting guide
  • How to write scripts
  • Adding shapes, text, and conversation bubbles
  • Tips and tricks to make your Comic Life experience easier

Explore On Your Own

Practice using Comic Life by creating a short comic about food chains. 

A food chain shows how animals are related to each other by what they eat. For example, grass is eaten by a grasshopper, which is then eaten by a mouse. All three are connected.

Steps:

  1. Choose your template.
  2. Find, save, and add images of grass, grasshopper, and mouse to your comic.
  3. Add speech bubbles to narrate what each animal eats.
  4. Add arrows to show the flow of energy.
