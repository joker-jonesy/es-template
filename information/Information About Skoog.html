What is Skoog?

The Skoog is a customizable electronic musical instrument and programmable control device. The Skoog has 5 colors on each side of the cube except for the bottom. Each color represents a different note. You can make your Skoog play 5 notes from an instrument like a piano or flute, you can have your Skoog play one note from 5 different instruments, or you can even use your Skoog to record 5 different noises and play those.

How Do You Use Skoog?

Turn Skoog On

To turn on the Skoog push the small black button on the bottom of the Skoog, a green battery light illuminates, click the small black button again and the Bluetooth icon flashes in the display on the red side.

Download the Skoog App

Skoog requires an iPad or an Apple device to operate. Open the Skoog app in the Digital Communications folder on your iPad or download the free Skoog app onto your Apple device. In the app click the Skoog icon in the top left corner and search for the Skoog device.

Connect to the Skoog

  • Step 1: Open the Settings app on your iPhone or iPad.
  • Step 2: Click on Bluetooth.
  • Step 3: Find your Skoog on the list of My Devices.
  • Step 4: Click the (i) logo for more information.
  • Step 5: Click Forget This Device.
  • Step 6: Reconnect your Skoog using the Skoog app.
  • Step 7: When prompted to "Pair" the device click "Cancel" instead of "Pair." Do this every time you connect.
  • Step 8: Select the Skoog to connect. The Skoog will calibrate then you are ready to play music.

To change the musical instrument, click the guitar on the menu bar on the top.

To change the notes and scales clink the music note. The default is the C-Major pentatonic scale.

Play a Song

To play a song click the Songbook icon in the menu bar at the top of the app. Scroll down and select Old MacDonald from the list. Follow the colored pattern using your Skoog to play the notes. What do you notice about the music notes? How are the 5 notes represented? Try to sing along while you play. Pick a different song to play. Try changing the instrument.

Turn Off Your Skoog

It's important to turn off your Skoog when you are finished to save battery life. To turn off your Skoog, hold down the button on the bottom of the Skoog until a red battery light flashes then let go of the button.
