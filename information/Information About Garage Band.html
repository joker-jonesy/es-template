What is GarageBand?

GarageBand is a digital audio workstation, it lets you record your own music and you can use instruments from the GarageBand library. You can play the sounds from a keyboard, guitar, bass, drums, strings, and more. You can record using these instruments and even sing along with the music. This app also allows you to edit and share your musical recordings.

How Do You Use GarageBand?

Getting Started With GarageBand

Each instrument has a play area and a control area. You can play melodies, chords, and sounds by touching the instruments and change the sounds by using the knobs, buttons, and other controls in the controls area.

  1. In the Sound browser, swipe left or right to view the instruments, and tap the one you want to play.
  2. Tap More Sounds to see additional sounds for that instrument.

Control Bar

The control bar is located at the top of the screen. It has controls that let you move to different parts of GarageBand like control playback, recording, and volume. You can also use the control bar to open the Loop Browser and song change settings.

The navigation buttons on the left include:

  • The My Songs button saves the current song and opens the My Songs browser, where you can create and save songs.
  • The Browser button  opens the Sound browser, where you can choose an Instrument to open the Live Loops Grid. Touch and hold the Browser button to choose a different Instrument.

  • The Tracks View button  lets you switch between the current instrument and Tracks view. Touch and hold the button while using an instrument to switch to another instrument in the song. The Tracks View button is available after you make the first recording in Tracks view.

  • The Grid button  lets you switch between the current instrument and the Live Loops grid. The Grid button only appears if a Live Loops grid has been created for the song.

  • The Track Controls button opens the Track Controls for the currently selected track.

The controls for playing the song (called transport controls) in the center include:

  • The Go to Beginning button  moves the spot to the beginning of the song. While the song is playing, the Go to Beginning button changes to a Stop button.
  • The Play button  starts the song playing, or stops it if it's currently playing.
  • The Record button  starts recording. To stop recording and stop the spot, tap the Play button.
  • The Master Volume slider lets you change the overall volume of the song. The Master Volume slider is also controlled by the iPad’s volume buttons.
  • The Metronome button starts and stops the metronome click.

Customize Loops

Drag your Loop into the Tracks area and make changes so it sounds even more like your contact.

  • Apply a patch to change the timbre, effects and other settings.
  • Change the tempo or other project properties as needed.
  • Change the key and scale if you want the notes to start at a higher or lower pitch, sound brighter, or more melancholy.
  • You can make these changes to the overall project or just specific tracks or regions.

Exporting

Depending on how you want to save and share your project, here are a few options:

  • Save and Share Project to iCloud
  • Save and Share to Media Browser
  • Share Project using AirDrop
  • Share Project by Exporting to Disk

Explore On Your Own

If you are new to GarageBand, be sure to start with the tutorials on the help page. Look up unfamiliar terms in the Glossary.

Go to the Loop Browser and search through the moods. (You may need to change the view to find the “moods” category.) Find a mood that fits with one of your contacts. Listen to the Loops until you find one that reminds you of your contact.
