What is Squishy Circuits?

Squishy Circuits is a hands on kit that allows you to explore electricity. Use conductive and insulating dough to build circuits using resistors like lights, motors, and fans.

How Do You Use Squishy Circuits?

Squishy Circuits Components

Create a Circuit

Use Squishy Circuits to build a circuit using insulating and conductive dough and resistors. Create a circuit by placing each end of the LED light bulb in the conductive (colored) dough. The longer LED terminal should go in the dough that also has the red battery pack wire.

Short Circuit

A short circuit occurs when the electrical current does not follow the correct path or circuit. In a normal circuit, there is a power source and wires connecting to a motor or light. The motor or light is called a resistor and when electricity flows through it some of it is used up making the buzzer, beep or the light, glow. Without a resistor, the electricity takes a shortcut and builds up in the wires, this can become very dangerous. In a short circuit, wires can heat up enough to cause a fire or explosion. If you pushed the conductive dough balls to touch in the circuit, the electricity flowing through would skip the LED light and cause a short circuit because the resistor has been left out of the circuit. A short circuit could also happen if you touched the positive and negative battery terminals together.

Storage and Cleaning

Dough Storage

It is recommended that you store your conductive and insulating dough in a plastic bag to keep it moist for as long as possible. If you notice your dough is starting to dry up, here are some suggestions that could help:

  1. Make sure to always close your dough container after use and store your dough in a sealed plastic bag. If the dough is starting to get dry, try adding a few drops of water or wrapping the dough in a wet paper towel overnight.
  2. You can make your own conductive and insulating dough following these recipes.
  3. You can purchase play-doh and clay products in place of the Squishy Circuits conductive and insulating dough.

Battery Safety

After using the Squishy Circuit power pack you should always make sure it is turned off and the batteries are removed before storing it. This will help the batteries last longer and prevent a short from happening if the metal battery terminals touch.

Cleaning

The salt from the conductive dough will affect the metal on the battery terminal and on the LED probes. The salt is corrosive which means it eats away at the metal coating. Your Squishy Circuits will last longer and operate better if you gently wipe the probes off with a wet paper towel before putting them away. The probes on the LEDs are very fragile so try not to bend them back and forth when you are cleaning them.
