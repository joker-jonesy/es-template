What is Google Maps?

Google Maps is an online mapping program developed by Google that allows you to explore the world or your backyard, plan trips, and find places. You can view satellite imagery, street maps, 360-degree panoramic views of streets (Street View), real-time traffic conditions (Google Traffic), and route planning for traveling by foot, car, bicycle, or public transportation.

How Do You Use Google Maps?

To use all the Google Maps features you will need a Google account. Open to the Google Accounts Login Page. If you have a Google account, sign in. If you need to create an account, select, “Create an account now” and follow the directions. 

Search for a Location

You can use Google Maps to search for any location on Earth. You can search for locations without a Google accounts, but if you want to use all the features you will need to use a Google account to login. Google Maps automatically opens to your current location, use the Search tool to change the location.

Map Viewing Options

There are different tools located in the top right, bottom right, and bottom left of your Google Map. 

Street View

Expand the Explore toolbar at the bottom right corner to see street views, photos, or video tours of Boston. You can also access the street view of a location if you drag and drop the yellow person at the bottom right of your screen on any location on the map.

Satellite View

Satellite View: Expand the Google Maps menu at the top right corner and click on Satellite to change from a simple map of Boston to a photographic view of Boston from above. 

Routing Tools  

When planning a trip, it is useful to draw routes from one place to another. For directions, click the blue Directions button to the right of the search bar. Type in as many locations as you want, and even rearrange the order in which you would like to visit them. You can create a path or route by car, bus, bike, plane, or foot.

Create a Custom Map

You can create a custom map for every trip you take. On this map, you can save destinations, or places you want to visit, and how you will travel there. When you're finished, you can share your map with your friends and family.

  1. Click on the icon with three lines to open the Google Maps menu.
  2. Click on Your places.
  3. Click on the MAPS tab.
  4. Click on CREATE MAP at the bottom of the menu.

Create Layers

You can organize your destinations into different categories, like restaurants, museums, fun things to do, hotels, and more. Add a Layer to your map for each category. You can rename the layer, change the color of each pin that marks the saved location, and rearrange your list of saved locations.

Add to a specific layer by clicking on the layer, clicking on a destination, and then clicking on + Add to map.

Map Tools

Use the map tools to:

  • add a marker
  • draw a line from one place to another
  • add a driving, walking, or biking path
  • add directions
  • measure the distance between two places

Save Your Map

As you explore, you can save the places you would like to visit on your virtual tour. When you click on a destination, an information box will pop up. To save this place to your map, click on + Add to map.

Explore On Your Own

Practice using the Google Maps tools. Open up Google Maps and use the Search tool to locate the Old North Church in Boston, MA.  

  • Add a star to save the location of the Old North Church.
  • Zoom in on the Old North Church.
  • Change to Satellite View and tilt and rotate your position. Try the 3D view.
  • Use Street View to place yourself at the church doorstep.
  • Look through some of the photos of the inside of the church.

  

Boston is an old city with lots of US history. Pick another one of these historic locations to explore.

  • Save the new location on your map.
  • Use street view to take a close up screenshot of your location.
  • Take a photo or video tour of the location.
  • Add a path showing directions from the Old North Church to the place you picked.
    • Find the distance to the Old North Church to your second location.
    • Find the time it takes to go from the Church to your location.
