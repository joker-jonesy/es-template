What is Makey Makey?

Makey Makey is a computer controller like your keyboard or mouse.

Makey Makey turns almost anything that conducts electricity into a tool you can use to tell a computer what to do. You can use Makey Makey to create projects that mix sound, music, art, everyday objects, and electronics. People have used Makey Makey to:

  • Turn flowers and fruit into musical instruments
  • Make a musical staircase
  • Use Play-Doh as a game controller
  • Create a talking water fountain
  • Make interactive clothing

How Do You Use Makey Makey?

Like a telegraph or keyboard, Makey Makey allows you to complete a circuit and send information with a pulse of electricity.  Using Makey Makey, you can turn many objects into keys. In order for the objects to work, they must conduct electricity. Metal objects like wire or aluminum foil work well. Some other usable objects may seem surprising:

  • Bananas, apples, and most fruits
  • Flowers
  • Hands and fingers
  • Play-doh

Makey Makey Parts

The Makey Makey kit comes with many different parts- a USB cable to connect to your computer, alligator clips and wires to connect to different conductive objects, and a circuit board to program.

Makey Makey Circuit Board

The front of the Makey Makey circuit board has 12 inputs. An input is a place on the board where the computer gets information.  Most of your work will use the top of the Makey Makey. This is what you will use when you turn the Makey Makey into a keyboard and mouse, a controller, or a musical instrument. You can use your alligator clips to connect your circuit board to your computer.
