What is Padcaster, Google Translate, and Do Ink Green Screen?

The Padcaster Studio Kit is a set of audio-visual recording equipment. The equipment includes an iPad, tripod, monopod, microphone kit, teleprompter, angle lens, green screen, and more. This equipment is used to create a variety of videos from vlogs to films to instructional content. The Padcaster Producer app is used to record and add special effects to the video.

Google Translate is an app used to translate words, sentences, and paragraphs from one language to another. It is a helpful app that can be used when trying to translate what you would like to say to someone else in another language. It is also useful to translate what someone may be saying to you in a different language.

Google Translate is an app that uses computer algorithms to translate from one language to another. Its process is not the same as how those that are native to the country may speak. This tool is excellent for temporary use but it is wise to be aware of possible errors that may occur.

Do Ink Green Screen is an app that uses green screen technology to combine videos and images from the device it is used on (iPhone or iPad) and live images. The user can input a number of different backgrounds, add text, animation, change colors of the images, and more.

How Do You Use Padcaster, Google Translate & Do Ink Green Screen?

Setting Up The Padcaster Equipment

Please note that instructions for assembly should be included with your Padcaster equipment. Be careful when you use the equipment because parts of it are fragile.

  1. Find the tripod and open up the legs. There are three settings for the height and latches to lock them into place. The center column also adjusts up and down. The handle can be attached to the side.
  2. Insert iPad into the Padcaster iPad case. Be sure that the lens of the iPad is matched up to the opening for the lens on the Padcaster iPad case.
  3. Attach the Padcaster iPad case to the tripod/monopod.
  4. Choose the lens you would like to use and attach it to the Padcaster iPad case (if desired).
  5. Attach the microphone to the top of the Padcaster iPad case.
  6. Attach the light (if desired)
  7. If you did not attach a lens, you can attach the teleprompter (if desired).

Padcaster Producer App

This Padcaster Quick Start Guide gives details about the effects, tools, and how to use the Padcaster Producer app.

Follow these steps to export your video when it is finished:

  1. Click the icon in the bottom left corner.

  1. On the next screen, click the video you want to export.
  2. Click the button in the top-left with a rectangle and arrow combined.

  1. Choose email or file sharing. Ask your teacher to know which option to choose.

Google Translate

Google Translate is very user-friendly. The main screen displays the original language and the language to translate to at the top of the screen. There is a section just beneath to input the text. The app allows for the upload of images, conversation, and transcription of the text to translate as well.

Do Ink Green Screen

Do Ink created a user-friendly way to combine images and videos to allow the user’s creativity to shine. There are a number of features included with this app. This is the perfect app to create a video about traveling around the world without having to actually travel anywhere.
