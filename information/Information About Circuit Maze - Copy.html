What is Circuit Maze?

Circuit Maze is an electric puzzle game where you connect pathway pieces to build a circuit and light up a beacon.

How Do You Use Circuit Maze?

Legend Cards

The legend cards review the challenge icons, game tokens, the switch token, and the token polarity (or the way the current needs to flow for the circuit to work).

How to Play

  1. Choose a challenge card. You will want to start with beginner, but eventually, you can also try intermediate, advanced, or expert.

  2. Look under Challenge. You will see the goal of the card, which is to light up specific colored light beacons. 

  3. Look at the big picture of the grid. Add the pieces you see to your grid. Make sure they are in the right spots and facing the right directions. 

  4. Look under Add to Grid. Figure out where to add those pieces in order to reach the goal.

  5. The Solution to the card is on the back. But try not to look! Challenge yourself to solve the card on your own.

  6. If you cannot figure out the solution, try solving a simpler card. The cards are numbered 1-60 with 1 being the easiest.

Every metal strip connector on every token should connect except the Finish Token and the Switch Token. The Finish Token is different because it has three metal strips and for different challenges, you may have one, two, or all three strips used. The Switch Token is also different: for some challenges, one side of the switch may be meant to stop the current flow or force it to loop back.

The Direction of Current Flow

Look at the example circuit, the arrows that show the direction of the electrical current. The direction is important because some electrical parts work differently—or not at all—depending on which way the current flows. The positive (+) and negative (-) sides of a power source, like the battery shown here, are called poles. 

In Circuit Maze, electricity flows from the positive sign (+) to the negative sign (-). Look at the blue power supply in your set. You can see that one side (the Start Token, which has the batteries) has a plus (+) sign to show that the electrical current starts flowing from there.

Look at the beacon tokens in your set. Each beacon also has a plus (+) and minus (-) sign on different sides of the piece. Current flows from the Start Token to the plus side of the beacon. The current flows out of the beacon from the minus side and continues down the path, back to the Finish Token (negative side of the blue power supply).

Avoid Short Circuits

A short circuit in a house can cause wires to heat up enough to cause a fire or explosion. A short circuit happens when the electrical current flows straight from one end of the power supply to the other without passing through a resistor.  Without a resistor, there is too much current flowing through the path; this can cause the batteries to overheat and can damage the game tokens. The Circuit Maze does not have enough power to cause a fire or explosion if a short circuit, but it could damage parts and break the Circuit Maze. 

In each of these examples, electrical current flows from one side of the blue power supply to the other without passing through a beacon (resistor).
