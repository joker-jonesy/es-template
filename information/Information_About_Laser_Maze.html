What is Laser Maze?

Laser Maze is a game that uses tokens (mirrors, beam-splitters, check points, and blockers) to direct a laser through a series of mazes to light up a target.

How Do You Use Laser Maze?

Instruction Manual

Each kit comes with an instruction manual that explains the parts of the game and how to play the game. There is also a symbol card that shows the lazer pieces and how they work. Be sure to review these cards or the instruction manual so you understand all the parts of the kit.

Game Tokens

Select each item to learn more.

Laser

The laser provides the laser beam. Push the button at the top of this token to turn on the beam.

Target/Mirror

The target/mirror can be used as a target, a mirror, or both. Only the transparent red side of the token works as a target. It will light up red when the laser has reached it.

Double-Mirror

Both sides of the double mirror token reflect the beam 90 degrees.

Beam Splitter

The beam splitter splits the beam in two paths. One path is reflected 90 degrees and the other path passes straight through the token.

Checkpoint

The checkpoint serves as a token the laser must pass through.

Cell Blocker

The cell blocker prevents other tokens from being added to the board without getting in the way of the laser.

Challenge Cards

There are 60 challenge cards that range from beginner, intermediate, advanced, to expert. Each card has the number of targets and pieces you will need to use on the front of the card and the solution on the back.

Laser Light Safety

As you work to direct the laser light, it is important to take precautions. You do not want to hurt you or anyone else. The red laser light in the ThinkFun Laser Maze is a Class 1 Laser- this means that it is not as strong as some other lasers and it is safe for you to use in your classroom; however, it is important to remember that any concentrated light can harm your eyes. DO NOT stare directly into the laser light or shine it towards the face of people or animals as it could damage your eyes and cause blindness.
