What is Climate Kids?

NASA’s Climate Kids website is a website with a collection of games, activities, videos, and articles that will help you explore climate change and Earth science.

How Do You Use Climate Kids?

Big Questions Tab

You can use the Big Questions Tab to find information about:

  • What is climate change?
  • Why is carbon important?
  • What is the greenhouse effect?
  • How do we know the climate is changing?
  • What is happening in the ocean?

Weather and Climate Tab

Use the Weather and Climate Tab to learn the difference between weather and climate and take a closer look at how NASA collects and studies weather data.

Atmosphere Tab

Use the Atmosphere Tab to learn more about the Earth’s atmosphere & NASA missions that studied the atmosphere.

Water Tab

Water is essential for life on Earth, use the Water Tab to see how NASA satellites observe where Earth’s water is found and how that is changing over time.

Energy Tab

Use the Energy Tab to investigate air pollution, fossil fuels, and energy from the sun.

Plants & Animals Tab

Investigate climate change, ecosystems, coral bleaching, ocean acidification.

Interactive Tabs (Games, Activities, People, Videos, and Mystery)

At the bottom of each page, you will find a collection of games, activities, people, videos, and more for each topic. Use the Interactive Tabs to learn even more about climate and weather.

NASA Tab (Logo)

National Aeronautics and Space Administration or NASA is part of the U.S. federal government and is in charge of the space program. NASA created the Climate Kids website to help students learn about weather and climate changes and their effects  To learn more about NASA, you can click on the NASA logo on the top of each page.
