Photos: Basic Photo Editing with Photos (For PC)

Your Challenge

It is important to have photos in your ePortfolio. You can use a program called Photos for PC to crop and edit your photos.  

What You'll Learn

  • Students will learn about basic photo editing for PC devices.

Stuff You'll Need

  • Photos software program
  • Pictures (from your camera or free use images from the internet)

What You Should Know

What Can Photos Do?

With Photos for PC, you can...

  • Crop
  • Rotate
  • Change how the picture looks (like its colors and brightness)
  • Draw or hand-write notes (This is different than typing text, which cannot be done with this program.)

Photos can also be used to edit videos.  

Complete Instructions

You can find basic instructions below. For the complete official instructions for Photos on Windows 10, go to the Microsoft Support Site. 

Basic Skill Overview

Open an Image

You may want to take your own photos. Or, you may use online images for your project or ePortfolio.

TO OPEN: Open File Explorer to find where you saved your image. Double-click your image; the image should automatically open in Photos. If not, right-click the image and select "Edit with Photos".

Edit an Image

To EDIT: Go to Edit & Create > Edit > Crop and Rotate (button at the top). 

  • To Crop, click and drag the crop outline.
  • To Flip or Rotate, click on those buttons. 
  • To undo all changes, click Reset. 
  • When you are done with your changes, click Done.

NOTE: If you want to draw or write on your image after editing, save first. Then, go back to the Edit & Create menu and choose Draw. 

Easy Enhancements and Pro Adjustment Tools

To change how the image looks, select Enhance. 

TO AUTO-ENHANCE:

  1. Click the magic wand under Enhance Your Photo. 
  2. Drag the white line right or left. This will make the image brighter and crisper or dimmer with softer edges. 

TO APPLY A FILTER:

  1. Pick a filter under Choose a Filter.
  2. Drag the dial right or left to adjust. This will change how bright and crisp the image looks.

FOR ADVANCED CHANGES: Click Adjust to change light, color, and other effects manually. 

NOTE: If you want to draw or write on your image after editing, save first. Then, go back to the Edit & Create menu and choose Draw. 

Draw or Write on an Image

Drawing and handwriting on a picture can highlight important things.

TO DRAW: 

  1. Choose Edit & Create > Draw.
  2. Pick a drawing tool: pen (round tip), pencil (draws like a highlighter), or calligraphy pen (chiseled tip).
  3. Click on the down arrow below the drawing tool to choose a color and size option. 
  4. Click and drag to mark your image. (Using a Wacom tablet and stylus will help you make clearer lines and letters.)
  5. Use the eraser to remove marks.  
  6. Click the Save icon when done. 
    • When you draw on an image, Photos records the order in which you write or draw. After you save, you can watch the recording by clicking on the three dots menu and choosing Show as a Living Image.
    • Once saved, click the Share button to share your image as a still photo or as a video (that shows your drawing process).

NOTES:

  1. There is no way to type text on your image using the Photos program. To add text, you will have to choose Edit & Create > Edit with Paint 3D after saving. This will send you to a program called Paint 3D where you can add text and other features to your picture.

  1. If you want to edit your image after drawing, save first. Then, go to the Edit & Create menu again and choose Edit.
