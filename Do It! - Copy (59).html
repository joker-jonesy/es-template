Do It!

This Liftoff Challenge has three parts. Your first challenge is to learn how to control Sphero BOLT using the Draw Canvas. Your second challenge is to design a path for Sphero BOLT to follow, and colors for Sphero BOLT to flash. When you are happy with your design, you will write your own unique algorithm so that you can make Sphero repeat its actions at another time. Finally, your last challenge is to design your own custom animation for Sphero BOLT's LED matrix and create a very simple program to show off your design.

Getting Started

You will use the Sphero Edu app to control your robot. If you do not already have one, create an account on Sphero Edu.

Getting started is simple. 

  1. Make sure your Sphero BOLT is charged. You will see a solid white light on the charger when it is fully charged.
  2. Log into the Sphero Edu app on your device. 
  3. Tap the "Connect Robot" button and select your robot to connect to your Sphero BOLT. (Your device ID is written just below the little white Sphero logo on the internal hardware of the Sphero BOLT.)
  4. Select the Activities icon on the bottom of your device screen.   
  5. Select the Sphero tab at the top of the screen.

Challenge 1: Draw Canvas

Choose the activity Draw 1: Shapes. You will learn how to control Sphero BOLT using Sphero's Draw Canvas.  Complete steps 1-3 presented in the Sphero Edu app. If your Facilitator says you have time, you can also do the robot drawing in step 4.

Key Skills:

  • Aim Sphero BOLT.
  • Draw shapes using Sphero BOLT.  
  • Learn how to undo a mistake.
  • Program Sphero BOLT to change colors as it moves. 

Challenge 2: Design a Path and Write an Algorithm

Now that you know how to control Sphero BOLT, decide on actions you want Sphero BOLT to take, and in what order.

Go to the Programs icon at the bottom of your screen in order to start a new Draw program. Draw a path on the Draw Canvas that includes color changes as well. Use the design specifications below as a guide. 

Design Specifications:

  • Sphero should change color at least twice.
  • Sphero should change direction at least four times.
  • Optional: Change speed.
  • Optional: Set up Sphero ramps for Sphero to drive over.

Take notes about what you learn in each step of the process. Take screenshots of your designs, and videos of Sphero BOLT in action for your ePortfolio.

Also, take screenshots of the sensor data. You can select Sensor Data from the menu (three vertical dots) at the top right of the screen. The sensor data shows the actual path Sphero BOLT travels. While the sensor data should look like your drawing, it may look different if Sphero BOLT runs into an obstacle, falls off a table, or drives over different surfaces.

                                   

Create Your Algorithm 

  1. Write an algorithm for your design on paper.
  2. Start a new Draw program and create your design by following only the written algorithm (do not look at previous drawings or photos).
  3. Test your design with Sphero BOLT: Did it repeat the same color, speed, and direction changes? If not, which part of your previous instructions did you leave out?
  4. Make changes to your current drawing and algorithm until your Sphero BOLT is able to do the same actions as in your first program.

Again, take notes, videos, and screenshots all along the way.

Reflect

  • What were the challenges of creating an algorithm that Sphero could follow? 
  • What changes did you make to the algorithm or path so you could get Sphero to repeat the same action(s)?

Challenge 3: Design an LED Matrix Animation

Now that you have experience controlling Sphero BOLT and using the Draw Canvas, it is time to explore and control the LED matrix. This requires just one block of code. 

Go back to the Activities icon at the bottom of your device screen and select the Sphero tab at the top. Choose the activity called BOLT: Matrix. Follow steps 1-3 and step 5 presented in the Sphero Edu app.

If you have time, you can also complete step 4, which asks you to create a longer, more complicated animation.

Be sure to take screenshots and videos of your animation for your ePortfolio.
