Getting Started

Laser Light Safety

As you work to direct the laser light, it is important to take precautions. You do not want to hurt you or anyone else. The red laser light in the ThinkFun Laser Maze is a Class 1 Laser- this means that it is not as strong as some other lasers and it is safe for you to use in your classroom; however, it is important to remember that any concentrated light can harm your eyes. DO NOT stare directly into the laser light or shine it towards the face of people or animals as it could damage your eyes and cause blindness. 

Review the Instruction Manual 

Read the Laser Maze Instruction Manual for complete instructions. There is also a symbol card that shows the pieces and how they work. Be sure to review these cards or the instruction manual so you understand all the parts of the game. 

Control Your Laser

Description

Piece

Straight Line- Laser beams travel in a straight line in one direction.

Changing Directions- Lasers cannot change direction without help. Scientists use mirrors to direct laser beams where they want them to go. If a mirror is placed directly in front of a laser, the beam will bounce back to the laser and may cause the laser to break. If the laser is pointed towards a tilted mirror in this kit, the beam will reflect off the mirror at a 90-degree angle.

Beam Splitter- A beam-splitter is a tool that takes the laser beam and splits it into two different paths. One path is reflected 90 degrees, while the other path passes straight through.

Straight Line Challenge

Laser beams travel in a straight line in one direction. Set up your board using the red laser piece and a purple target. Your goal is to light the purple target. You can change the orientation of all the pieces as you place them on the board. 

Changing Directions Challenge

Your challenge is to use the red laser piece, a purple target, and a blue mirror to change the direction of the laser beam to make it light up the target.  

Beam Splitter Challenge

A beam-splitter is a tool that takes the laser beam and splits it into two different paths. One path is reflected 90 degrees, while the other path passes straight through. Try to light two purple targets at the same time by using the red laser piece and the green beam splitter and two purple target pieces. *Don't forget that the part of the laser beam that reflects at a 90-degree angle will travel in the direction of the tilt.  

Document Your Work

Take a picture of the laser you created and explain how the laser light travels from the laser to the target. 

Here are some questions you can answer about your laser:

  1. How did you pick a starting place for your laser beam piece?
  2. What were some moves you tried? What did you do to make the laser light the target? 
  3. How do mirrors affect your laser beam?
  4. What are objects light can travel through? What are some objects light cannot travel through?
