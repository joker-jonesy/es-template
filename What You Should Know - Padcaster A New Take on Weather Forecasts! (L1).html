Important Vocabulary

  • Integer - a positive or negative whole number (does not include a fraction or a decimal)
  • Absolute Value - the distance of a number from zero on a number line (regardless of being positive or negative)
  • Number Line - a vertical or horizontal line that contains numbers spaced out evenly in both directions (can include positive and negative numbers)
  • Thermometer - an instrument used to measure temperature

Integers

Integers are positive or negative whole numbers. Integers do not include a fraction or a decimal. Examples of integers include 4, -4, 67, -67, -11 and many more. The opposite of negative four (-4) is positive four (4). The opposite of eleven (11) is negative eleven (-11). Integers are used in real life for a multitude of things. Some examples include money, sea levels, altitude, temperature, and much more. 

Integers can be displayed on a number line. A number line is a vertical or horizontal line that contains numbers spaced out in numerical order in both directions. This means numbers can be positive and negative on a number line.

Absolute Value

Absolute value is the distance of a number from zero on a number line, regardless of whether it is positive or negative. Zero is the starting value between positive integers and negative integers. When determining the absolute value, it is always positive because distance is always positive. For instance, -6 is 6 spots away from zero. Therefore, the absolute value of -6 is 6. Thermometer 

A thermometer is an instrument used to measure temperature. Temperature is one of many things provided in a weather report. Temperature helps an individual to know if it will be really hot outside or possibly extremely cold outside. A thermometer is similar to a vertical number line. The measurements are displayed as integers and include both positive and negative values. All values below zero are negative integers. Most thermometers do not include a negative sign. Providing an audience with a thermometer to display the temperatures allows them to have a visual to better understand the weather today, tomorrow, and the next few days to come.

Career Connection and Real-World Application 

Meteorologist

Meteorologists are scientists who study weather. Meteorologists take measurements and make observations to best predict upcoming weather patterns. Meteorologists work for a variety of companies as well as independently if they choose to do so. A career in meteorology requires a strong educational background in science and mathematics.

Broadcast Meteorologist

Broadcast meteorologists are often known as weather announcers. Broadcast meteorologists provide the public with the information gathered by other meteorologists. Sometimes it is possible that the broadcast meteorologist gathers the information themselves. Broadcast meteorologists may work for a television network, radio station, or website. Broadcast meteorologists use a green screen with graphics and animated maps to provide weather forecasts on television or over the internet. Broadcast meteorologists have an opportunity to start as an intern and work their way up.
