Do It!

Use the infrared thermometer to measure the temperature of various city materials including concrete, metal, windows, asphalt, planters, and street signs.

   

Using the Infrared Camera

The infrared thermometer can be used portably to collect individual material temperature or with the computer and the Go!Link cord to create a graph of the temperature readings with the Logger Lite software. 

  1. Turn on the infrared thermometer (IR thermometer) by pushing the MEAS button (this is required for portable use and if you are using the computer to collect and review data). 
  2. Point the sensor at the material you are measuring the temperature of (the IR thermometer works only in degrees Celsius, but it will convert the temperature for you if you use the Logger Lite software and select the Switch C/F button).  See the What You Should Know section to learn how to convert degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Celsius. 
  3.  The temperature measurement is acquired from a circular region of the material. The laser button on the IR thermometer helps you determine what spot your sensor is collecting temperature data from. The closer you are to the object you are measuring, the more accurate your reading. The farther away it is, the bigger the data spot. Use caution when using the tool. 
  4. The Hold button will freeze the temperature measurement when you are ready to write down your data. To un-freeze your temperature reading, press the Hold button again. 
  5. To turn the sensor off, push the MEAS button again. The sensor will automatically shut off in 30 minutes. 

Collecting and Analyzing Data

The Logger Lite Software automatically creates a graph displaying the data it collects. If you collect the data without a computer, make a t-chart or presentation of the materials and the temperatures. What do you notice about your graph? What information is the graph showing you? Why is this information important? Record your observations in your ePortfolio and share with your class.
