Do It! Dash: Create an Obstacle Course

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to program Dash to perform pet tricks to get it ready for the Wonder Workshop Dog Show! Then, create an obstacle course for Dash to get through, and write numerical expressions to represent each of the commands required to make Dash move.

 Getting Started

Getting Started with the Blockly App.

The puzzles in the Blockly app will teach you how to program Dash with code blocks.

  1. Open the Blockly app, and press the "Play" button.
  2. Select your profile.
  3. Click on “Puzzles.”
  4. Click on the first puzzle and follow the directions. When you complete it, more puzzles will unlock.

Tip: How do you skip Blockly challenges?

Challenges can be skipped one puzzle at a time.

  1. Open Blockly “Puzzles.”
  2. Select the puzzle you want to skip.
  3. Hold four fingers on each side of the iPad or tablet.

Train Dash

Complete the Robot Puppy Training Puzzle.  

As you complete each puzzle, consider the following:

  • If a program did not work the first time around, what did you do to correct it?
  • Did you learn anything new about programming Dash?
  • Take a video or photos of Dash completing your favorite challenge and a screenshot of the code.

Teach Dash a New Trick

Choose another trick to teach Dash so it could perform it at the Wonder Workshop Dog Show! Develop your own program to have Dash perform this trick. Remember that the order matters when putting together a sequence of movements.

To write your own program, follow these steps:

  1. Exit the Puzzle section by clicking on the arrow at the top left.
  2. Select “My Projects.”
  3. Select “Create New.”
  4. Select “Blank Project” and hit the “Create” button on the bottom right.

Need some trick ideas? Check out the results from the 2020 AKC Trick Dog National Competition.

Create an Obstacle Course

Create a new project. Make an obstacle course for Dash to get through and use the “Drive” blocks to direct Dash around the obstacles. Keep in mind that the farthest distance Dash can drive using one code block may not be far enough for your obstacle course. If that’s the case, how could you get Dash to drive farther?

Expressions of Code

Take a look at the code you used to direct Dash around the obstacles. Now turn that code into numerical-expression form using the table below.

You can focus on just three movements at a time as seen in the example below. Put a parenthesis around the first two values as shown, and then interpret the expression. You do not have to solve the expression.

Example:

If Dash starts by moving 10 cm, then moves forward 50 cm, then left 40 cm to get past a cone, it is represented as (10 ⋅ 50) - 40 in numerical expression form. To interpret the expression, you could say “(10 ⋅ 50) - 40 is 40 less than 10 ⋅ 50.”

Notice that you skip the “Mathematical Meaning” for the first number in the sequence since you can’t multiply by nothing; this is why the direction is not even mentioned for the first movement.

Another Example:

If Dash starts by moving 30 cm, moves right 500 to get past a chair, and forward 140 to a table, it is represented as (30+500) ⋅ 140 in numerical expression form. (30+500) ⋅ 140 is one hundred forty times as large as 30 + 500.

Sequence matters!

Create at least five different numerical expressions similar to those above to represent Dash’s movements through your obstacle course. You can modify the obstacle course each time if you would like. (Don’t forget to use the table provided for Dash’s movements.)
