Do It!

Your challenge is to build a scissor lift with LEGO Pneumatics and then explain how it works.

Build a Pneumatic Lift

The best way to learn the basics principles of pneumatics is to build an example. Use the worksheet as a guide as you do all five building projects.

Be sure to include the answers to the worksheet questions in your ePortfolio. 

Download:

  • Pneumatic Lift Student Worksheet
  • Basic Principles Instruction Manual

Basic Principles Reflection 

Now that you have built all five models and answered the questions, write down what you know, what questions you have, and what you want to figure out through your pneumatics project.

If you are still unsure about how pneumatics works, take a look at the pneumatics introduction booklet: What Are Pneumatics.

Build a Scissor Lift

Now that you understand pneumatics, it is time to build a scissor lift powered by air pressure. Use the worksheet as a guide.

Download:

  • Scissor Lift Student Worksheet
  • Scissor Lift Instruction Manual

Scissor Lift Reflection 

When you are done building and testing, answer these questions:

  1. Were your predictions in the worksheet correct? Why or why not?
  2. Can you follow the force of the air with your finger to see how the lift is able to rise?
  3. What knowledge from the basic principles activity helped you understand and build the lifts?
  4. What changes could be made to the lift in order to make it even better?
