Do It!

Challenge a team to create YOUR maze.

Design a maze that Ozobot can follow. Then, write clear, detailed instructions so another team can recreate your design. This activity uses markers and paper or an iPad.

You know you did a good job writing your instructions if the other team can make a very close copy of your maze without looking at the drawing. 

Design Specifications

Follow the design specifications listed here:

  1. Use either markers and paper or the OzoDraw app to create your maze.
  2. Include the following:
    • Two or more intersections
    • One curve or spiral
    • Two 90 degree turns

Steps

  1. Make a maze with the app or markers. 
    • If you use markers, follow the instructions in the Line-Drawing Practice section of What You Should Know.
    • If you use the OzoDraw iPad app, choose Playground or Freeform mode.
  2. Write clear and detailed instructions on how to recreate your design.
  3. Trade your instructions with another group. (Do not let them see your drawing.)
  4. Try to draw the other group's maze from their instructions.
  5. Use Ozobot to try out the maze you drew.
    • Calibrate Ozobot first. Review the Calibrating Ozobot section of What You Should Know to learn how to get Ozobot ready.
  6. See if the other group can figure out the maze you designed and if you can figure out their maze! 
  7. If a group is stumped and cannot draw the maze accurately, this means the instructions are not clear enough. The team that designed the maze should rewrite the instructions.
  8. Both teams should keep rewriting and re-drawing until the designs are right. 
  9. When the designs are right, let Ozobot follow the maze!

Example:

  1. Think about your start and finish. 
  2. Draw your maze with the design specifications above. 
  3. Pick your finish (WIN), Pick your start where you will place the Ozobot.
  4. Add direction codes to guide your Ozobot from the start to the finish of your maze. Does your robot pass through all of your design specifications?

Challenge:

Place your Ozobot anywhere on the maze. Does it still make it to the finish? What codes do you need to add to make sure your Obobot reaches the finish?

Reflect

  1. What details and strategies did you use to make your instructions clear?
  2. Which steps were the most challenging to write? How did you try to make your instructions clearer?
  3. What details did the other team add to make their instructions clearer?

For your ePortfolio, include screenshots, videos, design photos, your instructions, and the maze your classmates created from your instructions.
