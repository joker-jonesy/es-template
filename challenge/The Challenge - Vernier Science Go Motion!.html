The Challenge - Vernier Science: Measure Motion

The Challenge

You are probably used to hearing many sounds: your alarm clock buzzing, the phone ringing, your friends talking, and music playing. 

Have you ever thought about using sound to "see" motion? We tend to rely on our eyes for that, but by using sound waves, we can easily measure where something is located.

Your challenge is to explore different topics regarding the sounds of your own motion, echolocation, and simple harmonic motion using the Vernier Go! Motion sensor. You will conduct experiments that use sound to measure moving objects. Then, you will solve division problems based on scenarios related to the experiments. Finally, you will create a presentation to share your findings.

What I'll Learn 

  • I CAN use sound and a Vernier motion sensor to measure moving objects.
  • I CAN divide multi-digit numbers to determine the distance of an object.
  • I CAN illustrate and explain division using an equation, area model, or rectangular array.

Stuff I'll Need

  • Vernier Elementary Collection
  • Logger Lite Software, Logger Pro Software, or Graphical Analysis Software
