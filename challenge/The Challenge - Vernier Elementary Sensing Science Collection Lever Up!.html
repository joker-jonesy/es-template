The Challenge - Vernier Elementary Sensing Science Collection: Lever Up!

The Challenge

Your challenge is to find materials and create a lever that you can run tests on.  You will use the results of the tests and collected data, to determine how to build the ultimate lever to lift objects.  

What I'll Learn 

  • I CAN create a simple machine that includes a fulcrum and arm.
  • I CAN apply different amounts of physical force to determine how much force is needed to lift a book.
  • I CAN place the fulcrum and sensor in different locations to see if more or less force is needed to lift a book.
  • I CAN create a data table that shows the change in amount of force needed when I changed the position of the sensor and fulcrum.

Stuff I'll Need

  • Vernier Go Direct Force and Acceleration Sensor
  • Vernier Go Direct Force and Acceleration Sensor Instruction Manual 
  • Vernier Graphical Analysis Instructional Manual
  • Computer, iPad, or Tablet with Graphical Analysis 4 Software
  • Lever Up! Data Sheet
  • Download Vernier Graphical Analysis App from Google Play Store Here.
  • Materials (to create lever):
    • Some materials from the Vernier Elementary Accessory Collection
    • Other materials may include empty paper towel rolls, boxes, or cardboard pieces
    • String (tied in a loop) to put on the meter stick to hang your sensor on
