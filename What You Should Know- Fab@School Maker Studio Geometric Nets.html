Important Vocabulary

  • 2D (two-dimensional) Shape - a flat shape with 2 dimensions: width and height
  • 3D (three-dimensional) Shape - a solid shape that has 3 dimensions: width, height, and depth
  • Geometric Net - a 3D shape that has been flattened out or unfolded to show the 2D shapes that form it
  • Polygon - a 2D shape with closed sides and straight sides
  • Volume - the space in a 3D object that (a) can be measured by counting the number of cubes (b) within the object

What Is a 2D Shape?

A 2D shape has two dimensions: height (distance from top to bottom) and width (distance from side to side).

Any shape that can be laid flat on a piece of paper is a 2D, or two-dimensional, shape.

Rectangles, squares, circles, and triangles are all 2D shapes.

                           

What Is a Polygon?

A polygon is a 2D shape with closed sides and straight lines.

Polygons are made of straight lines that all connect. They are closed shapes, meaning they have no openings on their edges.

Watch the video to learn more about polygons.

What Is a 3D Shape?

A shape that is 3D has three-dimensions: height, width, and depth. Think of depth as a thickness that is added to a 2D shape.

Cubes, spheres, rectangular prisms, cylinders, and cones are all examples of 3D shapes. They have height, width, and depth.

                     

                             

What Is a Geometric Net?

A geometric net is a flattened-out three-dimensional shape. If you fold and glue a geometric net, you will make a 3D shape.

What Is Volume?

Volume is the space in a 3D object. Volume can be measured by counting the number of unit cubes within the object. A unit cube is a cube with a side width of 1 unit. This unit cube will have one cubic unit of volume and it is written as 1 unit3. Cubic units are then used to describe the measurement. Cubic units could be cubic inches, cubic feet, etc.

In this example, you can count the number of unit cubes to find the volume. There are 8 unit cubes, so the volume is 8 cubic units.

Look at the example below. The width of the shape is 4 inches. The depth is 3 inches. The height is 5 inches. To find the volume, we can multiply depth × height × width. For this example, you will need to multiply 4×5×3. The volume of this shape is 60 in3.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Volume in Careers

People in many different jobs measure volume. Bakers and cooks measure the volume of many ingredients as they prepare delicious meals and desserts. Movers use volume when they help people move from one place to another. They have to make sure that they have a truck large enough to move all of the customer’s belongings. Shipping companies also use volume. These companies rely on shipping containers and trucks. They must know the volume of these containers and trucks so they know how many boxes and products will fit.
