Do It! Vernier Elementary Energy Efficiency: Save Energy at School

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to figure out the wattage and lumen level of the bulbs in your classroom using a light sensor. Decide if they are energy efficient and if they give off enough light to help you learn. Then, create a bar graph that represents your data.

 Project Steps

  1. Explore Your Classroom

  2. Measure and Record the Lumens

  3. Create a  Presentation

Explore Your Classroom

What bulbs are in your classroom? Make a list of the different kinds of bulbs. Do you think the bulbs are halogen, incandescent, fluorescent, or light-emitting diodes? Can you safely figure out their wattage or volts? Estimate how much the lights are used each day. Use the lumen table to help you figure out the best light strength for your learning space. Do you think you have the best light bulbs for your classroom?

Measure and Record the Lumens

Using the Vernier Light Sensor, measure the light output in lumens for the different light bulbs you find around your classroom. Be sure to point to and measure as many bulbs as possible. Do you see any similarities or differences between the bulbs in the room? 

Record your information on the Lumen Data Collection Table.

Create a Presentation

Decide if your classroom has the best bulbs to maximize student learning and energy efficiency.

Use a presentation tool like PowerPoint or Google Slides to create a presentation demonstrating what you learned about light bulbs, lumens, and watts. Use the information from the data collection chart to design a bar graph that represents your findings. Be sure to include:

  • Screenshots of your work 
  • A bar graph that represents your findings
  • What you learned from the experiment 
  • Any recommendations you have for changing bulbs to increase efficiency and to better light your classroom
