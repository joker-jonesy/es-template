Do It!

Your challenge is to use Pixie to teach about your home state. You will create a reference map of your state after learning how to use Pixie through a Family Portrait activity.

Explore Pixie

Launch Pixie on your computer. Take 10 minutes to explore Pixie on your own.

Can you figure out what some of the menu, tab, and tool buttons do?

Which Version of Pixie Do You Have?

For this Do It activity, you need to know which version of the Pixie software you are using.

Does your SmartLab have Pixie 3 or Pixie 4?  Check with your facilitator to see which version you are using or follow the steps below. 

PC

Go to Help > About Pixie to find out if your SmartLab has Pixie 3 or Pixie 4. 

Pixie 3 and 4 work the same way, and you will be able to do all the activities in this Liftoff Challenge. 

Mac

Go to Pixie > About Pixie.

Do not forget you can always use the Help Menu if you need help using your version.

Introducing My Family Activity

Start with an activity to get used to Pixie. This activity is called Introducing My Family. Choose the activity that goes with the version of Pixie you have.

Introducing My Family Activity on Pixie 4

Introducing My Family Activity on Pixie 3

In this activity, you will make a two-page project about your family and print it as a table tent. You will learn many Pixie skills, including how to use the shape tool, paintbrush tool, and stickers.

Do the project activity twice to create two projects – one for each team partner. Take turns with your partner so you both learn how to use Pixie.

Since learning is different in a SmartLab, you will each do the project about your partner’s family, not your own. You will have to ask your partner questions to learn about his or her family. 

Be sure to save your final projects in your student folder and add it to your project presentation.

Create a State Reference Map

Are you ready for your State project?

Show what you have learned by creating a reference map of your home state. Or, if you prefer, you can pick another interesting state for your project.

  1. Launch Pixie and click on the Project button.
  2. Select New.
  3. Click on either Landscape or Portrait depending on which one will fit the shape of your state better.

Adding State Stickers

Pixie 3

  1. Click on the Stickers Tab. 
  2. Select the Maps folder. Then select the US States folder.
  3. Click on the State you will use for your project. 

Pixie 4

  1. Click on the Library Tab and then click the Stickers folder. 
  2. Select the Maps folder. Then select the US States folder.
  3. Click on the State you will use for your project. 

Adding Content to Your State Reference Map

What are some interesting facts about your state? Go online and find at least 15 interesting facts that you can add to your state reference map. Include pictures and labels to represent each fact.

Interesting Facts About Your State Ideas

  • Location of your hometown or school
  • State Capital
  • Largest Cities
  • Geographic features (mountains, lakes, rivers)
  • State Flag
  • State nickname or motto
  • State Symbols (bird, flower, song)
  • Population
  • Major Industries (oil, farming, technology)
  • Year of Statehood
  • Name of Governor
  • Location of important historical events
  • Landmarks (bridges, monuments)
  • Tourist attractions (parks, sports, museums)

Use all of the following tools/options in Pixie to create your reference map:

  • Pixie Stickers
  • Images downloaded from the web
  • Paintbrush
  • Text
  • Shapes

To use images from the web, save the image to your student folder. Then, click the "Browse for a File" button and select your image.

Did you learn anything new about your state? What were some of the most interesting facts you learned about your state?

Save Your State Reference Map

When you have completed your diagram, click the Project button.

Pixie 3

  1. Select Create Images.
  2. Click Full Page.
  3. Save your work to your student folder as a JPG image.

Pixie 4

  1. Select Export.
  2. Under Export Images, select Normal Layout and JPG Format.
  3. Click the Computer button and save your work to your student folder.
