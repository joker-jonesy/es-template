What is Snap Circuits Electricity?

Snap Circuits Electricity is an electronics kit that allows you to design, build, and experiment with circuits.

How Do You Use Snap Circuits Electricity?

Attaching Pieces to the Grid

  1. Set the Snap Circuits piece on the grid, and line up the snaps with the pegs on the grid or the snaps of another piece.
  2. Put a finger on each of the snaps on the top piece and push evenly.

Removing Pieces from the Grid

  1. Place two fingers on each side of the Snap Circuits piece.
  2. Carefully lift the piece directly up.
  3. If the piece is too hard to remove this way, tilt it sideways to remove.
  4. When removing the battery holder, tilt the holder towards the snaps to remove.

               

Taking Care of Snap Circuits

Each Snap Circuits piece has electrical components to make it work in your circuit. These pieces can be damaged if they're not taken care of properly. Use the tips below to prevent your pieces from being broken.

Avoiding Short Circuits

Always check to see if your circuit has a short before connecting power. Any electrical path without resistance from a load (like a light or motor) is a short circuit. Short circuits will DAMAGE your Snap Circuits kit. Be sure to check all possible electrical paths for short circuits BEFORE connecting your circuit to a power source.

There are three ways you can have a short circuit:

  1. The power source is connected directly to itself (Figure 1).
  2. The wiring path does not have a load, like a light bulb or motor (Figure 2).

Snap Circuits Sensors

Photoresistor- The photoresistor is a sensor that responds to light. As the name suggests, it is a resistor that is sensitive to light. In the dark, it has enough resistance to block all flow of electricity. When a bright light shines on it, its resistance drops, and electrical current can flow through the circuit.

Whistle Chip- The whistle chip is a sensor that responds to sound. It has two thin metal plates inside. In its normal state, the two plates do not touch, so the circuit is “open” and electricity cannot flow. The movement of air from loud noise, like a clap, pushes the plates together, briefly “closing” the circuit and allowing electricity to flow across it.

The whistle chip can also be used to make a sound. If the electrical flow across the whistle chip is turned on and off quickly, the metal plates will vibrate and make a whistling noise.

Snap Circuits Integrated Circuits (IC's)

There are a few different Integrated Circuits (IC's) in the Snap Circuits kit: The most commonly used ones are the Music Integrated Circuit (IC), the Alarm IC, and the Space War IC.  All of the IC’s use sound-generation integrated circuits.
