National Parks

In America, there is a National Park System. The National Park System (NPS) includes 423 sites that people want to protect, care for, and preserve for future generations. The National Park Service is a federal government agency, which is a group run by the government, that is responsible for taking care of those 423 sites. The sites include memorials, monuments, railroads, scenic trails, and parks. 

Out of the 423 sites, 63 are national parks. National parks are large areas of protected natural land and places where wildlife is also protected. National parks are open to the public for camping, exploring, kayaking, and enjoying the beauty of nature.

Earth Features

Earth features are the landforms and waterways that occur naturally on Earth. Landforms include valleys, volcanos, cliffs and mesas. The four major landforms on Earth are hills, plateaus, plains, and mountains. Landforms are a result of tectonic plate movement under the Earth and of erosion over many years.

Waterways are the different types of water bodies on Earth and include waterfalls, oceans, lakes, and rivers. Geologists can learn about the history of the Earth by studying the patterns of its features. Can you think of other Earth features?

Cars and Gas - Costs

Cars need energy to run. Cars run on different types of energy like gasoline, hydrogen, or electricity. Although electric cars are becoming more common, most cars in America have an engine that needs gasoline.

Gasoline is a type of fuel that supplies the car with the energy that it needs to run. If the car runs out of gasoline, it will stop and no longer move until it is refueled with energy. If the car uses gas for energy, it has a gas tank that holds the gas. The amount of gas that a tank can hold is called the fuel capacity. If a car has a 26-gallon fuel capacity, the most gas that it can hold at one time is 26 gallons.

When a car runs on gas, it is refueled at a gas station. Gas stations charge by the gallon for gas. A sign will give the cost for each gallon and is written as $3.00 per gallon. Per means each. If one gallon is purchased, the cost is $3.00. If two gallons are purchased, the cost is $6.00. If 3 gallons are purchased, the cost is $9.00 because 3 (gallons) multiplied by $3 is $9. Cars and Gas - Taking a Trip

Another important thing to know is the driving range. The driving range is an estimate of how many miles a car can run before it is out of gas. A 400-mile driving range means that a car can drive for about 400 miles before it is out of fuel. Think about the following scenario.

A family is driving on a trip that is 900 miles long. Their car has a 400-mile driving range. How many times will the family have to refill the car in order to have enough gas to drive 900 miles? The family will have to refill the car three times in order to have enough gas to drive 900 miles.  A number line can help to determine the answer.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Urban Planner

Urban planners help to create plans for the layout, design, and operation of cities. Part of their job involves creating and interpreting maps of neighborhoods, parks, and streets. Geographic Information System (GIS) is a computer used for creating maps from natural and manmade landforms data. An urban planner may use GIS for map creation and data analysis.


Geologist

In this challenge, you will observe features of the Earth. Geologists are scientists who look for patterns in Earth’s landforms and waterways. Through discovery of the patterns, they help us understand Earth’s past. Their research helps to predict future events such as earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
