Do It! Tinkercad: Create a Stamp

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to design a Stamp in Tinkercad using 2D and 3D images and drawings.

 Project Steps

  1. Copyright and Fair Use

  2. Searching, Downloading, and Saving Your Image

  3. Converting a 2D Image File
  4. Turning your Image Into a Stamp Using Tinkercad

  5. 3D Print Your Stamp

Copyright and Fair Use

For this challenge, you will need to search and download a 2D cartoon image online. There are many images online, just do a search using your favorite search engine. However, before you download and use the image you need to make sure that you legally can – so you have to check its Copyright & Fair Use policy. Some creators will let you use their images, some will let you use them in certain ways, and some will not let you use them at all.

To learn more about Copyright & Fair Use, watch the video Copyright and Fair Use from Common Sense Media.

Sometimes creators will place Creative Commons icons on their work so you know how you can use their images legally and not violate Copyright/Fair Use policies.

To learn more about Creative Commons, watch the Creative Commons video



Searching, Downloading, and Saving Your Image

  1. Use your favorite search engine to find a cartoon image.
  2. Download the cartoon image.
  3. Save the image in a folder on your computer or in a Cloud folder you usually save your work in.
  4. Give it a name that is easy to find.

Make sure you check the Copyright/Fair Use policy!

 

Here is a good site to find cartoon images. It's Copyright/Fair Use license says – “License: Not for commercial use!!!” Which means you can use the image but you can’t sell it. Write down where you got your image. Whenever you use anyone’s image, you should always give them credit. Just like if someone used something you created, you would want them to give you credit. You will upload the image information in your project submission. Make sure to capture:

  • Image name
  • Source (website you found it)
  • Date (you found it)

To help you get started with downloading and saving an image from the Internet, watch the video Tinkercad: Making a Stamp from a Sketch

Converting a 2D Image File

After you download your image you will need to convert it to a format Tinkercad can read. Tinkercad can read files with extensions: .stl, .obj, and .svg.

For this challenge, you will convert your cartoon image to a .svg file.

Watch the Tinkercad: Making a Stamp from a Sketch video to find out how to use Online – Convert to convert your image to .svg. You will use an Image Converter on Online-Convert.

Turning your Image Into a Stamp Using Tinkercad

Once you have converted and saved your cartoon image in your folder, watch the video Tinkercad: Making a Stamp from a Sketch to learn how to import it into Tinkercad and turn it into a 3D printable stamp. You will want to shrink your object down so it is not so big. Tinkercad measures in millimeters.

When you are done designing your stamp, take a screenshot for your project submission then, click on Export and save it as a .stl file to your folder.

3D Print Your Stamp or Share Your Stamp

Now it's time to 3D print your stamp. If you have a 3D printer in the SmartLab and are not sure how to operate it, ask the facilitator for help.

If you are not using the 3D printer in the SmartLab, keep in mind that all 3D printers are different so you may have to ask someone for assistance.

After printing your stamp, test it. Press it on an ink pad and then on a piece of paper. Did it leave an ink print?

Don’t have access to a 3D printer? You can still design your stamp in Tinkercad and make it public so others can see it, download it and print it. To make your stamp public in the Tinkercad gallery you will need to be in My recent designs site of Tinkercad. When you Sign in to Tinkercad it will automatically take you to this site.

  1. Notice that it shows your object as Private. You want to change Private to Public. To change this option, click on Private.
  2. On the next screen, click on the settings icon.
  3. On the next screen, click on Private and change to Public. Then click Save Changes.
  4. To get back to My recent designs from your design screen, click on the Tinkercad logo. Note: Click on the name to change it.
  5. Your 3D stamp is now in the Tinkercad Galley and visible to everyone!
