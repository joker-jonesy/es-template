Do It! Lux Blox: Marble Race

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build a track with a stable base that allows a marble or small ball to travel from the top of the run to the bottom in the most creative way, while remaining on the track. Keep in mind there is no right way to design the marble run.

Use a stopwatch to measure how long it takes the marble to reach the bottom of each design. Then make a scaled bar graph and picture graph to show which design allowed the marble to move the fastest.

 Getting Started

Design Your Marble Run

Plan your marble run on paper before you begin. You can (and will) modify your design as you build it, but it will be much easier if you start out with a general idea of what your marble run will look like and how it will work.

Design Tips

  • What is your challenge? What is your task? 
  • Break your overall design idea into a few basic sections and organize them by the order that you will build each section (sketches on paper might help).
  • For each section, plan out how each part will function. It might take several sketch attempts to plan out a design that seems to work.
  • Build each section individually to make sure it works on its own. Then begin bringing them all together.
  • Test, test, and test again! You will likely have to make continual small modifications until you can get it just right. If it still will not work, do not be afraid to completely change a section. You can design a whole new marble run if absolutely necessary (and if time allows), but make a reasonable attempt at modifying your first chosen design.

Keep track of both your design process and who worked on which parts. Be sure to note what worked well and what did not go as planned in My Portfolio.

 Project Steps

  1. Design a Tower

  2. Design a Base

  3. Connect the Bridge

  4. Design a Basket

  5. Test It Out

  6. Share Your Data

Design a Tower

  • Design two towers by creating a sheet of connected Lux Blox.
  • Fold the Lux Blox. Be sure to leave one side of the tower open.
  • The tower should be able to stand upright when connected to its base.
  • You will need at least two towers in order to connect the bridges of the marble run. There should be a slight difference in the height of the towers to allow for a bridge to be placed between them.

                       

Design a Base

  • Add bases to the towers in order to support the bridge that the marble will travel along.
  • The bases should be stable so that your tower does not fall over when connected to the base.
  • Connect the bases of your towers using a #7 axle.

Note: There are many options for designing the bases of your marble run. Try different variations until the most stable base is designed.

Connect the Bridge

Similar to the towers you built, the bridges will also be constructed from folded sheets of Lux Blox. Keep in mind that one side will be open to allow the marble to be transported throughout the run. Connect the bridges to the two towers on a slight incline. Take a look at the sample towers and decide which blocks and axles will allow the most efficient connection.

Design a Basket

Design a basket to hold and catch the marbles as they exit the run. The basket will require the use of red or white squares and trigons. It will be helpful if the basket is the same on both sides or symmetrical and has raised sides.

Test It Out

  1. Use a stopwatch or timer to measure the time it takes for the marble to reach the end of the marble run. Record the time in the row for Design A under Trial 1. Repeat this to have run times for 5 trials.
  2. Did you get the same time for each trial? How much difference was there between times? If one or more of the times are not close to the others, do more trials.
  3. Change the length of your ramp and the distance between the bases. Record times for this on the row for Design B. Repeat this to record run times for 5 trials.
  4. Were the Design B times longer or shorter than those for Design A?
  5. What change makes the marble go faster? What change makes the marble slow down?
  6. Try at least two more designs. For each design, record your thoughts, a sketch of the design, and observations in My Portfolio. Record all times in the data table.

Share Your Data

Make a scaled picture graph to show how the time for the marble changed with each design.

  1. Decide how you will scale the graph. What image will you use to represent the time? Each image could represent 5 seconds, 10 seconds, or a length of time that makes the most sense to you.  Be creative and choose your own image and scale.
  2. Examine your data. Is there a time for Design A that you got more often? Is there a time that seems to be in the middle?  Use one of these as the time for Design A in the graph.
  3. Repeat this for Designs B, C, and D using the image that you chose.
  4. Check that you have all parts of the graph.
    1. Title
    2. What was compared
    3. Unit of the data
  5. Now use the same data and steps to make a scaled bar graph. Begin by deciding how many seconds each bar represents.
  6. Answer these questions to submit with your project.
    1. Which design was the fastest? What was the time?
    2. Which design was the slowest? What was the time?
    3. How much faster was the fastest time compared to the slowest time?
    4. Look at the two slowest times. How many seconds longer was the slowest?
