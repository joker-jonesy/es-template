Numerical Expressions

Numerical expressions involve numbers and one or more operation signs, such as +, -, ×,  or ÷. Expressions can also use grouping symbols, such as parentheses and brackets. This helps to show the order in which you need to evaluate the expression. Expressions in parentheses are evaluated first. If there is a set of parentheses inside another set of parentheses, the inner set of parentheses is evaluated first.

The table shows how to evaluate the numerical expression [7×(3+4)]+1.

Given. [7×(3+4)]+1
Evaluate the expression in the inner parentheses, (3+4). [7×7]+1
Evaluate the expression in brackets, [7×7]. 49+1
Add 49 and 1. 50

Writing Numerical Expressions to Represent Information

Now that you know how to solve for numerical expressions, let’s practice writing them. You can use numerical expressions to share information about real life situations.

For example, let’s say you are writing a numerical expression to show how much treasure is hidden on a deserted island. There are gold coins, silver goblets, and jeweled necklaces hidden in the lagoon, on the beach, and in a cave.

  • 2 gold coins, 3 groups of 2 silver goblets, and 5 jeweled necklaces are in the lagoon.
  • 4 pouches filled with 3 gold coins each, 1 silver goblet, and 3 jeweled necklaces are on the beach.
  • 5 gold coins, 10 silver goblets, and 4 jeweled necklaces are in a cave.

Here are a couple of ways you could represent this information in numerical expressions:

  • If we want to show how much treasure is in each location, we could use the following numerical expression: [2+(3×2)+5]+[(4×3)+1+3]+(5+10+4) Each pair of parentheses represents the amount of treasure found in one of the three locations.
  • If we want to show the total amount of each type of treasure, we could use this numerical expression: [2+(4×3)+5]+[(3×2)+1+10]+(5+3+4)

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Numerical Expressions in Careers

Many jobs use numerical expressions. Accountants and financial planners use expressions and equations as they balance accounts, pay bills, and plan for the future. Financial advisors help individuals make smart money decisions about insurance, retirement, saving plans, etc., for their financial goals. These advisors compare plans with a variety of expressions so that the individual can compare costs, savings, and benefits. Data analysts also look at data and numbers. They compare information by using numerical expressions.
