What You Should Know – Geometric Shapes: Exploring 2D Shapes and Area

Important Vocabulary

  • Length - how long something is
  • Width - how wide something is
  • Area - the amount of space taken up by a 2D shape measured in square units
  • Two-dimensional (2D) Shape - a shape with two dimensions: length and width
  • Unit - the basic, single amount of something
  • Measurement - a number that shows the size or amount of something

What Is a 2D Shape?

A shape that is 2D has two dimensions: length and width.

Any shape that can be laid flat on a piece of paper is a 2D, or two-dimensional, shape. 

Examples of 2D shapes include rectangles, squares, circles, and triangles.

What Is a Polygon?

A polygon is a 2D shape.

Polygons are made of straight lines that all connect. They are closed shapes, meaning they have no openings on their edges.

Did you know? Circles are not polygons. They do not have straight sides or angles.

How To Measure Shapes

One way to describe a shape is to measure the length of its parts.

A point has no size. You cannot measure a single point because it is an exact place. To measure the length of something, there has to be a distance separating two different points. For example, we can measure the length of a line, the two sides of a 2D square, and the height of the tallest point of a pyramid.

Units of Measurement

Whenever we make a measurement of something, it is important to include the measurement's units.

A unit is the basic amount that your measurement is based on. Length can be measured in customary units, which is the measurement system used in the United States. These are inches, feet, yards, and miles. For example, if you measure the length of a pencil using inches, you would say that your pencil is "6 inches" long. You could write this as 6 inches, 6 in, or 6".

Another way to measure length is using metric units, which is the measurement system used around the world. These include centimeters, meters, and kilometers. You could have measured the same pencil using centimeters (cm) instead of inches. Six inches is a little more than 15 centimeters.

If you simply said your pencil's length was "6", someone else looking at your measurement would be confused.

Did you mean 6 inches? 6 feet? 6 miles?

The units that you use to measure should make sense for the size of the object. For example:

  • The cup of water can be measured using inches or centimeters.
  • The person can be measured using feet or inches, or meters or centimeters.
  • The mountain can be measured using feet, yards, or miles; or meters or kilometers.

You could measure a cup in miles and a mountain in inches but it’s not the best unit of measurement for each of those objects.

Finding Area

You may work at a desk that is in the shape of a rectangle. Maybe you have a table or countertop that is in the shape of a rectangle. You can measure to find out the length and width of the desk, table, or countertop. Then, you can use these measurements to find the area of the surface. Look at this example: This rectangle is 7 feet long and 3 feet wide. You know this because each square is 1 foot long and 1 foot wide. There are 7 squares across and 3 squares up.

Note: Each square is 1 foot long and 1 foot wide.

To find the area, multiply the two lengths: 7 3 = 21. The area of the rectangle is 21 square feet. You can check your work by counting all of the squares. There are three rows of seven, or twenty-one squares.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Area in Careers

Construction workers use area in their work. Construction workers that install drywall or floors, or paint walls, have to use multiplication to figure out how much material to buy. For example, if one gallon of paint covers about 400 square feet, and the walls of a house that need to be painted add up to about 2,000 square feet, the painters know they need about 5 gallons of paint to complete the job.

Not all areas take up large spaces. You may be surprised to learn that dentists also use area measurements! Dentists must know the area of teeth in order to repair them. They use area to learn the biting surface of teeth. This is important when they insert dental implants.
