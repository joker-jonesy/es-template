Do It!

Your challenge is to learn how to control Sphero and have it do a specific trick. Then, write an algorithms  based on the trick you created so that you can make Sphero repeat its actions.

Getting Started

You will use the Sphero and Sphero Edu apps to control your robot. If you do not already have one, create an account on Sphero Edu.

Use the Sphero Quick Start Guide to set up and connect your Sphero to your phone or tablet. Take notes about what you learn in each step of the process. Take screenshots of your designs, and videos of Sphero in action.

Design specifications:

  • Sphero should change color at least once.
  • Sphero should change speed at least twice.
  • Sphero should change direction at least four times.
  • Optional: Set up Sphero ramps for Sphero to drive over.

Practice with Sphero

Practice with Sphero App

  1. Open the Sphero app. Take 10 minutes to practice using the controls circled on the right.
  2. When you finish, write down what each control does in your ePortfolio.
  3. Once you are comfortable with controlling Sphero, take 10 minutes to practice drawing paths for Sphero to follow. 

Practice with Sphero Edu App

  1. Open the Sphero Edu app and tap the Programs tab at the bottom.
  2. Go to My Programs and tap the "+" button. 
  3. Type in "Test Drawing" as your program name and select the Draw option. 
  4. Click Create and start drawing. Try changing colors and speeds.
  5. Hit Start to send your instructions to Sphero.

   

Create Your Algorithm 

Use Sphero Edu to design a path that meets the design specifications listed under Getting Started.

  1. Test out your path with Sphero.
  2. Write an algorithm for your design on paper.
  3. Start a new drawing program and create your design by following only the written algorithm (do not look at previous drawings or photos).
  4. Test your design with Sphero: Did it repeat the same color, speed, and direction changes? If not, which part of your previous instructions did you leave out?
  5. Make changes to your current drawing and algorithm until your Sphero is able to do the same actions as in your first program.

Reflect
