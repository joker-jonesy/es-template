Graphic Design with Vectr: Basic Photo Editing

It is important to have photos in your ePortfolio. You can use the website Vectr.com to crop and edit your photos.  

What You'll Learn

  • How to use photo editing tools to document your work. 

Stuff You'll Need

  • Vectr.com website
  • Pictures (from your camera or free use images from the Internet)

What Can Vectr Do?

With Vectr you can:

  • Crop
  • Rotate
  • Change how the picture looks (like its colors and brightness)
  • Type Text
  • Create Shapes

Vectr can also be used to create endless graphics for websites, logos, presentations, posters, etc. Select the project type by changing the page settings. The page icon is at the top left of your screen. 

You can find basic instructions to use Vectr below. For the complete official instructions for Vectr, please refer to the user guide.  

Basic Photo Edit

Open an Image for Edit

You may want to take your own photos or you may use online images for your project or ePortfolio. There are two ways that photos can be added to the Vectr workspace. 

TO OPEN:

  1. Open a new file within Vectr. 

  1. After opening a new file, look at the toolbar to the left of the workspace and locate the insert tool.   

  1. Click the insert tool. Select an image file from your computer and it will be added to your workspace.     

Images can also be added to the workspace by dragging an image from your computer and dragging it into the workspace.

Edit an Image

To EDIT: Double click the image to select and edit the background image.

  • To Crop, double click the path/image you would like to edit. After double clicking, the flip buttons and box/edit points surrounding the image will appear. This will allow you to crop the image without actually having to cut it. Move the image in the direction that you would like to cut it. You can also crop the image, by altering the container path. This allows the image to edited into different shapes without cutting the image. Simply drag the edit points in the direction and shape that you would like image to resemble.

  

  • To Flip the image horizontally or vertically, hover over the buttons that appear above the image once the image is clicked. 
  • To Resize, click and drag the control points on your image. Hold the point as you resize the image.
  • To Rotate, click the image. Once the control points appear, hold the mouse just outside of the control points until the rotate handle appears. 
  • To undo any changes, select Ctrl + Z on your keyboard. You can also delete the path the paths and start over. 
  • When you are Done with your changes, click the export icon. You can save your design by either right-clicking the preview and selecting “save as” or by dragging the preview onto your computer desktop.

NOTE: If you want to type text on your image after editing, save first. Then, go back to the workspace and select the text icon. After selecting the text icon, click on the image so the text box appears. Add text, and edit as necessary.

  

Using Filters

To change how the image looks, use the filters located in the editing panel on the right side of the workspace. Filters include backgrounds, borders, shadows, inset shadows, and opacity. Filters can be added, disabled, deleted, and sorted.

TO APPLY A BACKGROUND FILTER:

  • Click the drop down menu under backgrounds. Choose Background.
  • Color Backgrounds: sets a solid color background with the option to change colors. Use the colorpicker to select the color of your choice. 
  • Image Backgrounds: sets an image background
  • Linear Gradient Backgrounds: sets a background of a range of colors instead of just one solid color. Each gradient background has two color points known as color stops. 
  • Radial Gradient Backgrounds: sets a background that starts from a single central point and radiates outward.

To learn more about applying and editing background filters, visit backgrounds.

TO APPLY A BORDER:

  • Click the drop-down menu under border. Choose border alignment. 
    • Outside aligns the border outside the image's boundary.
    • Inside aligns the border inside the image's boundary.
    • Center aligns the border to the center of the image's boundary.
  • Select a color for the border.
  • Adjust the size of the border.

Border can be added to the text. The border will display around each letter, and not the boundary of a word.  

To learn more about applying borders, visit borders.

TO APPLY A SHADOW FILTER:

  • Select a shadow color from the dropdown menu and colorpicker.
  • Select the offset for both the x and y axis.
  • If desired, add a blur to the youur shadow. The higher the number, the blurrier the shadow.
  • Adjust the size of the shadow spread by changing the shadow's spread number.

To learn more about the types of filters you can use and adjust, visit the Vectr user guide.
