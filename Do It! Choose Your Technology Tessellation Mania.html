Do It! Choose Your Technology: Tessellations and Mathematical Challenges

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to make tessellations with K’NEX, Pixie software, or another building technology of your choice. You will create your tessellations to meet four mathematical challenges. What amazing tessellations can you create?

 Project Steps

  1. Construct Tessellation: Repeated Polygons

  2. Construct Tessellation: Your Own Pattern

  3. Construct Tessellation: The Fourth Shape

  4. Construct Tessellation: Yellow and Green Shapes

Choose your technology and start your project. During these activities, you will use math to create your own works of art with tessellations.

Construct Tessellation: Repeated Polygons

Choose 2 different polygons to construct your tessellation.

  • Use your 2 polygons to create a repeating pattern with 12 total shapes, so that your 12th shape completes the pattern you chose.
  • What is the fraction of your first polygon to the number of total shapes in your tessellation?
  • What is the fraction of your second polygon to the number of total shapes in your tessellation?
  • How many times does your pattern repeat or start over? (4 times? 2 times?)
  • Expand your tessellation by 1/4. How many polygons did you have to add?
  • How did you calculate the number of polygons to add to your tessellation? Show your work.
  • Take a screenshot or picture of your tessellation.

Construct Tessellation: Your Own Pattern

Create a tessellation with a pattern that repeats 6 times.

  • Expand your tessellation by multiplying 6 × 2 1/3.
  • How many more repetitions or repeating patterns will you have to add to your tessellation?
  • How did you calculate the number of repetitions or repeating patterns added to your tessellation? Show your work.
  • How many total repeating patterns do you have?
  • Take a screenshot or picture of your tessellation.

Construct Tessellation: The Fourth Shape

Create a tessellation that demonstrates the following word problem:

Skye was given 24 total shapes. She wants every fourth shape to be green. How many shapes will be green?

  • How can you use fractions to represent this problem?
  • How did you calculate the number of green shapes in Skye’s tessellation? Show your work.
  • Take a screenshot or picture of your tessellation.

Construct Tessellation: Yellow and Green Shapes

Create a tessellation that demonstrates the following word problem:

Gio wants to create a tessellation with 30 total shapes. He wants 2/5 of the shapes to be yellow and 1/3 of the shapes to be green. The rest should be blue.

  • How many of each color tile are there? How can you use fractions to represent this problem?
  • How did you calculate the number of each color tile in Gio’s tessellation? Show your work.
  • Take a picture or screenshot of your final tessellation.
