Demonstrate Conservation of Angular Momentum

Have you ever wondered what makes a spinning top stay upright?  Build this Zometool model and experience the conservation of angular momentum yourself. Review What Is Conservation of Angular Momentum in the What You Should Know section to learn about this concept.

Build Your Model

   

Feel the Conservation of Angular Momentum

  • Hold the wheel in front of you with a hand on each end of the red axle. An axle is a rod at the center of a wheel around which the wheel can spin. An axle is a part of many types of spinning objects, like the handle of a spinning top.
  • Lower your right hand while raising your left hand to tilt the wheel. How does that feel? 
  • Pay attention to how easy it is to tilt the wheel.
  • Get the wheel spinning by twisting the red axle with your fingers.
  • Hold the axle loosely enough so it continues to spin. If you have trouble doing this, try slipping drinking straws over the ends of the axle.
    • How does it feel?
    • Are you able to tilt the wheel smoothly?
    • Is there any resistance to the movement?
    • What you feel is the conservation of angular momentum.

                    

The Spinning Top Challenge

Conservation of angular momentum explains why a spinning top stays upright for so long. See the What You Should Know section to review angular momentum. Friction is what causes the spinning top to slow down and fall over.  

Your challenge is to use these concepts to design a top that spins for 15 seconds or longer.

  1. Use your Zometool kit to build a simple top that you think will spin well.
  2. Spin your top. Use a stopwatch to time how long it spins before it falls over. 
  3. Spin it a few more times and record the times. Figure out its longest spin.
  4. Now change something in the top design to make it spin longer. (Example: height, width, and weight). Spin your top and time it again. 
  5. Continue making changes to your best design until you can no longer find ways to improve its spin time.

Take pictures of your different tops and label them with their longest spin times. 

How might the surface your top spins on affect its spin time?
