Metric System

The Metric System is a system of measurement that is based in units of ten. It uses grams (mass), meters (length) and liter (volume) as its base units. The metric system is the most commonly used measurement system in the world and is sometimes also referred to as the International System of Units (SI). The metric system uses “pre-fixes”, or a word placed before another word, to go from smaller units of measurement to larger units of measurement. Milligrams, grams and kilograms are used to measure mass or weight.

Metric System Conversions

Since the metric system is based in units of ten, you can easily convert between the different metric measurements by multiplying or dividing by multiples of 10. The “pre-fixes” of the words let you know how many of that measurement are in your base unit. For example, with mass your base unit is grams. In “milligram” the pre-fix is “milli”. Milli means “one thousandth part of” which lets us know that there are 1000 milligrams in 1 gram. If you were converting from grams to milligrams then you would divide by 1000. If you were converting from milligrams to grams then you would multiply by 1000.

Example 1: Convert 3 grams (g) to milligrams (mg)

1 g = 1000 mg

3 g × 1000 = 3000 mg

Example 2: Convert 400 milligrams (mg) to grams (g)

1 g = 1000 mg

400 mg ÷ 1000 = 0.4 g

Another example would be “kilogram”. The pre-fix “kilo” means “1000 of” which means there are 1000 grams in 1 kilogram. To convert from kilograms to grams, you would multiply by 1000. To convert from grams to kilograms, you would divide by 1000. 

Example 1: Convert 0.4 kilograms (kg) to grams (g)

1000 g = 1 kg

0.6 kg × 1000 = 600 g

Example 2: Convert 20 grams (g) to kilograms (kg)

1000 g = 1 kg

20 g ÷ 1000 = 0.020 kg

What is ppm?

PPM is an abbreivation that stands for parts-per-million and can also be represented as milligrams per liter. It is used to describe the concentration of different substances in air, water, or soil. When measuring the substances in air, it lets you know the number molecules of that substance per million molecules of dry air. For example, if you had a CO2  concentration of 100 ppm then you would have 100 molecules of CO2 for every million molecules of dry air. Due to the fact that it can also be written as milligrams per liter then  this could also be written as 100 mg/L CO2.

Line Graphs

A line graph is a type of graph that uses data points and a line that connects them. This graph is typically used to track changes over time. You can decide how you would like to display time. If you are showing changes over a long period of time you may want to use decades, periods of ten years, and the average change. If you are showing changes over a shorter period then you may want to go in periods of 1 year, 2 years or 5 years. It just depends on the data set.

Line graphs have two axes: y-axis (vertical) and the x-axis (horizontal). Time data is usually on the x-axis because it is the independent axis (the data does not depend on other factors). The data that is being measured is typically put on the y-axis because it is the dependent axis (the data depends on other factors). Both of the axes should be labeled to make them easier to read and understand. Your line graph should also contain a descriptive title so that readers know what information they are looking at.

When making line graphs scientists usually make a data table first. The data table should include the information that you are going to put on your graph. This data table is then used to quickly and efficiently plot your information. 

Greenhouse Gasses

Earth’s atmosphere contains gases, called greenhouse gases, that help keep earth warm by trapping the energy from the sun. This is called the greenhouse effect because like a greenhouse the light from the sun comes through and the heat gets trapped inside. These gases are very important because without them the heat would escape and earth would be too cold to support life. However, when the amount of these gases increases it leads to more heat being trapped and warmer temperatures.  The amount of these gases has increased a lot over the past few hundred years due to certain human activities such as driving cars and heating and powering homes and buildings. The energy needed for these activities is created through the burning of fossil fuels. Oil, gas, and coal are fossil fuels. When these fuels are burned to create energy they release chemicals and greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide (CO2 ), into the air.

Global Warming

Global warming is how the earth has gotten hotter over the past few hundred years. Global warming can be dangerous because it can cause glaciers to melt and sea levels to rise. The effect that global warming has on weather is called climate change. 

Climate Change

Climate change is the study of how the average weather in an area has changed over time. Climate change occurs naturally on Earth. Climate can change depending on how much energy the sun is giving off, changes in the ocean, natural disasters like volcanoes, asteroids hitting Earth, etc. However, over the past few decades our world’s climate has changed much faster due to global warming. When the climate changes too quickly it can be dangerous because it does not allow time for living organisms and ecosystems on Earth to adapt. Climate not only has changed with hotter temperatures but also in unexpected ways too such as intense and major storms. To slow down global warming, we need to stop burning so much fossil fuel. Instead of using fossil fuels, we can make electricity from renewable resources such as sunlight and wind. Making electricity from sunlight or wind does NOT cause air pollution.

Nonfiction Picture Books

A picture book is a book in which the text and the illustrations work together to help tell a story or present information. A nonfiction book is a book that is written about real events and factual information. A nonfiction picture book is a really great way to teach readers new information that may be hard to understand at first. Most nonfiction books contain specific features:

Glossary A section at the end of a nonfiction book that defines new or difficult words in alphabetical order
Table of Contents A section at the beginning of a nonfiction book that lets the reader the different sections (chapters) in the book and what page they can be found on.
Index Found at the end of the book. It lists important topics found in the book and what page to find them on.
Captions A sentence that explains what is happening in the picture or diagram. 
Headings A word or phrase that helps organize information in a book. It explains the main idea of the text section that follows it. Headings are usually found at the top of the page.
Bold Words Bold words are important words that the author wants the reader to notice. Words are typically bolded to show the reader this is an important or new information. Sometimes bold words are difficult words and the reader can find the definition of them in the glossary.
Labeled Diagrams Diagrams are used to visually show information to the reader so they can better understand. They are labeled so that the reader knows what information that they are looking at.

Writing Nonfiction Picture Books

When writing a book authors usually follow a series of steps to organize their writing and make it easier.

Select each item to learn more.

Outline

The first step is to write an outline. An outline can be thought of as a “map” that the author uses to know where they want to go next. Outlines typically contain the information that authors want to include and their headings, important vocabulary words, diagrams or picture ideas, etc.

Rough Draft

Once the outline is finished, authors then tend to go back and write the rough draft of their book. The rough draft is the very first version of a writing. Authors use their outline to help guide them as they add information.

Edit/Feedback

After authors write their rough draft they usually self-edit by reading through it and then have a friend read it too for feedback. This allows them to find grammar and information mistakes and correct them. Editing also ensures that authors did not leave any information out.

Final Draft

After authors have edited their rough draft, they will write their final draft. The final draft is their final version of their book. The final draft includes all of the edits that they made when they read through their rough draft. The final draft will also include all diagrams, pictures, and text features.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Author

An author is a professional writer. Authors do all types of writing including: children’s books, scientific articles, blog posts, etc. There are fiction writers who make up their stories using their imagination and nonfiction writers who write about actual events and facts.

Climatologists (Climate Change Analysts)

Climatologists are scientists that study the long term weather of a specific area (climate). They focus on the weather to study, observe, and try to understand climate change. They use tools to gather atmospheric data to understand and explain weather so they can predict what to expect in the future.

Environmental Lawyer

A lawyer is a professional that advises people on legal matters. Lawyers can represent businesses, government agencies and individuals. An environmental lawyer focuses on areas that relate to the health of the environment such as: climate change law, clean technology law, water law, etc. They may also support efforts for new policies that are related to global warming, clean air, land use, etc.

GeoScientists

A geoscientist is a scientist that focuses on the solid, liquid, and gas composition of earth and its history to learn how it has changed throughout time. Geoscientists study the earth’s processes, its history, and how it is structured. These scientists are important in helping us learn and understand important events in earth’s history so that we can try to predict how earth will continue to evolve and change as time goes on. This knowledge allows humans to try to moderate their activities to protect the earth.
