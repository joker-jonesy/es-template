What You Should Know - Google Maps: Maps and Coordinates

Important Vocabulary

  • Ordered Pair - a set of two numbers that identify the horizontal (the first number) and vertical (the second number) positions of a point on the coordinate plane
  • Coordinate Plane - a plane created when a horizontal (x-axis) and a vertical (y-axis) line intersect at a point (origin)
  • x-axis - horizontal (right to left) number line of a coordinate plane
  • y-axis - vertical (up and down) number line of a coordinate plane
  • Origin - the point at which the horizontal and vertical lines of a coordinate plane meet; (0,0)

What Is a Coordinate Plane?

Coordinate planes allow you to create a two-dimensional grid to plot points, graph lines, determine distance between two objects or locations, and more. The origin (0,0) is the starting point;  it helps you figure out where you are and how to move on the coordinate plane. When one is plotting or finding a point on the coordinate plane, the first number is on the horizontal line, or x-axis, whereas the second number in the set is on the vertical line, or y-axis. The points are represented using an ordered pair, often known as a coordinate (x, y).

What Is a Map?

People have been making maps for thousands of years. Most of us use maps to show the location of a place and plan or follow a route between places. But what are maps, really?

Maps are a type of model—a representation of an original structure, but on a smaller scale. They use sets of points, lines, and areas to represent a place, a pattern, or a relationship.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Maps and coordinate planes are used frequently by architects, land surveyors, and more.

Architects use coordinate planes to create a blueprint for designs of their buildings or models.

Land Surveyors use coordinate planes overlaid on maps to document precise measurements of property lines.
