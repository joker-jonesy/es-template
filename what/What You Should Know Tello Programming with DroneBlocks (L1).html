What You Should Know - Tello Drone: Programming with DroneBlocks

Important Vocabulary

  • Conditionals - are basic “if, then” logic statements that determine how a computer executes or carries out a program
  • Loop - Loops allow you to repeat a sequence of instructions several times or until a condition is met
  • Coordinate Plane - a plane constructed when a vertical (y-axis) number line and a horizontal (x-axis) number line intersect at a point (origin)
  • Quadrants - the four areas formed when a coordinate plane is created
  • Ordered Pair - a set of two numbers that represent the horizontal (the first number) and vertical (the second number) placement of a point on the coordinate plane
  • Origin - the point at which the x- and y-axis intersect (0,0)
  • X-axis - horizontal number line found on a coordinate plane
  • Y-axis - vertical number line found on a coordinate plane
  • Absolute Value - the distance of a number to zero on a coordinate plane; always represented as a positive number
  • UAV (unmanned aerial vehicle) - an aircraft without any human pilot, crew, or passengers on board (example: drone)

What is Tello and DroneBlocks?

The Tello Drone is a small quadcopter, meaning it has four motors connected to propellers that allow it to fly in any direction. The propellers on quadcopters rotate in two different directions to overcome the tendency to twist in the air. The force created by the propeller blades rotating in opposite directions is canceled out, which keeps the drone steady while in flight.

The propellers spin just like on a helicopter, but they do not tilt to change the direction in which the drone moves. How does it fly in different directions, then?

Drones, like the Tello, use smart flight control computer chips to change the speed of each propeller to change how the drone moves.

How Do You Use Tello/DroneBlocks?

Download and Launch DroneBlocks App

The DroneBlocks app requires the use of a Wifi-enabled smartphone, tablet, Chromebook, laptop, or desktop computer running Android, iOS, or ChromeOS. Note: DroneBlocks is not compatible with FireOS, the operating system for Amazon tablets.

You can download the free DroneBlocks app by clicking on the link associated with your device or by searching for it in the App Store and clicking "Install."

  • iOS App Store
  • Google Play Store
  • Chrome App Store

Connect to Tello with DroneBlocks

Tello can be operated with the DroneBlocks app by launching a pre-programmed mission or  building your mission or program. If you choose to create your own program, you will select one command block at a time, beginning with the takeoff block, until you have a complete program for your drone.

For the lengthier challenges towards the end of this project starter, it is best to build your initial program before powering on Tello and connecting via the WiFi. This is recommended to allow for optimal flight time because each battery allows for approximately 13 minutes of flight time. Programming and troubleshooting your mission code before connecting saves more power for the flight!

Launch Tello Blocks

  1. Open the DroneBlocks app and allow the code blocks to load fully.
  2. Tap the menu (three lines or “hamburger”) icon and select: Tello Blocks.
  3. Drag and drop the blocks to create your mission.

Connect to Tello

  1. Tap “Connect to Tello” at the top right of the app.
    1. A window will pop up with a “Connect” button, but do not tap this button yet!
  2. Power on Tello, which will broadcast a WiFi hotspot.
  3. Navigate from DroneBlocks to:  Settings > Wifi > Tello
    1. The hotspot name will look similar to Tello-XXXXXX.
  4. Close Settings and return to the DroneBlocks app.
  5. Click the blue "Connect" button.
    1. You should now see "Tello" and the "Battery %" listed at the top of your app screen.
  6. When you are ready to start your program, or test individual commands, tap the menu icon. Then, select: "Launch Mission."

Before you begin building your program, practice the sequence of connecting Tello to DroneBlocks. Remember that if connectivity fails, restart Tello, your device, and the DroneBlocks app.

Drone Safety

While the Tello drone has safety features built-in, drone pilots must be careful to avoid damage to the drone or nearby objects and people.

Follow these safety guidelines to reduce the chance of injury or damage while operating the Tello drone.

Always:

  • Check the drone before each flight to make sure that there are no damaged parts. Be sure to check the propellers, propeller guards, and the arms connecting the motors to the drone’s main body.
  • Operate the drone in an open space free from anything that could entangle in the drone's propellers.
  • Keep the propeller guards attached to the drone. They can help protect the propellers and other objects from becoming damaged during operation or storage.
  • Keep the drone within direct eyesight. The camera feed from the drone doesn't show you anything to the sides or back of the drone.
  • Keep fingers and other objects out of the path of the propellers. They spin very fast and can cause injury or damage.

Never:

  • Fly the drone above or at humans or animals.
  • Operate the drone in windy conditions. The drone can easily lose control in the wind and become damaged.
  • Do not attach anything to the drone without your Facilitator's permission. Objects attached to the drone can cause the drone to lose balance or become entangled in the propellers, resulting in damage.

Loops

There might be times when you need to do the same thing repeatedly in your program. Instead of writing the same lines over and over, you can use a loop! Loops allow you to repeat a sequence of instructions many times or until a condition is met.

In DroneBlocks, the loop command is known as the "repeat" block. You can use this block to multiply a series of flips and revisit the box mission to simplify the code. How can you problem-solve and program using fewer commands by using the loop block?

Nested Loops

A combination of loops is called a nested loop. The most common type of nested loops will be one loop inside another. The first loop is called the outer loop, while the second loop is called the inner loop. The outer loop always performs first, and the inner loop performs inside the outer loop each time the outer loop performs once.

Conditionals

Conditionals are basic “if, then” logic statements that determine how a computer executes or carries out a program. Conditionals are a key part of the decision-making process for computers. They tell computers what to do based on whether a condition or statement is true or false.

Example: Conditional statements are part of our everyday lives. As people, we can think about situations and make decisions based on what we observe or know to be true.

For example, “If it is raining out, then we will have recess inside. If it is NOT raining out, then we will have recess outside.” Since computers are machines that can’t do anything without us directing them, we direct the computer using conditional statements. This tells the computer what to do and when to do it.

Think about the route your drone might be taking to deliver the item. When might you have to program your drone using a conditional statement? What obstacles might get in the way of their mission? How can you ensure that your drone will use safety precautions while it is en route?

Graphing on the Coordinate Plane

A coordinate plane is a plane constructed when a vertical (y-axis) number line and a horizontal (x-axis) number line intersect at a point (origin). It is made up of four areas known as quadrants.

An ordered pair is a set of two numbers that represent the horizontal (the first number) and vertical (the second number) placement of a point on the coordinate plane, represented as (x, y), also referred to as coordinates.

  1. Start at the origin (0,0)
  2. Move right or left (along the x-axis)
  3. Then, move up or down (along the y-axis)

NOTE: It must be followed in this order, or the ordered pair will be reversed.

Finding Distance Using Coordinates and Absolute Value

If two ordered pairs have the same x-value, we can count to find the distance between the two points.

For example, the ordered pairs (5,2) and (5, -4) have the same x-value. Using the coordinate plane, start at one of these ordered pairs and count up or down to learn the distance between the two points. The distance between these ordered pairs is six units.

Additionally, the distance can be calculated using absolute value. (5, 2) is |2| meaning it is 2 units above the x-axis. (5, -4) is |-4|, meaning it is four units below the x-axis. Therefore, the distance between the two ordered pairs is |2| + |-4| = 6 units.

The ordered pairs (-2, -4) and (5, -4) have the same y-value. Using the coordinate plane, what is the distance? 7 units.

Using the absolute value method, (-2, -4) is |-2|, meaning it is 2 units to the left of the y-axis. (5, -4) is |-4| meaning it is 5 units to the right of the y-axis. Therefore, the distance between the two ordered pairs is |-2| + |5| = 7.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

While some people buy and fly drones for fun, drones are also used all over to help people.

Medical Deliveries

Drones can be used to transport medicine like vaccines and prescriptions. Medical deliveries have become one of the best uses for drone delivery because they can more easily fly into and out of remote or faraway places than trucks or motorcycles. Maps are utilized to determine locations, distance, and other important details to ensure the accurate delivery of medicines.

Medical drone delivery is credited with saving lives after delivering emergency supplies of donated blood to citizens living in rural areas of Rwanda. Drones were also used to provide relief in many areas that were impacted by hurricanes.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, drones have been making medical deliveries of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and COVID-19 tests all over the United States.

Food Deliveries

Drones have been proposed or suggested as a solution for the quick delivery of prepared foods like pizzas, tacos, and frozen beverages.

In San Francisco, the creators of Tacocopter are using drones to deliver tacos with the help of a smartphone app. This service is currently in beta testing, which means they're testing it with a small group of real customers.

Package Deliveries

Drones have been used to deliver packages since 2016. Big companies like Amazon, Google, and DHL have been using this service to deliver packages all over the United States. The Drone package delivery services are seen as a tool for improving package delivery in hard-to-reach areas. It is important for the companies to use tools such as coordinate planes/maps to plan delivery routes, the distance between locations, estimated delivery times, and more.

Let's take a look at some drone-centered careers.

First Responder

Drones are supporting our community heroes by providing rescuers with live video streams that allow them to evaluate risky situations. First responders use drone operators to help locate people who are missing in an area that is too large to cover by foot. At the same time, firefighters and police officers assign drones to discover people in danger and find the safest path to safety during life-threatening situations. First responders can use a coordinate plane to map out their route at the start of their rescue mission.

Photographer

Photographers can get aerial photographs. These sky-high snapshots are a great way for novice or new photographers to set their portfolios apart from the rest. Whether it’s shooting for weddings, corporate events, or just artistic curiosity, drone photography has become a popular request. Even real estate agencies look to photographers to shoot from above ground and get a 360° view of their properties.

Wildlife Conservationist

The greatest advantage of drones is the ability to travel to places that are too distant or dangerous for a human to visit. Wildlife conservationists use UAVs to monitor and protect fragile species all around the world. Wildlife conservationists can utilize coordinate planes to document these species’ locations in order to more easily find the species each day, week, or month to monitor their activity and safety.

Real Estate Agent

Many real estate listings now feature a high-definition video that takes you soaring over the rooftop of a house down to the backyard, then in through the open back door, and up the stairs to the master bedroom. Drones allow real estate agents to quickly capture much of the visuals they need to show off the features of a property.

Journalist

Traditionally, journalists would have to fly expensive news choppers or use bulky camera equipment to report on stories in hard-to-reach places. But now, drones with high-quality cameras allow reporters to cover news events happening in real-time and in areas previously considered inaccessible.

Over the last few years, drones have become a common tool for journalism, and aerial shots captured by drones have helped us understand how a flood has damaged an area, how large a crowd is during a major event, or even how far a wildfire has spread.
