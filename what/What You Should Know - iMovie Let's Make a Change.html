Do It! - iMovie: Let's Make a Change

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to encourage positive change within your school by editing video clips using iMovie. Conduct a survey to strengthen your argument. You will create a video using different camera angles and use iMovie to add text and sound effects to create an engaging video to motivate others to support your opinion.

 Getting Started

Reminders

  1. Ensure your actor or actors are available.
  2. Check to see when your location will be available to film.
  3. Gather your props and equipment.
  4. To start a new project in iMovie you need to click the + sign to add your videos.

Conduct a Survey

Conduct a survey as evidence to try to support your opinion.

  • You can use a template similar to this one if you would like. Survey Template
  • What do you expect the results of your survey to be?
  • Note: The results may not be what you want but it is good to have the evidence.

Create and Solve Word Problems 

Create and solve word problems involving your survey results and/or opinion to draw conclusions to strengthen your argument.

  • See the What You Should Know section for examples of word problems and how to solve them.
  • You can also use one of these word problem templates to help you.

I conducted a survey of ___________students and teachers. _____students voted in favor of _________. _______students voted against __________.

I need to find out how many total votes I have. I also need to find out if those that voted in favor of _________is more than those that voted against_______.

Find out how much to add to those in favor of to get those who voted against or find out how much to add to those against to get those who voted in favor.

Setup Camera

Setup the camera when filming is ready to begin.

Production

  1. Ensure the camera is steady and focused.
  2. Ensure everything is included in the shot that you want.
  3. Check the angle and lighting.
  4. Check the sound to be sure it is working properly.
  5. Begin filming using the video function on the iPad or within the iMovie app.
  6. Repeat these steps if you change locations or stop recording.

Post-Production

It is time to edit your film. Start by opening the iMovie app and clicking the + sign to add your videos to the iMovie editing app.

Select each item to learn more.

Edit the Transitions

  1. Insert video clips into the timeline.
  2. Click on the transition icon between the clips.
  3. Locate the menu at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Choose any transition you like.
  5. Play the clip to preview the transition.
  6. To change the length of time the transition lasts click on duration then click and adjust to the length you want.

Add Your Desired Text 

Consider these questions when choosing what text to add:

  • What is your overall message?
  • What text can you add to make your message more impactful?
  • How can you convince others to support your opinion?
  • Would adding your word problem make an impact on the film?
  1. Click on the part where you want to add text or a title.
  2. Locate the menu at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Click the “Titles” icon.
  4. Choose the text you want to use.
  5. Click out of the menu bar.
  6. Click on “Title Text Here” on the video itself.
  7. Type what you want to include in text.

Add Your Sound Effects

  1. Insert video clips into the timeline.
  2. Click on the part where you want to add sound effects.
  3. Locate the menu at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Click the + symbol in the top right corner.
  5. Click “Audio.”
  6. Click “Sound Effects.”
  7. Choose the sound you want to use.
  8. Click the + next to the sound to add.
  9. To shorten the sound click on the soundbar after it is added. Move it with your hand to adjust to the length you want.
  10. To remove a sound click on the soundbar. Click on the trashcan icon in the bottom right corner.

Change the Title

Change the title of your movie.

Save Your Video

Be sure to save your video to the iPad, then transfer to your own device.

  1. Click on save video (it will save on the iPad).
  2. Save it to your device.
  • If you have a Mac, airdrop the video to your computer.
  • If you have a PC, connect the cable, locate the iPad in File Explorer, and drag the iMovie where you would like to save it.
