What You Should Know

What Is a Machine?

You might not think about it, but when you use a tool make an activity easier, you are using a machine.

The job of a machine is to make work easier. It does this by changing how force is used to move an object or load. Machines include things you might see or use every day, like a seesaw, hammer, bike, and even your arms.

Work is done when force is applied to move an object or load. For example, you do work when you push your chair under your desk. Force is anything that changes the motion of an object. Kicking a ball and pulling a suitcase are actions that make those objects move, so they are examples of force. Pushing is the force you apply to your chair (load). Load is another word for the object that is moved.

Machines Need Energy

A machine needs a source of energy in order to do work.

With some machines, you may be that source of energy.

Wind, the sun, and batteries are examples of other energy sources.

Energy is the amount of work something could do. An energy source supplies energy to the machine that does the work.

Automatic Machines

Some advanced machines can do their jobs automatically because of the software programs that tell them exactly what to do. Software programs are instructions written in code, which tell a computer or machine how to do its job. They are the like the machine's 'thoughts.'

In fact, many are able to complete several different tasks in order.

Once you adjust the settings and turn them on, automatic machines are able to do their work without your help.

What are Robots?

A robot is a type of machine, but not all machines are robots. In fact, robots can be thought of as a special kind of automatic machine.

All machines, including robots, are made up of mechanical parts. However, only some machines and ALL robots also use electricity to function.

Like automatic machines, robots are also programmed to do their jobs automatically, but they are even more advanced because they have a controller, which is programmed by software, and sensors to adjust to new situations. With more advanced robots, you do not even have to adjust settings for these changes to take place.

Industrial Robots Military Robots

Industrial robots are used to help move or help build things in manufacturing plants. 

The military uses robots with sensors to gather information. They also have Jeeps that drive themselves, and robots that find, diffuse and dispose of bombs. 

Robots are very useful machines. By using sensors to understand the world around them, they can collect helpful information. With their ability to move in very exact ways, they are also able to do jobs that are normally difficult for humans. 

We use robots for all kinds of jobs in many different places. Many industrial robots are used to build things like cars, while other robots are able to find and safely remove bombs for the military. You might have even seen small robots that can vacuum the house.

Robot Checklist

A robot is a machine that has…

Mechanical Parts

Mechanical parts are the physical pieces that make up the machine. Examples: motors, wheels, and gears.

Software Programs

Software programs are instructions written in code, which tell a computer or machine how to do its job. They are the like the machine’s 'thoughts'.

Energy Source

An energy source supplies energy to the machine that does the work. If you use a bat to hit a ball, YOU are the energy source that helps the bat (machine) move the ball (load).

Sensors

A sensor recognizes light, temperature, movement, or another signal. A sensor controls the robot’s reaction.

Controller

A controller is like the 'brain' of the robot. The software program is like the 'thoughts' in the brain that tell the robot what to do.

Get to Know Your Robot: Ozobot

Ozobot moves and changes color. It also responds to codes that control these and other functions.

Its controller has been programmed to read color codes, but you can also write your own programs for Ozobot.

How Ozobot Uses Line and Color

Ozobot has five sensors underneath it, which it uses to sense color. Once you turn on Ozobot, it will begin to search for color. If it senses color, it will begin to move in search of lines (paths) to follow. 

Line-Drawing Practice

If you choose to use Ozobot with markers, keep in mind that it likes to follow thick lines (1/4 inch). Warm up by drawing lines for Ozobot to follow.
