What You Should Know – CMPSR: Create a Sound Story

Important Vocabulary

  • Sound Story - a story (usually a book) that uses sound to enhance the experience for the reader  
  • Sound Effects - sounds that are added to movies, films or other media to enhance the experience for the reader  
  • Onomatopoeia – words that name a sound while also sounding like the sound. Examples: pop, squeak, bang, boom  
  • Notes, Scales, and Keys – notes are different sounds in music that are grouped together to make scales while keys are specific scales like C sharp or E major

Connecting GarageBand to CMPSR

Before you can play your sound effects, you need to connect GarageBand to CMPSR. When you have them connected, you can play your CMPSR while on the GarageBand app and you can also play the instruments and sounds from GarageBand while using CMPSR. 

To connect GarageBand and CMPSR do the following: 

  1. Turn on CMPSR using a long press on the button underneath.
  2. Press the button once more to advertise Bluetooth 
  3. Go to the GarageBand App
  4. Go to an instrument and click the settings gear icon in the top right corner.
  5. Click on Advanced and select Bluetooth MIDI 
  6. Find CMPSR and press "connect"
  7. Test it by selecting an instrument or sound and seeing if you hear it when you move the joystick on your CMPSR. 

Sound Effect

A sound effect is a sound that is either created or enhanced artificially by a sound designer. A great example of this is when you are watching a funny show on television and you hear people laughing. While there may be a studio audience laughing, sound designers also usually add extra fake or artificial laughter to enhance the laughter heard by the audience. Sound designers add extra artificial laughs to help create a funny or comedic atmosphere which makes the viewers want to laugh. Sound effects are also added to videos we watch to enhance the plot or action. For example, if something shocking happens, an editor may add a “gasp” sound effect.  

Sound designers sometimes have to get creative with how they make a fake or artificial sound. For example, a sound designer might want to add the sound of crunching snow to a story, maybe they don’t have any snow, or it’s the middle of summer. To solve this issue, sound designers try to create a similar sound from something else. They may take flour and put it into a cloth. When they push a shoe on or walk into the cloth where the flour is, to create an artificial sound of crunching snow!  

When you are recording your sound effects you may have to get creative as well. Don’t be afraid to experiment!

Sound Story or Book 

Sound is an effective technique for telling stories. Sound effects are used to enhance stories and make them more entertaining. Some authors choose to write children’s books that use sound buttons to play as they are read. These are called sound books. As the reader is reading the book, they have buttons they can choose from to play. If the book is about animal sounds, the buttons may be different sounds that animals make and the reader can play the correct sound when they get to that animal. If they are on the cow’s page then they would play the “moo” sound, on the cat page they would play “meow”, and so on.

Sound books are not just limited to animals. They can be used to enhance other readings as well. Imagine you are reading a story about a princess and a witch. When you read about the princess you may press a button to play a light twinkle sound and when you read about the witch you may play a scary lightning sound. It just depends on the book. What types of books do you think would make the best sound books?  

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Author

Authors write books, short stories, essays, poems, and other types of texts. They can either write nonfiction which is based on real events, people, and facts, or fiction which is imagined or made up. Authors write for many different purposes like to inform, persuade, entertain, or to express feelings. Some authors like to pick a particular subject and audience to write for while others just enjoy writing about all things for all people. 

Sound Designer 

Sound designers work in theater, video games, movies, and music. Their main responsibility is gathering and ensuring they have all the sounds needed for the production. This can include editing and enhancing sounds, creating and recording new sounds, and collecting sounds. Sound Designers are also in charge of the sound equipment and ensuring that it is working and set up correctly. 

Foley Artist 

Foley artists are a special type of sound designer. They work to create sounds during post production. They must ensure that the sound they make fits perfectly into the scene. For example, there may be a moment in a video game where the character shoots an arrow into a tree that is covered in snow. The Foley artist is responsible for creating the sound of the arrow flying through the air, the arrow hitting the tree, and the snow falling to the ground. This is different from a normal sound effect that may just be an arrow flying through the air. Foley artists use everyday objects to create these sound effects and watch the scene closely as they are recording to verify they are not missing any key  details.
