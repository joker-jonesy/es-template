Physics

Your challenge is to study how physics works in the real world by building an amusement park. Let's start by understanding concepts that will help you to explain how your amusement park ride works. Physics is the study of how stuff moves. When you study physics, you consider the forces and energy needed to move things.

Velocity and Force

Amusement park rides are machines that use velocity and different forces to make the experience fun and exciting. Velocity is the speed of something moving in a particular direction. Force is anything that changes the motion of an object. Kicking a ball is an example of force.

 

Centripetal Force

When a motorcycle increases in velocity (green), this is called acceleration. When it is going fast and then slows down, this is deceleration. When movement happens in a circle instead of a straight line, velocity keeps changing direction. The reason it changes direction is because of centripetal force (red). Centripetal force is the tension that pulls something back to the 'center'.

 

Newton’s Laws of Motion

An English scientist named Sir Isaac Newton defined three laws of motion that help explain how objects respond to forces.
