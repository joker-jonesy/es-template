Important Vocabulary

  • Survey - to gather information about a specific question or topic
  • Variable - an unknown number represented by a letter
  • Word Problems - a real-life math problem written in the form of sentences or a short paragraph that needs to be solved
  • Storyline - details about what will happen within the video
  • Video Clips - short videos that can be put together to create a longer video
  • Transitions - a way to combine different video clips to make it fit together in a more natural way; completed during post-production
  • Sound Effects - sounds added to a video to enhance the experience for viewers
  • Camera Angle - location of the camera to create different viewpoints for a video

What is a Survey?

A survey is when an individual gathers information about a specific question or topic. First, a question must be created.

Examples:

  • Do you think recess should be longer, shorter, or stay the same?
  • Math is the best class ever! Do you agree or disagree?
  • We should be allowed to chew gum in class. Do you agree or disagree?

Then, the results can be added up and used to support the specific question or topic presented.

When to Use a Letter in Math 

Sometimes we aren’t sure what a number is yet, but we want to show it in mathematics. We can do that by using a letter. The letter is called a variable and it represents an unknown value (number). Any letter can be used but x and y are the most common variables used.

x + 10 = 20
50 ÷ x = 25
y - 43 = 7
6 ⋅ y = 12

Writing it out like this helps to easily solve problems to find the missing value (variable).

x + 10 = 20 x = 10
50 ÷ x = 25 x = 2
y - 43 = 7 x = 50
6 ⋅ y = 12 y = 2

What are Word Problems?

Word problems are real-life math problems written in the form of a sentence or a short paragraph that need to be solved. Word problems include math operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Multi-step word problems have more than one math operation. Word problems can be used to support someone’s thoughts, ideas, and opinions.  Variables can be used to help with finding the answer.

What is Pre-Production?

Pre-production involves work that needs to be done before filming begins. This is the time when directors plan the storyline. A storyline includes the details about what will happen within the video.

It is important for the director to have the following prepared before filming begins.

  1. A script (if one is needed)
  2. A location
  3. An actor or actors- the number of actors needed will depend on the story
  4. Gather and setup equipment and props

What is Production?

Production is when the filming actually takes place. Camera angles are an important aspect of filming. Examples of camera angles include high level, low level, and eye-level. A camera angle is the location of the camera to create different viewpoints for a video.

Things to consider when filming begins:

  1. Be sure the camera is steady and focused.
  2. Include only what is wanted in the shot.
  3. Ensure proper lighting is used.
  4. Ensure the sound is working properly.
  5. Avoid interruptions when possible.

One of the great things about film editing is if there is an interruption, you can piece the video clips together during post-production. Video clips are the short videos filmed that can be put together to create a longer video. These are especially helpful when a director wants to film in more than one location.

What is Post-Production?

Post-production is when the video clips are pieced together and edited to create the final product for a target audience. Saving the recordings and making a copy helps avoid losing everything that has already been recorded. This is when sound effects, transitions, text, and more can be added to complete the film.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Film Makeup Artist

Film makeup artists are responsible for applying makeup to actors before filming begins. Sometimes the makeup is very elaborate, while other times the makeup may be very subtle. Film makeup artists have been known to create a look on an actor that resembles a scar or injury. They have also been known to completely transform someone to look like an animal or other type of mythical creature. These looks aren’t always created on the computer. Film makeup artists need to use things such as surveys to figure out the best method for representing the actors character in a film or tv show.

Film Prop Master

A film prop master keeps up with all of the props needed for the film. Props are  anything used by the actors during the scene. Film prop masters may also be in charge of creating props themselves or hiring someone to create it. They have to ensure everything is accounted for and available when it is needed. Film prop masters may use surveys to figure out what props are needed for each project.

Creative Director

A creative director in film creates and oversees a film project’s theme from start to finish. Creative directors are in charge of creating the message of the film. This is often known as the vision of the film. The creative director communicates with the entire team to make sure the purpose of the film is clear when someone watches it. At times, the creative director will do a test screening. In a test screening, a group of people get to watch the movie early and complete a survey to share their reactions with the creative director. This helps the creative team edit the film and advertise it to an audience.
