Getting Started

Lux Squares and Trigons 

Lux Blox have two main pieces: a square-shaped piece called Lux Squares and a triangle-shaped piece called a Trigon. These pieces can be connected to create many different shapes and designs.

Dimple and Nest

The dimple is the ball-shaped part of the Lux Squares and Trigons. They click into the empty space or "nest" on the other pieces.

Taking Pieces Apart

Lux Blox snap together easily and hold strong so taking them apart can be hard. If your Lux Blox is too hard to pull apart, try placing a pencil into the center hole to help the piece pop off with leverage. Leverage is when you use a simple machine called a lever to help you do hard work. You can learn more about levers here. Try not to twist the pieces to avoid breaking off the dimples.

                       

2D and 3D Shapes

2D Shape

A shape that is 2D has two dimensions: height and length. The height is how tall the shape is and the length is how long the shape is.

Another name for a 2D shape is a polygon. A polygon is a closed 2D shape with straight sides.

Any shape that can be laid flat on a piece of paper is a 2D, or two-dimensional, shape. Examples: square and triangle.

3D Shape

A shape that is 3D has three dimensions - height, length, and width. Think of width as how fat or wide the shape is.

All 2D shapes can be made 3D by adding width.

A 3D shape is any shape that can hold liquid or objects inside of it is a 3D, or three-dimensional, shape. Examples: cube and pyramid.

Regular and Irregular Polygons

A regular polygon is a closed 2D shape that has equal sides like a triangle, rectangle, or a square. An irregular polygon is a closed 2D shape with sides that are not all equal.

Measuring Shapes

Perimeter of a Shape

The perimeter is the outline of a 2D shape.

When you measure the perimeter of a polygon or 2D shape, you have to calculate how long (length) and tall (height) all the sides of the shape are. If you add all the sides of a regular polygon together, you get the perimeter.

If you are trying to measure the perimeter of an irregular polygon, you will still add up all the sides


*The perimeter of a circle is called the circumference. You use a special formula to measure the circumference of a circle.

Area of a Shape

The area is the inside of a 2D shape.

When you measure the area of a polygon or 2D shape, you have to calculate what is inside the shape. If you multiply the length and the height of a regular polygon together, you get the area. After you figure out the area, you will write the units of your answer with a small number 2 at the end to show that you multiplied inches by inches. Another way to say inches x inches is inches "squared" or inches2.

If you are trying to measure the area of an irregular polygon, you will still multiply the length and height then subtract what's missing!

* When you are measuring the area of an irregular polygon, a circle, or a triangle, you still have to multiply the length and the height, but you will have to pay attention to what might be missing from your shape.

Why are Perimeter and Area Important?

Perimeter and area are two common measurements that professionals use every day. In construction, perimeter and area affect how much materials such as shingles, drywall, carpet and even nails are needed, and these calculations are important to help professionals calculate the cost of a job. Farmers use perimeter and area to determine how to fence grazing yards or to help them figure out how many seeds to plant. Engineers use perimeter and area to design cars and airplanes and to help determine the strength and pressure of different materials.
