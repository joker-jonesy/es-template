Important Vocabulary

  • Coordinate Plane - a two-dimensional plane that is formed when two number lines intersect perpendicular (a line that crosses another line at a 90° angle or right angle) to one another
  • Coordinate Pair - an ordered pair of numbers that is used to locate a specific point on the coordinate plane
  • Constellation - a group of stars that create a picture
  • Circumpolar Star - a star that is near one of the two poles
  • Luminary - an object that gives off light

Constellations

What is a Constellation?

A constellation is a group of stars. When the stars are connected, they form an imaginary outline of a shape. Each constellation is given a name.

The first constellations were named thousands of years ago by Egyptians, and other groups of people around the world.

   
How are Constellations Used?

People used the constellations to navigate long ago.

The stars have helped many people navigate. Harriet Tubman, and other slaves, used the North Star, Polaris, to travel North, where they could be free.

Sailors use the stars to navigate their boats in the ocean.

Even some animals, like Mallard ducks, use the stars when they migrate.

Why Do the Constellations Appear to Change?

Polaris is a circumpolar star, which means it is near one of the poles. Circumpolar stars near the North Pole can be seen all year round by people living in the United States.

Not all stars can be seen all year. If you went outside and spent the whole night watching the night sky, you would notice that the constellations look like they are moving from East to West. This happens because the Earth rotates, or spins. 

The constellations in the sky also change with the seasons as the Earth orbits, or moves, around the Sun.

Luminaries

A luminary is an object that gives off light.

Paper luminaries are usually made with paper bags. A small candle, like a tealight candle, is set inside.

Sometimes patterns are cut out of the bag or drawn on the bag. Luminaries are used to light pathways or outdoor spaces, especially on special occasions.

Coordinate Plane

A coordinate plane is a two-dimensional plane that is formed when two number lines intersect perpendicularly, at a  90° or right angle, to one another. The horizontal number line is called the x-axis, and the vertical number line is called y-axis. The point where they intersect is the origin. Coordinate planes use coordinate pairs to show locations on the plane. The first number is the x-value. This gives the horizontal distance from the origin. The second number is the y-value. This gives the vertical distance from the origin. Coordinate planes can be used to graph different forms of data. Doctors use coordinate planes to track changes in heart rate over time. Explorers and hikers use coordinate planes on maps to find the location of trails, ponds, and other attractions.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Coordinate planes can be useful in a variety of jobs:

Forest Ranger

Forest Rangers use coordinate planes in their daily jobs. Coordinate planes help them when they are mapping out an area, searching for the location of lakes, animal species, or other items, as well as a number of other daily tasks.

Astronaut

Astronauts use their knowledge of constellations and coordinate planes when they are finding a specific location. They also use their knowledge of space and coordinate planes to describe where satellites and other space equipment is located.
