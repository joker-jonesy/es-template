Important Vocabulary

  • Energy - power that can be used to do work, like making things change or move
  • Matter - a physical substance that takes up space
  • Transfer of Energy - when energy moves from one place to another or through light, sound, and heat
  • Conservation of Energy - the idea that energy can’t be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another
  • Reflect - to bounce back from a surface
  • Electromagnetic Radiation - a type of energy known as light energy
  • Weather - the weather conditions (temperature, precipitation, wind speed, air pressure) in an area at one time
  • Climate - the weather conditions in an area over a long period of time

Energy

Energy is the ability to do work and to make things change. Energy is used to lift your backpack, to grow plants, and to power your electronics. There are many forms of energy.

One type of energy is called electromagnetic radiation, which most people know as light energy. This type of energy travels in waves. The type of light energy you can see is called visible light. What types of light energy have you heard of before? What do you know about them?

Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It always has to go somewhere. Energy can travel from one place to another.

Transfer of Energy

Energy can be transferred from one object to another. It can also be transformed into different types of energy.

Examples:

  • The sun’s energy is absorbed by a plant and changed into food so it can grow.
  • A car collides with a trash can, making the trash can move a few feet and fall over.
  • You rub your hands together, and the friction of your two hands moving creates heat.

Sometimes, when energy is transferred, it is the same type of energy, like the example with the car and trash can. Sometimes when energy is transferred, it changes the type of energy, like the example where the sun’s thermal, or heat, energy changes into chemical energy inside the plant.

Climate

To design your expedition suit, you need to learn about the climate of your destination. Climate is different from the weather.

The weather describes the atmosphere in an area at one given time. We describe the weather by talking about the temperature, precipitation (rain, snow, hail), wind speed, and air pressure.

The climate is the weather in an area over a long period of time – at least thirty years! The climate of an area could be described as tropical with:

  • Warm temperatures
  • Frequent rain
  • High humidity

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Fashion Designers

Fashion designers create clothing. Fashion designers have helped NASA create space suits for their astronauts. Fashion designers are creative and use math skills, like measuring and calculating area and perimeter, to design clothes. Most fashion designers go to college for fashion design or another arts degree.

Space Scientists 

Thermodynamics and heat transfer researchers help engineers design spaceships, planes, and different kinds of technologies. These researchers have a strong understanding of engineering, so they must use science and math to build machines and equipment that are safe.
