The Three R’s

Do you know about the Three R’s? The Three R’s represent important ways to create less waste and protect our environment.

The average American throws out 4 ½ pounds of trash a day.  That may not sound like much, but it adds up to a LOT of trash. In one year, we throw away enough trash in the United States to fill up enough garbage trucks to form a line halfway to the moon! 

Just like it adds up when everyone throws away trash, it also adds up when everyone throws away a little less. That is why it is important for everyone to reuse, reduce, and recycle.

Reuse

This means to use things more than once, or find other uses for them instead of throwing them away.

Reduce

This means to use less. Using less reduces the amount of energy required to make things and the amount of trash we create when we throw things away.

Recycle

This means to use the materials in our waste (like paper, glass, or plastic) to produce new things.

Creating Comics in Pixie

Making comics is easy in Pixie! Create an image for each frame in your comic using stickers and painting tools along with images you find on the internet. Add bubbles and text to tell your story. Click the Page + button to begin the next frame in your comic. Save your project in your student folder with the name of your comic.

If you need help using stickers, paint tools, or any other Pixie feature

  • Choose your version of Pixie and you will see all of the tutorial options.
  • Go to Help > Recipes4Success. Select "Recipes" (to try practice projects).
  • Go to "Snacks" (to learn individual skills in Pixie).


Publishing Your Comic

When you have finished creating all the frames for your comic, click on the Project button and select Create Images (Pixie 3) or Export (Pixie 4).

You can choose a variety of ways to arrange and present your comic book images. If you select Booklet, you can print and fold your project so a reader can turn the pages.  Make sure you save your work to your student folder. You can also save your images as JPG's and create you comic in Word or PowerPoint. Or Export your project as a video, website or slideshow.
