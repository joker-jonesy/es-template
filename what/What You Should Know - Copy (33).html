What You Should Know

Machines Make Work Easier

The job of a machine is to make work easier. It does this by changing how force is used to move an object, or load.

Work is done when a force moves an object, or load. Force is anything that can change the motion of an object.

For example, you do work when you push a grocery cart at the store.

Pushing is the force you apply to your cart (your machine) to easily move groceries (your load). You also do work when you pull something in a wagon. A force can be a push or a pull. 

Machines Need Energy to Work

A machine needs a source of energy in order to do work. It takes energy to do work. Energy is the ability to do work.

With some machines, you may act as that source of energy, like the man and boy in the example. 

An energy source supplies energy to the machine so that it can do the work.

Parts of a Lever

All levers have three parts.

  • Load - This is the work we want to do. A load is anything that provides resistance to the lever. The elephant is the load. We want to lift the elephant.
  • Fulcrum - This is the balance, or pivot point for the lever. This means the lever tilts around it. The green triangle is the fulcrum.
  • Effort - This is where we apply force. If you wanted to lift the load, you would apply effort by pushing down on the lever.

Three Classes of Levers

Class 1 Levers

In a class 1 lever, the fulcrum is between the effort and the load. 

Class 1 levers make work easier by reducing the amount of force needed to move a load.

Examples: crowbars and seesaws

 

Class 2 Levers

In a class 2 lever, the load is between the effort and the fulcrum.

Class 2 levers also make work easier by reducing the amount of force needed to move the load.

Examples: wheelbarrows and doors 

 

Class 3 Levers

In a class 3 lever, the effort is between the fulcrum and the load.

Class 3 levers actually need more force than it takes to just pick up the load, but they increase the distance and speed of the load's movement.

Examples: brooms and baseball bats
