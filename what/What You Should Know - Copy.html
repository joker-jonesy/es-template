What You Should Know

What is Skoog?

The Skoog is a customizable electronic musical instrument and programmable control device. You can make your Skoog play 5 notes from an instrument like a piano or flute, you can have your Skoog play one note from 5 different instruments, or you can even use your Skoog to record 5 different noises and play those. 

Music Notation 

When you compose music, you write music. There are lots of ways to write down your music so that you remember it. With the Skoog you can write down the pattern of colors. A more popular way to write music is using music notation or music language. Music notation lets people all over the world read your music and play it. Music notation can look complicated at first, but it's just like any other language, if you practice, it will become easy to read and write.

Composers

Composers are people that compose or write music of all types including vocal music, instrumental music, and electronic music. A composer may create music in any music genre, including, classical music, musical theatre, blues, folk music, jazz, and popular music. Some musicians compose their own music to perform while other musicians perform music from other composers.

Major Scales and Pentatonic Scales

A regular major scale has 7 notes (the beginning and end are different octaves of the same note) C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C  and is called a heptatonic scale. A major scale example is the famous Do-Re-Mi-Fa-So-La-Ti-Do. 

  

A pentatonic scale is a musical scale with only 5 notes per octave. When you play your Skoog, it uses 5 different sounds on each of its 5 sides and therefore it can be used to easily compose and play music in a pentatonic scale.  Pentatonic scales are known for being nice to listen to no matter how you organize the individual notes and because of this, pentatonic scales can be found in lots of different types of music including jazz, rock, and folk music across the world. Old MacDonald, Auld Lang Syne  (the New Year's Eve song), and Ring Around the Rosie are all songs that use only the 5 notes in a pentatonic scale.
