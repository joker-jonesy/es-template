Important Vocabulary

  • Angle - a corner or point where two sides of a shape meet
  • Array - ordered rows that have the same number of columns
  • Isometric Grid Paper - a special kind of grid or graph paper with dots or lines that do not form right angles
  • Quadrilateral - any closed shape with four sides
  • Rotation - turning a shape around its center, a corner, or a side
  • Reflection - flipping a shape along a side or corner to get a mirror image

What Is a Property?

A property in math is a way to describe something. Shapes are grouped into categories by their properties. Closed figures are a large group that share the property that all lines, straight and curved, connect.

  • Circles, squares, and triangles are all closed figures.
  • Polygons are closed figures with only straight lines.
    • Circles do not fit in this category.
    • Squares and triangles are in this category.

A property of all triangles is that they have 3 sides. Rectangles are not triangles because they have 4 sides. Polygons with 4 sides are quadrilaterals. Squares, rectangles, and trapezoids are quadrilaterals. There is a special category of quadrilaterals for squares and rectangles. Any quadrilateral with 4 square corners is a rectangle. A square corner is called a right angle. So a square is always a rectangle, but a rectangle does not have to be a square. Squares have one more property. Squares have all 4 sides equal!

You also use properties when you add and multiply. Properties help make learning new things easy. If you know 5 × 7 = 35, you also know 7 × 5 = 35. And, 7 + 5 = 5 + 7. A change in order does not change the answer when you multiply or add; this is called the commutative property.  But this property is not true when you subtract or divide.

To subtract or divide, you use a different property. 7 + 5 = 12, so 12 - 7 = 5. To divide 35 ÷ 7 = ?, you can think, “what times 7 equals 35?” You can use multiplication facts to divide.

What Is a Tessellation?

A tessellation is a special kind of pattern. Tessellations are flat surfaces with one or more shapes that repeat without any gaps. You may have used pattern blocks to make tessellations.

Career Connection and Real-World Application 

Robotics 

Robotics is a type of engineering that makes machines to help humans. Some robots do routine tasks, such as cleaning the floor or putting a car together. Other robots can learn to do new things.

Photography

Photography is both a hobby and a career. Some photographers focus on nature. Pictures taken can help people see patterns in nature. The pictures above showing tessellations in nature were taken by a photographer.

Urban Planners

Urban Planners use shapes and how they fit together to make decisions about the streets and blocks in an area. Think of a city as a very large tessellation. A city has triangles, rectangles, and quadrilaterals. They sometimes use five- or six-sided figures to best fit the spaces in the city. The side lengths and angles are chosen to make the best use of the space.

Urban planners also make sure that there are parks, playgrounds, and open spaces so that we have access to trees and grass. How many shapes can you find in this photo?

Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

Computer-Aided Design (CAD) is a type of computer software that allows many different types of designers to make 2D and 3D drawings. These drawings are vector-based, meaning that the drawings use mathematical formulas to make lines that can be combined to form shapes. This allows the designer to enlarge or shrink the images so that they look the same at any size. Vector images are different from bitmap images, which use tiny square dots to create lifelike images (like photos), but lose detail when enlarged. Architecture, mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, manufacturing engineering, and interior design are just a few professions that use CAD type software for design work.
