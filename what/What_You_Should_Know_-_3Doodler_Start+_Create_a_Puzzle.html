Important Vocabulary

  • Symmetry - when a shape or object has two matching sides; objects that are symmetrical can be divided into two identical parts by a line of symmetry
  • Line of symmetry - a line that is drawn on a shape to show where the one half of the shape can be mirrored to create the other half
  • Interlocking - parts that connect to each other and stay connected unless you take them apart
  • Template - a pattern or tool that helps you create the same shape over and over

Background Information

Shapes and Symmetry

Shapes that are symmetrical have two matching halves. You can tell that a shape is symmetrical by drawing a line of symmetry where both halves of the shape are the same.

Jigsaw Puzzles

You have probably solved a jigsaw puzzle before. Did you know that early jigsaw puzzles were used to teach geography? These kinds of puzzles are designed with interlocking pieces that connect to show a complete picture. Jigsaw puzzles made of wood or cardboard start as complete puzzles before they are cut into lots of pieces.

DoodleBlock Templates

The green rubber pieces in the 3Doodler kit are called DoodleBlocks. They are templates that help you create specific shapes for different projects. One of the templates is a puzzle piece. You can use the puzzle template to create different kinds of puzzle pieces.

Make sure that you plan how each piece will connect when creating your puzzle with the puzzle template.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Mural Painter

Mural painters use an art technique called the grid method. This method is like breaking a picture into smaller pieces to create a giant version on a wall. Sometimes, instead of using a square grid, artists use random doodles on a wall to divide the mural into smaller pieces. Those smaller pieces are just like puzzle pieces that an artist (or group of artists) can use to create their murals. As you work on your puzzle, try to think like a mural artist to make sure that your pieces fit together to create one complete picture.
