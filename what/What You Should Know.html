What You Should Know

Theme Music

Effective horror film music is not just about using a spooky film score that plays when the villain comes on screen. Finding the right jump scare sound effect to make audiences leap out of their seats can be more difficult than you think.

Music Composition 

Composers make music. Since horror movies rely on music, composers carefully consider how to use familiar sounds in unusual ways. Composers change sounds in different ways to help them tell a story.  

Select each item to learn more about sounds.

Rhythm 

The word rhythm is used to describe a specific pattern of sounds. This pattern of sounds is made up of regular and irregular beats that are repeated throughout the song.  A heartbeat, ticking clock, footsteps, a galloping horse—these all have rhythm. 

Dynamics

Dynamics describes how loud and intense music is. Dynamics describes how loud or soft the music is. Musicians will change whether they play a part quietly or loudly depending on how they want the listener to feel.

Pitch

Pitch is how high or low the note or sound goes. Have you ever noticed how people’s voices often get higher when they feel nervous? Composers may create this in their music to help add feeling to their music. 

Dissonance

Dissonance refers to a lack of harmony in music. Some musical notes sound good together, creating harmony. Some don't and they make us feel icky or uncomfortable. An example of a creepy combination is the tritone—a musical chord that can annoy the ears and suggest something is terribly wrong. 

Silence

Silence is no sound. In our day-to-day life, we don’t usually notice all the noise that surrounds us. Stop what you’re doing right now and listen. Even if you are alone in an empty room, you surely hear many noises. We normally just tune out these everyday sounds, so on the few occasions when we are alone in the dead of night and find ourselves surrounded by total silence, it can be scary!

Non-Linear Sounds

Non-linear sounds are sound waves that have a very high range and a higher volume compared to other sounds. They often contain repeated jumps, unusual harmonies, or chaos, and noise – and you can create them by quickly changing the speed of acoustic instruments. 

Non-linear sounds, like the cry of an animal or a human scream, are scary to us because they extend beyond the normal range of the vocal cords. And our brains have grown to understand the strangeness in these sounds, so we instantly know something is wrong or that there’s danger close by. 

Getting Started With GarageBand

GarageBand is a digital audio workstation, it lets you record your own music and you can use instruments from the GarageBand library. You can play the sounds from a keyboard, guitar, bass, drums, strings, and more. You can record using these instruments and even sing along with the music. This app also allows you to edit and share your musical recordings.

Select an Instrument

Each instrument has a play area and a control area. You can play melodies, chords, and sounds by touching the instruments and change the sounds by using the knobs, buttons, and other controls in the controls area.

In the Sound browser:

  1. Swipe left or right to view the instruments, and tap the one you want to play.
  2. Tap More Sounds to see additional sounds for that instrument.

Use the Tool Bars

The tool bar is located at the top of the screen. It has controls that let you move different parts of the GarageBand like Control Playback, Recordings, and Volume. You can use the tool bar to open the Loop Browser and Song Change Settings. 

The navigation buttons on the left include:

  • The My Songs button saves the current song and opens the My Songs browser, where you can create and save songs.
  • The Browser button  opens the Sound browser, where you can choose an Instrument to open the Live Loops Grid. Touch and hold the Browser button to choose a different Instrument.

  • The Tracks View button  lets you switch between the current instrument and Tracks view. Touch and hold the button while using an instrument to switch to another instrument in the song. The Tracks View button is available after you make the first recording in Tracks view.

  • The Grid button  lets you switch between the current instrument and the Live Loops grid. The Grid button only appears if a Live Loops grid has been created for the song.

  • The Track Controls button opens the Track Controls for the currently selected track.

The controls for playing the song (called transport controls) in the center include:

  • The Go to Beginning button  moves the spot to the beginning of the song. While the song is playing, the Go to Beginning button changes to a Stop button.
  • The Play button  starts the song playing, or stops it if it's currently playing.
  • The Record button  starts recording. To stop recording and stop the spot, tap the Play button.
  • The Master Volume slider lets you change the overall volume of the song. The Master Volume slider is also controlled by the iPad’s volume buttons.
  • The Metronome button starts and stops the metronome click.
