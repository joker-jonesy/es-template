Important Vocabulary

  • Metal - a solid material that usually has a shiny appearance and is a good conductor of electricity and heat
  • Reduce - to decrease the amount of something
  • Reuse - finding new ways to use something that might otherwise be thrown away
  • Recycle - turn waste into a reusable material
  • Community - a group of people living together in a particular area
  • Magnet - an object that attracts iron, steel, or nickel
  • Attract - to pull something towards something else
  • Programming - a series of coded software instructions to control the operation of a computer or another machine
  • Block Code - refers to the technique of adding extra bits to a digital word in order to improve the reliability of transmission
  • Decomposing - breaking down a problem into smaller parts

Decomposing Problems

Decomposing means breaking down your problem into smaller parts. Here is an example of decomposing an everyday task like brushing your teeth.

  1. First, grab your toothbrush.
  2. Next, get the toothpaste and put toothpaste on your toothbrush.
  3. Then, wet your toothbrush.
  4. Next, brush your teeth.
  5. Then, rinse your toothbrush and your mouth.
  6. When you are finished brushing, put your toothbrush away.
