Important Vocabulary

  • Technical Requirements - every detail of what a technology needs to be able to do
  • Calibrate - the process of checking to make sure your measuring tools and sensors are correct
  • Software Programs - instructions written in code that tell a computer or machine how to do its job
  • Simulation - a model or imitation of something
  • Fraction - part of a whole
  • Partition - splitting something large into smaller pieces

Unit Fractions

You can think of a pass across a lawn as one part of the whole lawn. Look at this lawn. The dashed lines partition the lawn into six equally sized rows. Something that has been partitioned has been divided up into smaller pieces. Imagine that the robot lawn mower moves back and forth, following the arrows along the lawn. Each pass is one part of the whole. Since there are six passes, each pass is 1/6 of the whole lawn. If the lawnmower makes two passes, it will have mowed 2/6 of the lawn. After three passes, it will have mowed 36 of the lawn, and so on. The whole lawn is 6/6, or one whole lawn!

What Is a Robot?

Click below to find out what machine parts are included in a robot.

Mechanical Parts

Mechanical parts are the physical pieces that make up the machine. Examples: motors, wheels, and gears.

Software Programs

Software programs are instructions written in code, which tell a computer or machine how to do its job. They are like the machine’s “thoughts”.

Energy Source

An energy source supplies energy to the machine that does the work. If you use a bat to hit a ball, YOU are the energy source that helps the bat (machine) move the ball (load).

Sensors

A sensor recognizes light, temperature, movement, or another signal. A sensor controls the robot’s reaction.

Controller

A controller is like the “brain” of the robot. The software program is like the “thoughts” in the “brain” that tell the robot what to do.

How Robots Are Like People

A robot can look human-like but does not have to.

What makes a machine a robot is that it has sensors to gather information, and then uses that information to figure out what to do.

Robots do not actually make “decisions” or “think”. Their software programming tells them what to do in different situations. However, a really advanced robot with complex software can seem like it is making decisions, which makes it seem human-like.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Baker

Have you ever baked or cooked something, or watched someone else bake or cook? Bakers and chefs use recipes that show ingredients needed in fractions of whole measurements, such as half a cup or one fourth teaspoon. If a baker wants to bake multiple batches of something, they need to be able to multiply the fractional amounts to make sure all of the batches come out just right. Bakers and chefs use fractions everyday from making food, to packaging it, and selling it.

Clothing Designer

Clothing design is another career that uses fractions. Clothing designers are like bakers, except they make things with cloth instead of edible ingredients. They must measure and cut fabric to fractions of inches, feet, yards, or meters. Understanding how fractions work ensures they have enough material to complete their project. They don’t want to come up short!

Robotics and Coding

When you grow up, you may have a career in robotics. This is a field that includes making, using, designing, and developing robots. It also involves coding. Codes make the robots work. Robots need engineers to design and create them. They need robotics technicians to repair them. They need sales people to sell them. There are a lot of careers related to robotics.

Coding is key to several careers as well. Software engineers use computer code to develop software. Web developers  use code to build and program websites. Since much of our world is now digital, professionals with coding experience have many opportunities available to them.

Sometimes, careers overlap. For example, someone designing clothes or other fabric objects may work with someone who can program a robot (or other machine) to partition long sheets of cloth into equal parts for a pattern. These machines would need to be calibrated accurately so the pieces are the right size.
