Researching Online

  1. A good online search happens when you use good keywords (words about your topic).
  2. Use “quotation marks” when searching for an exact phrase. “Cars movie” will bring up websites about the Cars Disney movie. Cars movie will bring up all kinds of websites about cars and movies.
  3. Use AND when searching for two words or ideas. Cartoons and robots brings up robot cartoons, lists of animated robot movies, cartoon pictures of robots, and more.

What would you type if you want to find a place that serves lemon pie?

  • Lemon pie? You would probably a lot of recipes, and possibly a pie bakery website.
  • Lemon pie restaurant? Possibly restaurants that serve lemon pie, but it may also bring up restaurants that serve other types of pies and lemon dishes (not necessarily lemon pie).
  • “Lemon pie” and restaurant? This method uses both quotations and “AND” effectively and will probably give you several results you want.
