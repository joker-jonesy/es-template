What You Should Know

What Are Conductors?

You might have noticed that electrical devices, like light bulbs and toasters, use metal parts and wires. This is because electricity flows easily through metal. When electricity flows through something, we say it conducts electricity and we call that material a conductor. Did you know that other materials conduct electricity too? 

                                        

Water Is a Conductor

Almost all water has small particles of other materials floating in it that help conduct electricity. One example see (and probably eat) every day is salt. When you stir salt in water, the salt dissolves into particles too small to see. These tiny particles of salt mix with the water and make it conduct electricity.

Your Body Is a Conductor

Did you know that your body conducts electricity? Your body contains water with particles mixed in. The food you eat and the water you drink mix with liquids in your body. This allows electricity to flow through you. Your body is mostly water. If you weigh 100 pounds, about 65 pounds of that is water.

Electricity will also flow through other things that contain water. MaKey MaKey works with many materials – like fruit and Play-doh – because the water they contain conducts electricity.

How Do Computer Controllers Work?

Have you ever wondered how a mouse, keyboard, or game controller work? What do you think they would look like if you took one apart to see inside?

If you look inside a computer keyboard, you would see a board with lots of tiny metal ribbons. When you push a button on the keyboard it connects two ribbons and completes an electrical circuit. It’s like turning on a switch. Every button completes a different circuit in your keyboard. Computer software determines what each button does – like typing the letter “A”, or make a game player jump.
