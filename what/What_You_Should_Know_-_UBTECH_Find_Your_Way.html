What You Should Know - UKIT Beginner: Find Your Way

Important Vocabulary

  • Axes - the horizontal and vertical lines used to make a graph or chart
  • Constraint - restrictions or limitations
  • Coordinate - a pair of numbers that identify the location of a point on a graph
  • Coordinate Planes - a graph created by two intersecting number lines
  • Criteria - requirements
  • Horizontal -  a line that runs from left to right
  • Origin - where the x and y axes meet
  • Vertical - a line that runs up and down

Design Constraints

When engineers, scientists and mathematicians work on solutions to problems, they must understand the criteria and constraints of the problem. Criteria are the requirements that must be met in order for the project to be considered successful. If you were designing a new type of basketball, a criteria might be how much it can weigh and how big it can be. Cost, size, and weight are common criteria. Constraints are the limitations that are imposed on the project. This might be cost and time to make it, the types of materials available, etc. 

For your project, you will have both criteria and constraints. The criteria for your project is that the robotic car you build will be able to steer. The constraints are that you must use at least three wheels and three servos and you may only use the UKIT Beginner kit materials. The time you are given to build by your teacher is also a constraint.

Coordinate Planes

What is a Coordinate Plane?

Coordinates are two numbers that determine a position on a grid. The point where they intersect is called the origin. They are written as (x, y) which means that the x axis is written first and the y axis is written second. 

A coordinate plane is created at the intersection of two number lines. A coordinate plane has two axes. The horizontal number line is the x axis and the vertical number line is the y axis. It looks like this:

On the x axis, you move back and forth. On the y axis, you move up and down. So a coordinate plane is like a map.

The coordinate plane has four squares - these are called quadrants. We will be working only in Quadrant one which is above and to the left of the y axis. All of the numbers in this quadrant are positive numbers.

How Are Points Graphed on a Coordinate Plane?

We will use this set of coordinates (4,5) This indicates the point is on the x axis at the number 4 and on the y axis at the number 5.

  1. Start at the origin (0,0). This is where the x and y axis meet.
  2. Move over x units to the right.
  3. Move up the y units.
  4. Mark the coordinate (point).

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Air Traffic Controllers

A coordinate grid can be used to identify locations or positions of objects. Air traffic controllers use coordinate planes to track the tens of thousands of airplane flights in our skies every day. The coordinate planes help the controllers to pinpoint locations, altitudes, and speeds of the flights. This helps the controllers to direct the movement of the aircraft and keep everyone safe.
