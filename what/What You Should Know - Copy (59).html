What You Should Know 

What Is Astronomy?

Astronomy is the study of the universe, its objects, and how they move. Astronomers observe space to learn about the past, present, and future of the universe. They also observe space to understand how the universe works. This includes looking at things like planets and moons.

Solar System Planets

A planet is a round object that travels around (or orbits) a star. Planets come in many sizes and are made up of many different materials. Each planet can also have one or more moons nearby. Moons are smaller rocky bodies. They orbit planets the way planets orbit stars. 

There are eight planets orbiting our own star, the Sun. These objects make up our solar system.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest and smallest planet in our solar system. It is a rocky planet with very little air (or atmosphere) on its surface. It has no moons, and is actually just a little bigger than our own moon

Venus

Venus is know as Earth's "sister planet." It is about the same size as Earth. It is the second closest planet to the sun. Venus has a very thick atmosphere that traps heat from the sun, this makes it the hottest planet in our solar system. Like Mercury, Venus has no moons. 

Earth

Next is our home planet, Earth. Earth is the only planet we know of that supports life. The reason Earth can support life is because it has the right atmosphere and temperature, and has plenty of water. Earth has one moon.

Mars

Mars is the last rocky planet of our solar system. It may have once had liquid water on its surface, but today it is dry with a very thin atmosphere. Because of its red surface, Mars is also knows as the "Red Planet."

Saturn

This gas giant is famous for its large rings. It is slightly smaller than Jupiter and is also made up of layers of gas. Saturn also has many moons. We know of 62 so far.

Uranus

Uranus is the first of the "ice giants" at the far edge of our solar system. It is incredibly cold because it is so far from the Sun. It has a very heavy liquid and gas surface covering a rocky center. Uranus has 27 known moons and is famous for its unusual sideways spin.

Neptune 

The last and farthest planet in our solar system is Neptune. It is an ice giant similar in size to Uranus. It also has many liquids and gases around a small rocky center. Neptune has 13 known moons.
