Important Vocabulary

  • Work - done when a force moves an object, or load
  • Force - anything that can change the motion of an object
  • Energy - a property that can be transferred between things to do work
  • Machine - a tool that makes work easier
  • Coordinate Plane - a two-dimensional plane that is formed when two number lines intersect perpendicular to one another

Machines Make Work Easier

The job of a machine is to make work easier. It does this by changing how force is used to move an object, or load.

For example, you do work when you push a grocery cart at the store.

Pushing is the force you apply to your cart (your machine) to easily move groceries (your load). You also do work when you pull something in a wagon. A force can be a push or a pull.

A machine needs a source of energy in order to do work.

With some machines, you are the source of energy, like the man and boy in the example.

An energy source supplies energy to the machine so that it can do the work.

Simple Machines

There are six types of simple machines. You can learn all about simple machines by watching this video or check out the definitions below. 

Lever

A lever helps lift, pull, or push objects. It is a hard bar or board that rests and rotates on a point, but does not bend. The point (fulcrum) does not move, but it supports the bar and allows it to move.

examples: seesaw, claw of hammer

Wheel and Axle

A wheel connected to a bar called an axle makes moving and turning work easier. 

examples: bicycle wheels, door knobs

Pulley

A pulley can help lift or move a heavy load. It is a wheel with a rope, belt, or chain wrapped around it. With effort from you or a motor pulling at the rope, you can lift things. 

examples: flagpole, construction crane

Inclined Plane

An inclined plane is a ramp or another surface that is higher on one side that eh other. It helps us move heavy things up and down more easily. 

examples: slide, truck ramp

Wedge

A wedge has two flat surfaces (planes) It is wider at one edge and pointer at the other. It is used to push things apart. When you push down on a wedge, it move things away from it. Because of it's shape it can also be used to stop objects from moving like a door and door stop. 

examples: doorstop and ax blade

Screw 

A screw is an inclined plane wrapped in a spiral around a cone or a cylinder.  We often use screws to hold things together. 

examples: screw, jar lid

Simple machines can be used separately, or they can be combined into one machine, called a compound machine. Think about how each of these machines work. Which type(s) of simple machine(s) are in each design?

Wedge Screws

A wedge is made of two back-to-back inclined planes. They are used to change the direction of a force to make work easier. A wedge can be used to separate objects or to hold them in place.

Imagine you wanted to split a piece of wood. When you apply force down on the wide end of the wedge, the two sides of the wedge produce forces that point out and away from each inclined surface.

Now imagine you have a door that will not stay open. What do you do?

You most likely use a doorstop.

When a doorstop is placed under a door, there are two forces acting on the wedge:

  1. The force of the door pushing against the top of the doorstop.
  2. The friction force between the bottom of the doorstop and the ground.

These two forces point in opposite directions. If the friction force is stronger than the door's closing force, the door will not move.

Inclined Planes and Triangles

All triangles have certain attributes. These attributes describe what triangles are. All triangles have:

  • Three sides
  • Three angles
  • Three vertices
  • The length of any two sides is greater than the length of the third side.
  • The sum of the internal angles is always equal to 180°.

There are some different categories of triangles, as described below. All of these types of triangles still have all of the attributes of triangles.

  • Right triangle: Has a 90° angle
  • Acute triangle: All angles are less than 90°
  • Obtuse triangle: Has an angle that is greater than 90°
  • Equilateral triangle: All sides are equal
  • Isosceles triangle: Two sides are equal
  • Scalene triangle: None of the sides are equal

An understanding of triangles and their attributes can help your understanding of inclined planes. Inclined planes are created from triangles. You can use a tool called a protractor to find the angle of an inclined plane.

Coordinate Planes

A coordinate plane is a two-dimensional plane that is formed when two number lines intersect perpendicular to one another. The horizontal number line is called the x-axis, and the vertical number line is called y-axis. The location where the lines intersect is the origin. Coordinate planes can be used to graph points, lines, and/or shapes.

Coordinate Pairs

Coordinate pairs, such as (3,5), are used to identify different locations on the plane. The first number of the ordered pair is the x-value. This gives the horizontal distance from the origin. The second number is the y-value. This gives the vertical distance from the origin. To get to the location (3,5) on the coordinate plane, start at the origin,(0,0). Then, move to the right 3 units along the x-axis. Then, move up 5 units, parallel to the y-axis.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Coordinate Planes in Careers

Coordinate planes are used in many professions. Engineers use coordinate planes when they design parts and products. These items need to be a specific size and design, and coordinate planes help engineers make sure they are making the products correctly. Map makers and surveyors use coordinate planes when they are marking the location of roads, buildings, and property lines.
