Important Vocabulary

  • Fossil Fuels - fuel made from the remains of plants and animals
  • Non-Renewable Energy - comes from sources that get used up (examples: wood, coal, natural gas)
  • Alternative Energy - comes from sources that are different than our main sources, which are⁢ non-renewable fossil fuels
  • Renewable Energy - comes from sources that do not run out (examples: wind, water, the sun, and human power)
  • Area - is the measure, in square units, of the inside of a two-dimensional figure
  • Perimeter - is the distance around a figure
  • Solar Panels - are similar to the solar cells used in your model, except they are larger and meant to generate more electricity

Finding Area and Perimeter of Rectangles

What Types of Energy Do You Use?

Each day, you use energy. You use it to go to school, to play, to do chores, and even to grow! What types of energy do you use? Light from the Sun is energy that helps us see and keeps us warm. Food is energy. The food you eat gives you the energy you need to learn, play, and grow. We burn oil, gas, and coal to run our cars, heat our houses, and make electricity.

Fossil Fuels: Buried Energy

Oil, gas, and coal are fossil fuels. These are non-renewable energy sources because we cannot make them as fast as we use them. They are quickly used up and it takes millions of years for new supplies to form. Click here to learn more about non-renewable energy. 

Fossil fuels are made when plants die and decay. Some plants become buried deep in the Earth. Heat and pressure change dead plants into coal, oil, and natural gas.

What Is Renewable Energy?

Renewable energy sources include wind, solar, water, biomass, and human power.

  • Wind: Wind turbines are turned by the wind. As the turbines turn, they spin a generator. The motion creates energy, which converts into electricity.
  • Solar: Sunlight shines on solar panels, which are made to convert light energy into electricity.
  • Water: Flowing water turns a wheel to do work or to make electricity.
  • Biomass: Biomass is energy made from plants and other organic materials.
  • Human power: People do work to create energy.

Alternative Energy and Electricity

Renewable energy is also called alternative energy since it is an alternative to fossil fuels.

Electricity is a very important part of most alternative energy systems. We cannot plug tools into renewable sources directly. In many cases, we need to convert the energy into electricity, which is a usable power source. One way to make electricity is to use a force—such as wind, water, or human power—to turn a generator. A generator is like a motor, but instead of electricity making the motor spin, the spinning generator creates electricity.

Click here to learn more about electricity.

Wind Power: Wind turns the blades, which spins the generator. The motion of the generator creates energy, which converts to electricity.

Human Power: Pedaling the bicycle spins the wheel, which turns the generator. This converts the energy into electricity, which powers the blender. 

Circuitry: Moving Electricity

To use electricity, we must move it from the source of the renewable energy (like a bicycle or a solar panel) to where we need it (like a blender or your home).

Circuits are the way we move electricity. They are electrical systems that allow us to produce, move, store, and control electricity. In a circuit, electricity flows from a power source (like a solar panel or battery) through a wire path to an electrical device (like a light or motor), and then back to the power source through another wire.

Changing Light into Electricity

The solar cell in your kit changes light energy into electricity. Light is made of particles called photons. Photons move very fast. The photons bump into particles in the solar cell causing them to move. The moving particles create a current of electricity.

Storing Electricity

We use electricity all day, every day. Where does electricity come from at night if we rely on solar panels? Or how do we power our homes if we use wind turbines and there is no wind? We need a way to store the electricity produced by renewable energy sources.

Batteries of all sizes and shapes store electricity. Solar panels and turbines change renewable energy into electricity. The electricity charges batteries and the batteries store the electricity until it is needed.

Career Connection and Real-World Connection

Area and Perimeter in Careers

Area and perimeter are used frequently by landscapers, architects, merchandisers, and designers. Designers use area measurements by planning and calculating the costs for things like flooring and wall coverings. Landscapers use area and perimeter measurements to design outdoor spaces and determine the costs for things like fencing, green space, patios, and water features.
