Important Vocabulary

  • Attributes - characteristics of a shape
  • Line Plot - a chart that shows data, using a horizontal number line
  • Quadrilaterals - closed shapes with four sides
  • Rhombus - a closed shape with four sides that are all the same length
  • Square - a closed shape with four right angles and four sides that are all the same length
  • Line Segment - a part or section of a line
  • Subcategorized - to break into smaller groups

How Engineers Solve Problems

Engineers solve problems. After finding a problem, they write the steps they need to take to solve the problem. Engineers call these steps the technical requirements of the problem. Next, they write detailed instructions or design specifications to show how their actions should solve the problem. Then, they collect and study information, make sure their design specifications work, build on their steps, and document their instructions so that others and they can do it again. This is all part of the Engineering Design Process (EDP). The Engineering Design Process is a method engineers use to develop solutions to problems.

Using Rulers

Rulers are used to measure the length of objects. Rulers can measure objects with different forms of measurement, including inches. Have you ever seen a ruler with a lot of little lines between each inch marker? This shows parts of an inch. It helps users to be more accurate when they measure length. When measuring, look for the nearest quarter-inch, half-inch, or whole-inch mark. If you measure a dollar bill, you will see that it is about 6 ¼ inches long. A toothbrush is about ½ inch wide. An unsharpened pencil is about 7 ½ inches long.

Graphing Data

When there is a lot of data, it is best to organize it. This can help viewers understand the data. Graphs and charts can make data easier to read. One way to display data is on a line plot. Line plots use a horizontal number line. The numbers in the number line should match the data that is being shown. In this activity, you will be using whole, halves, and quarter inches on a ruler. So, a line plot organizing this data should include whole, half, and quarter inches.

Quadrilaterals

All shapes have characteristics or attributes used to separate or categorize them into groups. A shape with four sides is categorized as a quadrilateral. Quadrilaterals can also be split or subcategorized into smaller groups such as rectangles, squares, and rhombuses. Each subcategory is a four-sided shape with different features or attributes.

Career Connection or Real-World Application

Step-by-step Directions in Careers

People follow instructions and step-by-step directions every day. Imagine you just got a new table for your room. You get the table out of the box and see that it is not assembled. What are you going to look for next? You need the instructions on how to put it together. Individuals who write these instructions try to describe the process so that the reader understands what to do. Directions that are clear and detailed are easier to follow.
