What You Should Know - CoDrone EDU: Pilot Practice

Important Vocabulary

  • Drone - an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) or flying robot without a pilot onboard 
  • Trimming - to make adjustments to fix flaws in how the drone flies or hovers  
  • Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV) - a flying vehicle with no pilot onboard

What Is a Drone?

Imagine a tiny airplane that can zoom through the air without a pilot inside. Instead, someone controls it from somewhere else using a controller and/or a computer program. A drone, also called an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), is a flying vehicle with no pilot onboard. Drones can do amazing things that people sometimes struggle with including: 

  • Fly high in the sky: Drones can fly over mountains and clouds, giving us views of the world we wouldn’t normally get to see. 
  • Go to places that are hard to reach: Drones can squeeze through tight spaces and explore dangerous areas like caves or volcanoes. Not only can they explore these places, but they can send back pictures and information without putting any humans in danger. 
  • Help people in many ways: Drones can deliver medicine and supplies to people in faraway places, plant trees and crops, or even help firefighters find hot spots in a widespread fire.  

Of course, just like any powerful tool, drones need to be used carefully. Anyone who flies a drone needs to make sure they follow rules and fly safely. 

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Commercial Drone Pilot

This is the most common type of drone pilot job. Commercial drone pilots fly drones for a variety of purposes. They can take pictures and video from the sky to help real estate agents sell homes and also to help with mapping. They can fly over buildings to see if they need to be repaired. They can deliver packages and goods. They can also survey the land so people can see where their property ends and another begins. 

Agriculture

Drones can soar high above the fields, giving farmers a clear picture of everything that's happening. Special cameras can detect tiny changes in the color of leaves, which might mean a plant is sick or needs more water. They can carry special sprinklers to spray water exactly where it is needed. They can even drop seeds exactly where they should go.

Search and Rescue

Drones can be used to search for missing people in large or dangerous areas, including natural disasters. They can also be used to deliver medicine and supplies to people in faraway areas. 

Film and Television

Have you ever seen a movie with amazing views of the world from above, like you are a bird soaring in the sky? These shots may have been taken by drones.  Drone pilots are often used to capture aerial footage for movies, TV shows, and commercials.
