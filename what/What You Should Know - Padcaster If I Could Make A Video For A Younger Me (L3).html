Proportional Relationship

Proportional relationships occur when two or more values increase or decrease at the same rate (y=kx). Proportional relationships can be represented in different ways to include graphs, functions, and ratios. It is possible for movie speed to represent a proportional relationship. Slope can be determined when a proportional relationship is graphed. Slope is the steepness of a line on a graph.

Unit rates compare two values with one of the values simplified to a rate of 1 and show proportional relationships. One example of a unit rate is frames per second. This is used when creating a stop motion short film.

Stop Motion Animation

Stop motion animation is a type of filming that consists of using images of still objects to create the appearance of movement from frame to frame to create short films, movies, commercials, and more. This type of animation has been a part of film for many decades. As technology has evolved and improved, it has helped update stop motion animation techniques and final products. Stop motion animation allows viewers a unique experience and animators a different way of creating content. Examples of stop motion animation films include Nightmare Before Christmas, The Box Trolls, Coraline, and many more.

What is Stop Motion Animation?

Career Connection and Real-World Application 

Stop Motion Animator

A stop motion animator creates clips, commercials, short films, and movies using clay, puppets, models, and more. Stop motion animators use cameras, video technology, and production equipment to create and edit the material. Stop motion animators have to choose the right angles to film, speeds, frames, and more to generate a product appropriate for the selected audience. 

Film and Video Editor

A film and video editor is vital to the finished product of a short film, commercial, movie, and more. A film and video editor’s job is to put the pieces of a film together, add sound effects, title, credits, and anything else pertinent to creating a quality final product for the intended audience. A film and video editor works to speed up or slow down a film to get the intended message across to the viewers.
