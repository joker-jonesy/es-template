What You Should Know

What is a Map?

People have been making maps for thousands of years. Most of us use maps to show the location of a place and plan or follow a route between places. But what are maps, really?

Maps are a type of model – a representation of an original structure, but on a smaller scale. They use sets of points, lines, and areas to represent a place, a pattern, or a relationship.

What Is Google Maps?

Google Maps is an online mapping program developed by Google that allows you to explore the world or your backyard, plan trips, and find places. You can view satellite imagery, street maps, 360-degree panoramic views of streets (Street View), real-time traffic conditions (Google Traffic), and route planning for traveling by foot, car, bicycle, or public transportation.

How to Use Google Maps

To explore the historic city of Boston, Massachusetts (or any city of your choosing), follow the steps below.

STEPS:

  1. Open Google Maps and log into your Google Account (if you are not already logged in).
  2. Since Google Maps automatically opens to your current location, use the Search tool to change the location to Boston, MA.

Map Viewing Options

Use the Explore tool to see street views or take a photo or video tour of the area.

Street View

Expand the Explore tool bar at the bottom right corner to see street views, photos, or video tours of Boston. To see a street view of a location, drag and drop the yellow person to the place you want to see. 

Satellite View

Click on Satellite to change from a simple map of Boston to a photographic view of Boston from above. Notice that the navigation tools change. 

How does the 3D (three-dimensional) option change your point of view? 

Routing Tools

When planning a trip, it is useful to draw routes from one place to another and to know available modes of transportation. For directions, click the Directions button to the right of the search bar. Type in as many locations as you want, and even rearrange the order you would like to visit them. You can create a path by car, bus, bike, plane, or foot. 

Exploring 

Practice using the tools by searching for the Old North Church. 

  • Add a star to save the location of the Old North Church.
  • Zoom in on the Old North Church.
  • Change to Satellite View and tilt and rotate your position. Try the 3D view.
  • Use Street View to place yourself at the church doorstep.
  • Look through some of the photos of the inside of the church.

    

Boston is an old city with lots of US history. Pick another one of these historic locations to explore.

  • Save the new location on your map.
  • Use street view to take a close up screenshot of your location.
  • Take a photo or video tour of the location.
  • Add a path showing directions from the Old North Church to the place you picked.
    • Find the distance to the Old North Church to your second location.
    • Find the time it takes to go from the Church to your location.

Create a Map

You can create a map for every trip you take. On this map, you can save destinations, or places you want to visit, and how you will travel there. When you're finished, you can share your map with your friends and family.

  1. Click on the icon with three lines to open the Google Maps menu.
  2. Click on Your places.
  3. Click on the MAPS tab.
  4. Click on CREATE MAP.

Saving Destinations

As you explore, you can save the places you would like to visit on your virtual tour. When you click on a destination, an information box will pop up. To save this place to your map, click on "+ Add to map."

Creating Layers

You can organize your destinations into different categories, like restaurants, museums, fun things to do, hotels, and more. Add a Layer to your map for each category. You can rename the layer, change the color of each pin that marks the saved location, and rearrange your list of saved locations.

Add to a specific layer by clicking on the layer, clicking on a destination, and then clicking on "+ Add to map."

Map Tools

Use the map tools to:

  • add a marker
  • draw a line from one place to another
    • add a driving, walking, or biking path
  • add directions
  • measure the distance between two places

*You cannot use the Street View feature when you're creating your own map. You will have to close out of your map to see the Street View from a destination.
