Callouts

Standard Callout

Best Practices

A standard callout allows you to insert a unique piece of content within the regular content. If you think of regular content acting like the lecture you would give to teach leadership qualities, the callout box might serve as:

  • an aside (for example, As an Aside: Not everyone will embody all the traits of a leader but should find the balance that is comfortable to them as an individual.);
  • a personal anecdote from the content writer (for example, Let Me Tell You About: Early in my career, I faced a leadership challenge and I…)
  • a case study (for example, Warren Buffet: Warren Buffet is not only one of the wealthiest men alive, he is also an example of a great leader.)

Icon Callout

Best Practices

Icon callouts should be used for consistent, standardized purposes. The icon will work as a visual cue indicating to the learner what type of content will be in the callout box or what they will be expected to do with the content in the callout box. For example, a checklist might always indicate key take away points. These purposes should be determined in advance of designing the course and used consistently throughout the course (or program).

Images

Best Practices

Images can be added to any of your pages to create visual interest and help break up text-heavy courses. This section illustrates the different ways images can be used.

You can do one of the following:

  • Follow the directions below to insert new images on other pages
  • Copy the image alignment samples from this page and past them to other pages

Replace Image

This template comes with some image placeholders. To replace the image:

  1. Select the image and delete it (Note: Do not move your cursor)
  2. Click the Insert Image icon
  3. Select the source of your image
  4. Click the Choose Destination button to save it to the folder you want

Recommendation for banner image size: at least 1200 pixels wide and 400 pixels high.

Insert New Image

Inserting your own image is simple. Once in the Edit HTML mode, you can use Insert Image to add an image. If the image is not the right size, you can edit it using the inline image editor.

Image Alignment

The HTML editor provides Align Left, Align Right and Align Full functions to place an image in particular location.

For example, if you want your image to appear on the left with the text next to it:

  1. Insert your image on the empty line before the paragraph
  2. Crop the image or set its dimension to your desired size
  3. Apply Align Left on the selected image

Two Column Panels

Best Practices

Two column panels are useful for displaying two pieces of closely linked but separate content, for example, two cases exploring the same law or two examples of applying the same principles. Avoid using two column panels in place of two columns of text, as the box surround emphasizes the separation of the two pieces of content.

Video Embed

Best Practices

Whenever possible, videos should be embedded in the content, rather than linked to. This simple stylization houses all the required information related to a video, including a title, the context or explanation of the video and its relevance to the learner, a link to the video’s location and a downloadable transcript of the video.
