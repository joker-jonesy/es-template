Accordion: Standard

Best Practices

Accordions are useful when you need to present learners with a list of items. There may be a lot to learn about each item, but the first priority is for the learner to recognize and learn the list of closely associated items first, then allow them to dig deeper (by opening the accordion) to learn more.

Accordion: Numbered

Best Practices

Numbered accordions are useful when you need to present learners with a list of items in which there is always a set number of items in the list, for example, the top ten films of the year, the five steps of the scientific process, or the 192 breeds of dogs (Wait a minute! That last one is trick. You should never have 192 accordions, Find ways to break down the content, categorize and organize it into smaller groups of content to improve learner retention. In the example of dog breeds, you may divide them into smaller categories based on size, area of origin, or groups).

Click and Reveal: Standard

Best Practices

A click and reveal is a useful tool when you want to present a learner with a question, statement, or fact, that you'd like them to reflect on before you present them with the key learning related to the original statement.

Click and Reveal: Standard: start copy

Johnny's mother has three children. The first child is named April. The second child is named May. What is the third child's name?

Johnny, of course!

Click and Reveal: Standard: end copy

Click and Reveal: With Icon

Best Practices

A click and reveal with an icon is useful if you have a series of click and reveals that you'd like to connect. For example, you may consider a thought bubble icon if you have a series of click and reveal reflections.

Click and Reveal: With Icon: start copy

Icon Click and Reveal

Before Mt. Everest was discovered, what was the highest mountain in the world?

Mt. Everest; it just wasn't discovered yet.

Click and Reveal: With Icon: end copy

Flip Cards (2-col) w/ Image overlay

Best Practices

Flip cards are an excellent choice for displaying a collection of items, that don't necessarily have a strict structure in their relationship to one another. To clarify, there is usually an established or required order to presenting the items listed in accordions or tabs. Think of flip cards items more like items that belong in the same group but can be laid out at random without impacting meaning. Flip cards are an especially engaging choice when you can incorporate images.

Tabs: Vertical

Best Practices

Tabs are useful for elaborating on an idea that has set structures or categories of sub-information, particularly, if you have a collection of pieces of content that all have the same set structures. For example, laying out a series of studies that each has a hypothesis, a method, and findings. Tabs are especially effective when the information presented in the first tab is built on in the second; the second built on in the first, and so on (as in the example of a study's hypothesis, method, and findings).
