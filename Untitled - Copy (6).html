What You Should Know - 3Doodler Start+: Design a Uniform

Important Vocabulary

  • Uniform - special clothing that people wear for work. Uniforms are designed for specific purposes depending on the needs of a job.
  • Mannequin - a pretend human, usually made of plastic, used to design or display clothes
  • Model - a small-scale version of something that represents an actual-size object. Models can be used for different reasons, including testing designs and demonstrating the features of a design.

Background Information

Purposes of Uniforms

Different jobs require different kinds of uniforms. Uniforms can help protect workers from danger. They can also be used to tell others about the roles or responsibilities you have. Other uniforms help promote a sense of unity or professionalism in the workplace.

Safety

Some jobs sometimes require special clothing to help keep workers safe. For example, workers on a construction site, in a factory, or in a warehouse need to wear hard hats, high visibility vests, and heavy-duty boots. Sometimes they might also need gloves, safety glasses, and hearing protection.

Responsibilities

Doctors, nurses, and other medical workers wear uniforms called scrubs. In some hospitals, the color of a worker’s scrubs tells others what their role is. For example, a doctor might wear a white lab coat, a nurse might wear light blue scrubs, and a surgeon might wear teal scrubs. This color system can help communicate what responsibilities each person has at work.

Unity and Professionalism

Sometimes a job requires a uniform to help workers feel united with one another. They might also have a uniform to encourage confidence in a professional team working to help customers. Workers at restaurants, stores, or even the post office wear uniforms to help customers know who they can ask when they have questions. Lawyers and other people working in businesses wear suits to help their clients feel confident working with them.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Safety Officer

Safety Officers are responsible for the safety of their coworkers on a job site. They must know the laws and regulations from the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and make sure that proper equipment and procedures are used to keep their workplace safe.

Fashion Designer

Fashion Designers create clothing concepts and designs for lots of different purposes. Some fashion designers focus on cutting-edge ideas for models to wear at fashion shows, while other designers work with existing clothing ideas to improve their functionality or safety. Fashion designers use mannequins to help them create clothing that fits people of different sizes.
