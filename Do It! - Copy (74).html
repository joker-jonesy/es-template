Do It!

Your challenge is to use Google Maps to plan and take a virtual trip to any locations around the world. 

STEPS:

  1. Create a Google Account.
  2. Review the items that you need to collect during your trip.
  3. Explore Google Maps by taking a virtual trip to the historical city of Boston, Massachusetts.
  4. Create your own virtual trip. 
  5. Create a presentation about your trip. 

Create a Google Account

Check with your facilitator to make sure you can create a Google account or see if your SmartLab already has one.

  • Access the internet.
  • Open to the Google Accounts Login page.
    • If you have a Google account, sign in.
    • If you need to create an account, select, “Create an account now” and follow the directions. 

Take a Virtual Trip to Boston

Refer to the What You Should Know: How to Use Google Maps. Explore Boston as you learn how to use the Google Map tools. Once you are comfortable, plan your own virtual trip.

Plan Your Trip

Explore a location that interests you.

Have you ever wanted to explore the Pyramids of Giza, the mountains of Nepal, or the Taj Mahal?

Answer these questions and record the answers in your ePortfolio:

  • Where do you want to go? 
  • Why do you want to go there?
  • How will you travel to the place of interest: By plane, train, bus, bike, car, on foot?
  • How will you get around once you arrive at your destination? Once you get to your place, will you walk, bike, drive, or use other transportation to explore the area?

Ideas to get started:

  • Most popular cities in the world to visit (according to worldatlas.com)
  • Ancient civilizations (TheTopTens®)
  • Stunning natural landscapes (©Lonely Planet) 

Use Google Treks (https://www.google.com/maps/about/treks/#/grid) and Street View (https://www.google.com/streetview/) to get some ideas.

Record Your Trip

Keep a record of your trip in your ePortfolio. Take screenshots of Google Maps locations.

Collect these items during your travels:

1 large map

One large map of the area you visited.

2-3 small, detailed maps

Two or three small, detailed maps. Zoom the map in on some special locations you visited.

1 map and 1 satellite view map

Use both map and satellite view to show the same place but with different tools.

1 street view map

One or two street view images of your favorite places on the trip.

2-3 images of interesting places

Several photographs of the most interesting places your visited. Find your favorite photos posted by other people on Google Maps.

1 complete path map

Add a star to each of the places you explore in detail. Then, Create a complete path of your trip showing how you got to each place you visited. Try to find the visiting order and directions that make your travel time is as short as possible.

Create a Presentation

Create a presentation of your trip using presentation software like PowerPoint or Google Slides. 

Include pictures and labels that answer the following questions:

  • Where did you go?
  • Why did you pick this place?
  • What did you learn about this place?
  • What would you want to see in person if you could travel there?
  • How would you get to your place? How would you get around when you got there?
  • What would you pack for your trip?
