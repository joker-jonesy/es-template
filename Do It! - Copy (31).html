Do It!

Your challenge is to learn about architecture using the ArcKit GO Colors 2.0 building set to build a community space.

Collect Information

Before designing your community space, you should collect information about the people who will use that space.

  • What is the space going to be used for?
  • Where will it be built? How much space is available for building?
  • How big does the space need to be for people to use it correctly? 
  • Who will use the space? What are their specific needs? 

Design Your Community Space

With the information you gathered, sketch the community space you want to build.

Many architects begin by sketching on paper the basic layout of the walls and door openings.

As you design, you may want to use the ArcKit building pieces to experiment with the layout of the building.

  

As you design, remember to think about what the community needs. Don't forget about any constraints or limits, you may have, like how much money you can spend, how much space you have to build, and the kind of materials you can use, etc.

Build Your Community Space

Using the ArcKit GO Colors 2.0 pieces, build the community space you've designed.

  1. Begin by setting up the floor pieces.
  2. Use the yellow and clear wall, window, and door pieces to continue building up.
  3. Once the walls are in place, add the roofing.
  4. Use the tile pieces and texture sheets to give your model a polished and professional look.

Need more texture sheets? Go to the Architectexture page on ArcKit's website. You can print, cut, and attach these texture sheets to your building to make it look like there is grass, stone, brick, tile, and wallpaper for the floors, walls, and roof of your model.

  

When you finish building, share your model with the people who would want to build this space. This group could be your principal, school board, local government, or a private business. When you share the design, make sure to explain how this community space will benefit your community. Record your ideas in your project journal.
