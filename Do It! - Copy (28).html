Do It!

Your challenge is to use the force of gravity and tower playing pieces to get a ball to fall from the Start Position to the Target. See how many challenges you can solve!

Getting Started

Instruction Manual 

Read the Gravity Maze Instructional Manual so you understand all the parts and terms used in the game. If you have questions, you can probably find the answer in the instruction manual.

How to Play

  1. Choose a challenge card. You will want to start with beginner, but eventually, you can also try intermediate, advanced, or expert.
  2. On the card, you will see the Start Position (represented by a colored tower icon with a white dot inside) and the red Target Tower. You may also see more towers represented on the card. Add the towers you see to your grid. Make sure they are in the right spots and facing the right directions. Dot markings help you figure out which way the towers should face.
  3. Look under Add to Grid. This shows you which additional towers you will need to add to your grid in order to find the solution. Colored squares represent vertical towers. Colored rectangles represent horizontal towers. 
  4. The Solution to the card is on the back. But try not to look! Challenge yourself to solve the card on your own.
  5. If you cannot figure out the solution, try solving a simpler card. The cards are numbered 1-60 with 1 being the easiest. 

Did you solve your challenge? Try another!

ePortfolio Ideas

Choose two or three challenge cards that you think were particularly hard to solve. Explain how gravity worked to bring the ball down to the Target Tower in your chosen examples. 

Take pictures of your challenge cards and set-ups, even if your solutions did not work. Once you solve the challenge or review the solution, you can explain what you tried to do originally, and how you changed your tower arrangement to make it work.
