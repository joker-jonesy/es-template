Do It! Google Maps: Maps and Coordinates

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create a custom map of any location in the world using Google Maps. Then, use a coordinate plane and coordinates to navigate your custom map.

 Getting Started

Create a Google Account

  1. Check with your facilitator to make sure you can create a Google account or see if you already have one.
  2. Access the internet.
  3. Open to the Google Accounts Login page.
  • If you have a Google account, sign in.
  • If you need to create an account, select, “Create an account now” and follow the directions.

Plan Your Trip

Explore an area that interests you. Have you ever wanted to explore the Pyramids of Giza , the mountains of Nepal , or the Taj Mahal?

Consider these questions:

  • Where do you want to go?
  • Why do you want to go there?
  • How will you travel to the place of interest: By plane, train, bus, bike, car, or on foot?
  • How will you get around once you arrive at your destination? Once you get to your location, will you walk, bike, drive, or use other transportation to explore the area?
  • What will the coordinates be of the places visited if you create a coordinate plane over your map?
  • How do coordinate values relate to real-world application?

Record Your Trip

Keep a record of your trip, take screenshots of Google Maps locations.

Collect these items during your travels:

1 large map

One large map of the area you visited.

2-3 small, detailed maps

Two or three small, detailed maps. Zoom the map in on some special locations you visited.

1 map and 1 satellite view map

Use both map and satellite view to show the same place but with different tools.

1 street view map

One or two street view images of your favorite places on the trip.

2-3 images of interesting places

Several photographs of the most interesting places your visited. Find your favorite photos posted by other people on Google Maps.

1 complete path map

Add a star to each of the places you explore in detail. Then, Create a complete path of your trip showing how you got to each place you visited. Try to find the visiting order and directions that make your travel time is as short as possible.

Create a Coordinate Plane

Use Google Slides to create a coordinate plane and add your map. 

  1. Open up Google Slides and create a Blank presentation. 

  2. Click the Insert button to add the screenshot of your map onto a blank slide. 

  3. Click the Insert button and select line Arrow. Draw an arrow along the edge of your map (y-axis) on the left hand side, from bottom to top with the arrow pointing North. 

  4. Draw an arrow along the bottom edge of your map (x-axis), from left to right with your arrow pointing to the right or East. 

  5. Add numbers to the x-axis and y-axis using the text box function in Google Slides. Begin with zero in the bottom left where both lines meet; this is the origin known as (0,0). 

  6. Go up by ones (1,2,3,4...). Hit the tab button twice between each number to ensure even placement between numbers for an accurate representation of your number line. You can ensure the placement is even by use the ruler function in Google Slides. 

Plot Your Coordinates

After you have created the coordinate plane, you will need to plot the coordinates for the places you visited. The coordinates should be represented in ordered pair form (x,y).
