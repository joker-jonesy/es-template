Important Vocabulary

  • Volume - the space in a 3D object
  • Circuit - a path for electricity to flow
  • Conductors - materials that allow electricity to flow freely through them (e.g. metals, salt water, graphite, and the human body)
  • Components - parts that you can add to your circuit to allow it to complete different actions (e.g. LED lights, buzzer)
  • Electricity - a form of energy

Circuits

Electricity flows from a power source (like a battery) along a path (a wire or another conductive material) to a load (an electrical device like a light), and then back in a loop.

All circuits have these three parts:

  1. power source (battery)
  2. load or resistor
  3. conductor (wire path)

A load is also called a resistor because it creates resistance between the two ends of the power supply. The resistor needs electricity to work and as it does, it reduces the current flow. In this example, the resistor is a light. As the bulb uses some of the electricity to light up, it reduces the current.

A path does not have to be made of wires. It just needs to be made of a conductive material so there is a path between the power source and the resistor. Some circuits use metal strips while others use magnets. Tinkercad Circuits uses wires (which you can color code.) Copper wire is conductive. Can you think of other conductive materials?

Closed Circuit

In order for electrical current to flow, the circuit must be closed. A closed circuit is a closed path (or loop) where electrical current can flow. If there is a break anywhere in the path, the electricity cannot flow and it is called an open circuit.

An electrical path can accidentally be broken, or you can use a switch to control the flow of electricity. When a switch is "on," it connects the two sides of the path, closing it and letting the current flow through. When it is "off," it breaks the connection; the path is open and current cannot flow through. This is why an on-off switch is sometimes called a circuit breaker.

To activate the components in Tinkercad Circuits, you must create a closed circuit. You can test to see if you have connected all components correctly in a closed circuit by running a simulation. If your components such as lights, speakers, and motors do not work when you click "Start Simulation," you will need to troubleshoot to figure out where your connection is broken.

Volume

Volume is the space within a 3D shape. The volume of a rectangular prism or cube can be determined by multiplying the length, width, and height together: V=lwh. Career Connection and Real-World Application

Volume in Careers

Many jobs measure volume. Nurses and doctors need to know volume so that they can give patients the correct amount of medicine. Trucking companies also use volume; they must know the volume of their trucks, so they know how much product they can put into each truck. Movie theater operators must also use volume when purchasing containers for drinks and popcorn. The containers must hold the advertised amount of popcorn and liquid.

Electrician

Electricians make circuits as they construct and repair lights, sockets, and other electrical items. All of these items connect to a device called a circuit breaker. Circuit breakers help control the electrical flow to devices and outlets in a home. Electricians need to consider the volume of objects and spaces that house circuits to ensure that all components will fit.
