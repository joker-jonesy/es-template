 Create a Large-Scale Path

  1. Refer to one of the paths you created previously, or create a new path.
  2. Place chart paper or bulletin board paper on the floor.
  3. Recreate the path on a larger scale on the floor.

    For example:
    Point A to Point B measures at two inches on a smaller scaled path.
    You want the larger scale to be five times bigger.
    2 × 5 = 10; therefore, the distance from Point A to Point B will be 10 inches long on a larger scale.
  4. Make a prediction.

    If you think Ozobot’s speed for each interval will be the same, can you predict how long it’ll take Ozobot to travel through your large scale maze? Use this equation: t = d/s to find out the time when you know the distance and speed.
  5. Calculate the distance and speed of your Ozobot on a larger scale.

    The formula for speed is as follows:
    s = d/t
    where s = speed; d = distance; t = time
  6. Compare the distance and speed from the smaller scale path to the larger scale path. What do you notice? Were your predictions accurate? Did Ozobot travel slower or faster than you predicted? Why do you think that is?
