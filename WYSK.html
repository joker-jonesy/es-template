Important Vocabulary

  • Conduction - heat moving because of direct contact (example: is when you touch a metal spoon after it has been placed in a bowl of hot soup)
  • Insulators - do not allow heat to flow through easily
  • Conductors - allow heat to flow through easily
  • Elapsed Time - the amount of time that passes from the time an event starts to the time the event ends
  • T-chart - a two column chart used to organize data or information

The Science of Insulation

Heat is a type of energy called thermal energy. Heat will always travel from areas of high thermal energy to areas of low thermal energy. This is why your hot soup gets cold if you leave it sitting out. Since the soup has a much higher thermal energy than its surroundings, its heat gets transferred from the soup to the air, bowl, and table. We cannot stop it from moving completely, but there are ways to slow it down.

Conductors and Insulators

Heat travels at different rates through different materials. For example, you may have noticed that many metals are good conductors of heat, meaning they transfer heat quickly from one object to another. Other materials, like most types of plastic, conduct heat very slowly.

Barriers that slow down the transfer of thermal energy between two substances are called insulators. There will always be some heat that can move through an insulator, but it is much less than if it were a conductor. This increases how long a substance can stay at its original temperature when in a hotter or colder environment.

Why Keep Heat In or Out? 

We have many reasons to keep heat where it is.

In the winter, you wear heavy coats and mittens that act as insulators. They help keep your body heat inside you instead of moving into the cold air around you. Your house even uses insulation material inside of its walls to help keep the heat inside in the winter and keep it outside in the summer.

Scientists, engineers, and designers are always working on better ways to insulate things. This includes finding better insulators, making existing insulating materials lighter, and reducing the costs of insulating technology.

What is a T-Chart?

During your challenge you will notice that some of the data is presented in a t-chart. A t-chart is a two column table used for data or information. A t-chart has a label on both of the columns and can have numbers to words in the rows below.

What is Elapsed Time?

Elapsed time is the amount of time that passes during an event or an activity. You will need to know the time that the event started and the time that the event ended to find the elapsed time. For example, if I start eating lunch at 1:00 pm and I end at 2:00 pm, the elapsed time is 1 hour.

How Can I Find Elapsed Time Using a T-Chart?

The t-chart is used to help you organize and keep track of the amount of time that is passing.

Step 1: Add the word start and the start time to the t-chart.

Step 2: Add hours and minutes and keep track of the time. Stop when you get to the end time.

Step 3: Add up the hours and minutes to find the elapsed time.

How Can I Find Elapsed Time Using a Number Line?

A number line can also be used to organize and keep track of the amount of time that has passed to find the elapsed time.

Step 1: Add the start time and the end time to the number line.

Step 2: Add time (minutes or hours) to the number line. Write the amount of time above the number line.

Step 3: Record the time below the number line.

Step 4: Continue adding time in minutes and hours and recording the time until you get to the end time.

Step 5: Add all of the minutes and hours together to find the elapsed time.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

Air Traffic Controller

An air traffic controller is responsible for the safe take-off and landing of airplanes in airports. In addition to needing great concentration and communication skills, air traffic controllers use math to track and compute speed, time, distance, and altitude.

Have you ever wondered who is responsible for planning and designing the roads, bridges, highways, and other infrastructure in your city or town?

Civil Engineer

Civil engineers have this responsibility. In order to plan for a new bridge, civil engineers will need to complete complex calculations to ensure that the bridge does not collapse. Civil engineers will also use a lot of data about traffic patterns to decide where to place a new highway. The data is organized into charts and tables and then computations are made to help inform the location, cost, and labor needs of roads and highways.
