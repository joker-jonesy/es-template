The Challenge - Vernier Elementary Energy Efficiency: Keep Our City Cool

The Challenge

Your challenge is to use the infrared thermometer to measure the heat generated by different materials. Then, consider how the materials we use to build our city impact the temperature of our cities. Create a pie chart to represent the surface materials that make up a certain area by measuring the size of each slice on the pie chart and drawing it on a circle. Finally, make recommendations to help minimize your contribution to urban heat islands. 

Have you ever walked from grass onto concrete and burned your feet? Why is concrete so much hotter than the grass? Why does concrete feel warm even after the sun has set?

What I'll Learn 

  • I CAN use an infrared thermometer to measure the heat generated by different materials.
  • I CAN measure angles in degrees using a protractor.
  • I CAN recognize angles as geometric shapes formed when two rays share an endpoint.
  • I CAN recognize an angle as the circular arc between two rays.
