Do It! UKIT Beginner: Find Your Way

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build and code a robotic car using the UKIT Beginner kit. Then, program your robotic car to navigate to selected coordinates on a grid created on your classroom floor.

 Project Steps

  1. Build Your Robotic Car

  2. Set Up a Grid

  3. Code Your Robotic Car to Coordinates

  4. Freda's Neighborhood 

Build Your Robotic Car

Build your robotic car. 

Criteria: The robotic car must be able to steer, use 3 or more tires, and use 3 or more servos.

Constraints: You may only use the materials provided in the UKIT Beginner kit. Your teacher will tell you the time limit for building and capturing your data. Take a picture of your robot from at least two different angles. You will upload these in your project submission.

Building Tips:

  • Use one servo for steering and two servos for driving
  • Keep it simple! While it is tempting to add to your robotic car, it doesn’t need to have anything fancy, focus on building a robotic car that drives.
  • If you have not completed UBTECH: Walk the Line project starter, it is recommended that you review the building instructions in that activity first.  

Set Up a Grid

You will need to construct a large coordinate grid in your classroom. You only need to construct Quadrant I.

  1. First, find an area that will be large enough to create a grid that is at least 5 units x 5 units. Tip: Sweep the floor before laying the tape to make your grid!
  2. Use masking tape to show where the x and y axes are. This will look like a large L on the floor since you are only mapping out Quadrant I.  
  3. Label the axes and mark off every unit. If your classroom has tiles, use the tiles to indicate 1 unit. If you do not have tiles, mark the units at every foot.

Tip: Use a measuring tape to makes sure each unit is the same.

Code Your Robotic Car from the Origin

Program your robotic car to drive to each of the coordinates in the table below from the origin (0,0). When your robotic car has successfully reached each coordinate, make a mark on the grid with tape and label it with the appropriate letter from the table. When you have completed all of them, take a picture of your grid and a screenshot of at least one of the programs you wrote. These will be uploaded in your project submission.

Location Coordinates
A 4, 2
B 2, 3
C 1, 5
D 3, 0

Programming Tips:

  • Be sure that your robotic car always begins in a forward direction.
  • Always line up the robotic car at the origin, using the front axle as your guide.
  • For your first try, set the steering angle at 45* and run the robotic car. Observe how it moves. Adjust your program from there.

Freda's Neighborhood

Now that you have a robotic car that is able to move and steer and you have programmed to move from the origin to specific coordinates, try programming the robotic car to reach coordinate pairs by moving from one coordinate pair to the next in order. Below is table with coordinates, each coordinate pair represents a place in Freda's neighborhood. Freda's house is located at the origin (0,0).  Add the coordinate to your floor grid with the location labels then program your robot to move to the coordinate pairs in the order they occur in the story below. 

Location Coordinates
Home 0, 0
School  3, 6
Library  5, 3
Grocery Store 4, 4
Mary's House  6, 2

Freda starts her day at home (0,0), she has breakfast and gets ready for school. After breakfast, Freda heads to school (3,6). After school she meets up with her friend Mary at the library to work on a project (5,3). After Freda and Mary spend some time at the library, Mary's mom calls to let her know it's dinner time. Mary asks her mother if Freda can come over for dinner. Mary's mother agrees, but tells the girls that she forgot something she needs for the meal at the Grocery Store. Freda and Mary head to the Grocery Store (4,4) then quickly to Mary's house for dinner (6,2). After dinner, Freda has the head back home for bed. She thanks Mary and her mother for dinner and heads home (0,0). 

After you have programmed your robotic car. Plot the coordinates on a coordinate plane like the one below and trace the path that Freda followed from one coordinate pair to the next. You will upload this in your project submission. 

Finally, reflect on the following questions:

  1. What is the farthest place from Freda’s house? What is the closest?
  2. How many units total did Freda travel? How do you know?
