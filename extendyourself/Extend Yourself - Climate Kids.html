Explore Local Climate Change

Learn about your local climate changes. Locate data and create a graph that shows a change in your local climate. Below is an example that shows change in average high and low temperatures for the USA over the past 50 years.

Record High and Low Temperatures in the USA 1950 to 2009

  1. Use NOAA.gov or Weather.gov to find data about the annual temperature, rainfall, or number of wildfires for a span of 50 or 100 years for your city or state.
  2. Decide how best to graph this by marking every decade (10-year pattern).
  3. Describe what the graph shows. Has your rainfall increased or decreased? Do you see any patterns? Can you make any predictions from the data?
