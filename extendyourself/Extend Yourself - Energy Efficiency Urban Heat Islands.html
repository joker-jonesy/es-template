Extend Yourself - Vernier Elementary Energy Efficiency: Keep Our City Cool

  • Research why certain materials are hotter than others. How does color and density affect how materials hold onto heat? What is the hottest material you can find with your IR sensor? What is the coolest?
  • Urban heat islands can be dangerous for vulnerable populations like the old, young, sick people, and pets. Are there people or animals in your community that you can help during heat and cold swings in weather? What can you do to help keep them safe? Make a list of ways these people and animals could combat extreme heat and cold and share it with their caregivers.
  • Pie charts are great at displaying data about numbers or categories such as colors or animals. Find another type of data that you could present using a pie chart. Gather the data and make the pie chart. This tool at Math is Fun, can help you construct a pie chart. Just click the pie button at the top.
  • Keep track of the daily high temperatures in urban locations and rural locations near where you live. Analyze the data to see if you notice a difference. Find a visual way to present your data. To look up current and historical high temperatures, just type your zip code into this National Weather Service site.
