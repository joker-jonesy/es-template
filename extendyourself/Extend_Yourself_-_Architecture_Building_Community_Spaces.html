Extend Yourself - Arckit: Design and Measure Community Spaces

Expanding Community Spaces

Continue to expand your community space. Design another building that would give your community members another space to use. Collaborate with another student or another group to combine your designs for a larger community space.

What spaces will your city or town need if it continues to grow? What places do you wish your city or town had? How can your designs create a larger area for use?

Create a 3D Model

Use Tinkercad to create a digital 3D model of your design. Once you've designed it, you can share your digital model with classmates, your teacher, and community members.

Scaled-Down Drawing

Begin by choosing a building you want to work with. You will use this building to create a scaled-down model on paper. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Find the length and width of the building.
  2. Decide on a scale factor.
  3. Find the scaled-down model size: Divide the length and width of the building by the scale factor you chose.
  4. Finally, sketch your scaled-down building design on paper. Remember to include the new dimensions.

Create a 3D Model with Objects

Using objects such as blocks, boxes, or tiles, create a 3D model of a building. You can even use LEGOs! Once your design is complete, write down the dimensions of the footprint (length and width). Then, multiply your dimensions by a factor to determine the measurements for the full-sized building. For instance, you could say your building is 200 times as large as the model, which is 16 inches by 4 inches.
