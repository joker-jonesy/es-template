Extend Yourself - Gravity Maze: Gravity Word Problems

Working Backwards with Challenge Cards

Choose a more difficult Challenge Card. After spending time trying to solve the card, look at the solution and see if you can figure out how the tower should be built. Which parts of the towers are being used? Are there sections of towers involved that you didn't even notice before?  Does the ball roll through the same tower more than once? If so, how does that work?

Create Your Own Challenge Card

Create your challenge card! Invite your friends to solve your challenge. Be sure to build the gravity maze so you know it works.

Write Word Problems

Write your own word problem using measurements from three of your challenge cards. Give these to a friend to solve, and include a drawing or a number line. 

Example A: A maze has 4 sections. The height of the sections is 15 cm, 20cm, 20 cm, and 24 cm. How high would the sections be if they were stacked into one tower?  

Example B: A metal ball has a mass of 135 grams. The total mass of 3 glass marbles is also 135 grams. Each glass marble has the same size and mass. What is the weight of one glass marble?

Predictions Based on Mass

Find marbles or small balls made of different materials. Find the mass of each with a gram scale or kitchen scale. Predict which marble or ball will reach the bottom in the least amount of time. Test your prediction with at least 3 different mazes. Was your prediction correct? What can you conclude about mass and gravity?
