Extend Yourself - Vernier Elementary Energy Efficiency: Design an Expedition Suit

Develop a Prototype

Develop a prototype, or sample, of your expedition suit. Prototypes can be created at a smaller scale, like the size of a small doll instead of your size. You can even start by developing a glove so that you can put your hand inside it.

Test it out! Put your prototype in a cold (fridge, freezer, outside during the winter) or hot (sunlight, under a lamp, outside during the spring or summer) environment. Use your Vernier Go Director Temperature sensor to measure the temperature of your hand with and without the expedition suit prototype.

Consider these questions:

  • What do you notice?
  • Was your expedition suit successful?
  • How do you know?

Iterate on Your Expedition Suit

The climate in an area isn’t just the temperature. You also have to think about precipitation, humidity, and wind speed. Iterate, or improve, on your expedition suit by adding, changing, or removing parts of it. If the climate in the place you’re visiting includes a lot of rain, what changes would you make to your suit? What kinds of materials would you use?
