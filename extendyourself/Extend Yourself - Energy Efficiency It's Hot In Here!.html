Extend Yourself - Energy Efficiency:  Keep Our SmartLab Cool

 How Old?

Figure out how old the electronics are in your classroom. Look into how old the air conditioning unit is in the school building. Research more energy-efficient options that could lower the energy usage in your school.

How much would it cost to replace the air conditioner? Can you calculate how long it would take to pay for the new electronics?

 Sleep Over

To save resources and money, many schools turn off the heating or cooling at night after students leave. What happens to the temperature in your classroom after all the students and teachers have gone home? Set up an experiment using the temperature probe to record the temperature changes in your classroom overnight.

 It’s Hot in There!

Visit another classroom and take temperature readings of the same electronics in their room. Did you find the same devices giving off the most heat? If so, why might this be happening? If not, what might be the reasons to explain the differences? Share the data collected from both classrooms with the students in the other classroom. Can you brainstorm any new ideas or suggestions about how to save energy?

 Changing Places

Round your temperature data to the nearest tenth. Does this change your results? How? Is it enough of a change for you to rethink your suggestions for saving energy? Why or why not?
