Extend Yourself - Observing With NASA: Astronomy

Take Your Own Photo

You can take your own photo of an astronomical object using Observing with NASA.

How to request an image:

Step 1: Click on the Control Telescope button.

This web page shows you all the objects you can photograph with the Observing with NASA telescopes. Notice that it shows you which objects will be visible tonight from its telescopes (assuming it is not too cloudy).

Step 2: Click on the Observe button to take a picture of the moon.

Step 3: Make your Field of View selection, Exposure Time selection, and Make your Filter selection.

Step 4: Email your request and wait for your photo. NASA usually takes the photos at night when it is easier to view astronomical objects.

Step 5: Access your image. 

When you get your email, you can follow the Access your image link to get the photograph you took with the telescope.

Right-click on the image and select “Save picture as…” Then save the picture to your student folder. You can now complete your presentation.
