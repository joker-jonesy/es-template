Extend Yourself - Stellarium: Stars in the Distance

Create a Constellation

You have explored the 88 constellations used by astronomers today. But using Stellarium, anyone can trace out their unique constellations in the sky.

  1. Turn off all constellation labels, artwork, lines, and borders.
  2. Pick a starry area in Stellarium and take a screenshot. You will use stars in this area to "connect the dots" and make a new constellation.
    1. Make sure at least two stars have their name labels showing in the screenshot. This makes it easier to find that area of the sky again using the Search tool.
    2. If you plan to print your screenshot, go to the Tools tab in the Configuration window and check the "Invert colors" box under Screenshots. This will use less printer ink.
  3. Open the image in a photo-editing software program, or print out the screenshot to draw on it directly.
    1. What will your constellation represent? An animal? A person? An object?
    2. Does your constellation have its own background story?
  4. After drawing your constellation outline, go back to Stellarium and find the names of all of the stars you used. Add these names to your image.
  5. How far apart are your stars? Find the distance between a star and two others in a different constellation or different from the previously used stars. Compare the three numbers. Which is the greatest? Which is the smallest? Explain how much greater one place's value is than the others using what you have learned.

Example: Using the website, choose three different distances to compare. Within the constellation Aries, the following stars and distances exist: Hamal is 66 lightyears from Earth, Sheratin is 59.6 light-years from Earth (about 60 lightyears), and Boetin is 168 light-years from Earth. 

The greatest distance from Earth is Boetin because 168 light-years are much larger than 60 or 66. The closest to Earth is Sheratin because 60 is the smallest number of the three distances.

If we look at the distance in the light-years of Hamal, 66 has the same digit in two place values. The 6 in the tens place is ten times greater than the 6 in the ones place because 60 is ten times greater than 6. Look for these relationships in your research.
