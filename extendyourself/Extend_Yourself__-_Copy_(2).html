Chance and the Platonic Solids

For many years, people have used the Platonic solids in games of chance. Since all of the faces of the Platonic solids are the same, they make perfect dice with each face having equal chance of landing upward.

The most common die is the 6-sided cube, but others show up in modern role playing games and have been used throughout history.

Look at the different dice. There are only five platonic solids yet there are seven golden dice shown here.  How is this possible? Can you figure out which ones are not Platonic Solids? Are they still “fair”?
