 Hunting for Regular Tessellations

How many regular polygons can tessellate? A regular tessellation is made with only one regular polygon.

Regular polygons have sides and angles that are all the same. A square is a good example  Experiment with the different regular polygons shown here.

Hint: You can make all the regular tessellations with Pixie or Zometool, but you can only make one with K'NEX. Do you know why?

 Challenge Your Classmates

Challenge your classmates to create a tessellation following a set of rules. For example, you may ask a student to create a tessellation where 1/4 of the tiles are blue and 1/3 of the blue tiles are squares.

Be sure to include:

  • The number of polygons to use
  • The total number of shapes
  • Fractions of the total for each color
  • Directions to expand the tessellation
