Extend Yourself - Tello Drone: Programming with DroneBlocks

Use Your Drone's Camera

Take several photos with your drone and use software to “stitch” them together to produce a photo-realistic map of your route! How often might you need to update the map to ensure that it remains up to date? Would it stay the same in each season?

Or, try modeling the steps it would take to deliver food, water, and medical supplies to an area where a disaster occurred. Take a load of materials of minimum weight (you decide) to a specified location. Deliver it and fly the drone back to you. How can the drone's camera help you with this mission?

Check the Distance

Tello uses sensors, not GPS, for location. Measure the actual distance Tello flies and compare them to the value entered. Does the Tello travel exactly the distance it's programmed to travel? If not, how far off is it? The difference between what it is supposed to do and what it does is called a discrepancy. Collect data and compare the data to see whether these discrepancies are consistent, or the same over a number of identical attempts, or seem random. What do you think is causing the difference? Do some tests to figure it out.

Impact of Weight

Because the drone will be carrying an item or set of items, explore how the weight of an object might affect the flight. Can you add an object to your Tello and attempt the same flight? What did you notice about the speed? How can your Tello accommodate the weight of the object?

Detour Ahead

Have you ever been on your way to your destination and had to take a detour? Think about how you could create a detour on your map. Are you able to think of a different route that will get your drone to its destination? Which route is quicker? Test it out!
