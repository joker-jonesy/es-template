Extend Yourself - FabMaker Studio: Create Your Own Constellation

 Use Stellarium to Find Tonight's Sky

As the earth moves around the sun, we see different constellations at different times of the year. Use Stellarium to find a constellation, or multiple constellations, that you can see in tonight's sky. Create another paper luminary that will display the stars in tonight's sky.

 Research a Constellation

Use the Internet to research a constellation. What stars are part of the constellation? What shape does the outline make? What stories are connected to the constellation?

 Constellation Battle

Get a partner and each of you will draw one of the constellations from the “What You Should Know” section  on a coordinate plane on graph paper. Be sure that you do not see each other’s constellations. Mark the coordinate pair for each star in your constellation. Play a game similar to “Battleship.”

Click here for instructions for playing paper Battleship. Take turns guessing the locations of the stars in each other’s constellations. Mark off, with a pencil, each coordinate pair that your partner guesses for your constellation. The first person to guess all of the coordinate pairs or the name of the constellation wins.
