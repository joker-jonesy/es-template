Extend Yourself - Vernier Elementary Sensing Science Collection: Lever Up! 

Make it a Story 

Write a story about someone using a lever to pick up something very heavy.

In the story: 

  • Explain how the lever works.
  • Add what class lever it is.

Simple Machines Around Us 

We are surrounded by simple machines: levers, wedge, wheel and axle, pulley, inclined plane, and screws. Take a walk around school, home, or the neighborhood with a clipboard and camera. Make a tally chart. For each simple machine, mark it in the correct category and then take a picture of it. After your walk, count up all of your tally marks in each category. Make a bar graph showing the data you have collected. Think about these questions;

  • What do you notice about the total number of simple machines that surround us? Which are the most common? Why do you think that is?
  • What if there were no simple machines? Which tasks would be the hardest to do? Easiest?
