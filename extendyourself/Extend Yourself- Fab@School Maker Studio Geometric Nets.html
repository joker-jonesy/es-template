 Calculate Surface Area

Calculate the surface area of your shape. If you know how to find the surface area for a 2D shape, start there!

 Make Another Model

Find a 3D object in your classroom, school, or community and design a geometric net for it. Look in your classroom, on the playground, or even at a building in your community. See if you can make a model in the FabMaker Studio.

 Play with Volume

Make two more of the same shape you created, but instead of keeping the dimensions the same, make one that is the same shape but a smaller volume, and one that is the same shape but a larger volume. What parts of the 3D shape changed? What stayed the same? Calculate the volumes of your new shapes.

 Calculate the Volume of Common Objects

Calculate the volume of different shapes, such as a model of a house. Make the geometric net of a house using FabMaker Studio, construct the house, and then calculate its volume.

Imagine this house separated into two 3D shapes. The bottom part of the house is a rectangular prism. The top part of the house is a triangular prism. You will need to find the volume of each part of the house and then add it together to get the total volume. Remember, to find the volume of a rectangular prism, multiply width × height × length. To find the volume of a triangular prism, multiply ½ × base (of the triangle) × height (of the triangle) × depth (of the prism).
