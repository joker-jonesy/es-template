Do It! Vernier Science: Measure Motion

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to explore different topics regarding the sounds of your own motion, echolocation, and simple harmonic motion using a Vernier motion sensor. You will conduct experiments that use sound to measure moving objects. Then, you will solve division problems based on scenarios related to the experiments. Finally, you will create a presentation to share your findings.

 Getting Started

Using the Go! Motion Sensor

These experiments use the Vernier Go! Motion sensor.

If you have not used this sensor before, download the Practice Activity.

Go! Motion Experiments

Use the Vernier Go! Motion sensor to conduct some experiments with echolocation. Start with the experiment that interests you the most. Answer each of the questions on the Vernier activity sheets. 

Even if you do not have time to complete both of the experiments, complete all of the project steps that end with “Scenario” since these are math challenges that can be done even if you did not do the related experiment.

E-Motion!

Learn how to make different-shaped graphs with your own motion.

Download E-Motion!

E-Motion Scenario

Imagine a person walking back and forth, trying to make graphical images using her motion the way you did in the e-Motion! challenge. She takes 160 steps in 10 minutes. How many steps does she take in one minute?

  • What numbers do you need to divide in order to calculate the answer?
  • Show how to solve this using an equation, area model, or rectangular array. Choose two of the three methods to illustrate.
  • Be sure to include the units of measurement in your final answer.

Batty About Science Scenario

Imagine a bat is following an insect. It flies 14 meters  in one minute. Later, the same insect flies at the same speed for 266 meters. How long does it fly this time?  What numbers do you need to divide in order to calculate the answer?

  • Show how to solve this using an equation, area model, or rectangular array. Choose two of the three methods to illustrate.
  • Be sure to include the units of measurement in your final answer.

Presentation

Use a presentation tool like PowerPoint or Google Slides to create a presentation demonstrating what you learned about motion and echolocation measurements.

Be sure to include:

  • Screenshots of your work in Logger Lite
  • Your data, graphs, and other experimental results
  • Photos or movies of your experiment(s) in action
  • Your observations during the experiment(s)
  • What you learned from the experiment(s)

 

What new questions do you have about motion or echolocation?

Think about how you could change one of the above experiments to answer some of your questions, or how you might create your own experiment from scratch.
