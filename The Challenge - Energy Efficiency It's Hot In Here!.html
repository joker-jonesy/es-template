The Challenge - Energy Efficiency: Keep Our SmartLab Cool

The Challenge

Your challenge is to measure how much heat is produced by the electronics in your classroom. Electronics create heat and must be cooled down to prevent damage from overheating. Use temperature sensors to measure and record the temperatures to the nearest hundredth. Compare the temperatures to determine which electronic gives off the most heat. Develop ideas to help lower the temperature of your classroom environment and reduce future demand for energy to cool your classroom. Create a presentation to share with your principal. 

How can you save energy and reduce the demand for air conditioning in your classroom? 

What I'll Learn 

  • I CAN use temperature sensors to measure temperature to the nearest hundredth.
  • I CAN compare decimals to the nearest hundredth.
  • I CAN develop ideas to help solve a problem.
  • I CAN create a presentation to share my data and ideas with others.

Stuff I'll Need

  • Vernier Surface Thermometer 
  • Logger Lite Software, Logger Pro Software, or Graphical Analysis Software
    • Mac: in your dock
    • PC: All Applications folder

  • Presentation Tool
