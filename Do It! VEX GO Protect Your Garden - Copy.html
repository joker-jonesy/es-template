Do It! VEX GO: Clean Up Your Community

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to build a robot and program it to collect as much magnetic metal as possible. There is a shortage of magnetic metal in your city and the mayor has asked you to be on the lookout. The metal you collect will be given to the city to be reused in building a community project.

Click on the link below to get to the VEXcode GO web based programming and start your project. This is what you will use if you are on a computer or an iPad.

Firmware Update

Updating firmware is very important because it helps keep everything running smoothly. When using an iPad, VEX Go will automatically update when the brain is connected to VEX CODE GO. The brain will blink green and red while it’s updating. It will return to green when it’s finished. If you are using a Chromebook or Chrome browser you will need to update on an iPad with VEX Classroom for the first time. After that, you will select the GO Brain. The GO Brain that needs an update will be shown in red. Click update and wait for the blinking red and green light to remain green.

Firmware Update Instructions

Connect the Brain to VEXcode GO

Make sure the VEX GO Brain is connected to the VEX GO battery. Turn on the Brain by pushing the button in the middle. Click on the drop down Brain button and click connect. Select the name of the VEX GO Brain you want to pair and click pair. Make sure your brain is flashing green. Once the Brain is connected the brain icon will turn green.

  2. Test Metal

  3. Plan Your Program

  4. Program Your Robot
  5. Collect Metal
  6. Record Your “Metal Collector”

Build Your Robot

Start by building your robot. How will you collect your metal?

Here are a few ideas to help you get started building the base of your metal collector.

Claw Instructions

Magnet Car instructions

Want a challenge? Incorporate the electromagnet into your build. Think about how you can use the electromagnet to collect metal.

Test Metal

Use your north and south magnet to test out all of the metal you are collecting. Consider these questions:

  • What happens when you use the magnets to pick up your metal pieces?
  • Do the magnets pick up all types of metal?
  • How does that affect your project?

Plan Your Program

Remember to decompose your problem. How can decomposing your problem help you? How can you break down your program into smaller pieces?

You can find more information about decomposing problems in the “What You Should Know” section.

Program Your Robot

Choose how you will program your robot to collect the metal. Depending on what type of collector you build, this may look different. For example, if you build a claw, you will probably have more code written than if you build a truck. Remember to break your algorithm into smaller parts then add them together.

Collect Metal

How will you collect your metal? Did you incorporate the magnets into your robot or did you test to see if they were magnetic before and separate them into piles? This may determine how you collect your metal.

Record Your “Metal Collector”

Record your “metal collector” collecting magnetic metal for your project submission. Don’t forget to include:

  1. The metal being collected
  2. The programming on your screen
  3. An explanation of what your robot is doing
