Photos: Basic Photo Editing with Preview (For Mac)

Your Challenge

It is important to have photos in your ePortfolio. You can use a program called Preview to crop and edit your photos on a Mac. 

What You'll Learn

  • Students will learn about basic photo editing for Mac devices.

Stuff You'll Need

  • Preview software program
  • Pictures (from your camera or free use images from the internet)

What You Should Know

What Can Preview Do?

With Preview on Mac, you can...

  • Crop
  • Rotate
  • Change how the picture looks (like its colors and brightness)
  • Draw on the picture

Complete Instructions

You can find basic instructions below. For the complete official instructions for Preview, go to the Apple Support Site.

Basic Skill Overview

Open an Image

You may want to take your own photos. Or, you may use online images for your project or ePortfolio.

TO OPEN: Find your image in your folder. Left double-click on your image; the image should automatically open in Preview. If not, right-click the image and select "Open With... Preview".

  

Edit an Image 

To EDIT: Click on the Markup button, which is at the top of the toolbar. This will open more tools below.

To CROP:

  1. Choose the Rectangular Selection from the Selection Tools dropdown menu in the toolbar.
  2. Click and drag the corners of the crop outline.
  3. When you are like how it looks, click the "Crop" button on the right side of the toolbar.

 

TO RESIZE: Click on the Resize button, and fill in your different sizes. Then, click OK.

TO ROTATE: Click on the Rotate button located in the toolbar.

TO UNDO A CHANGE: Click Command + Z buttons on your keyboard. If you want to make many changes, keep typing this command. 

If you want to go back to how the image looked when you last saved it, exit Preview. When you close out, you will see a popup window. In the popup window, click "Revert Changes".

Color Adjustment Tool

For quick and easy color changes, select the Color Adjustment button  .

With the Color Adjustment Tool, you can adjust the following:

  • Exposure: the amount of light hitting the image
  • Contrast: the difference between dark and light colors
  • Highlights: major light parts of an image
  • Shadows: major dark parts of an image
  • Saturation: intensity of colors
  • Temperature: warmness or coolness of the image
    • For example, red is considered a warmer color, while blue is considered a cooler color.
  • Tint: how much white is in the image
  • Sepia: reddish-brown filter
  • Sharpness: how clear the image appears

Add Text and Shapes to an Image

Text

  1. Click on the Text button to create a text box.
  2. Type in the text you would like to add. 
  3. Click on the Text Style button .
    • You can use this popup window to adjust the font style, color, size, and alignment.
    • You can also make it bold, italicized, or underlined.

 Shapes

  1. Click on the Shapes button .
  2. Once you have added a shape, you can change.
    • The width of the shape’s outline
    • The color of the shape’s outline
    • The fill color of the shape

Draw on an Image

Drawing on an image can help highlight important areas of the picture.

  1. Select Draw button from the toolbar.
  2. If you click on the Line button, you can choose the line style. For example, you can use a clean line, or a sketchy pencil-like line.
  3. If you click on the Color button, you can choose your color.
  4. Draw on your image.
