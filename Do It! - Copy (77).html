Do It!

Your challenge is to explore your favorite constellation and see what other amazing objects can be found within its borders.

Helpful Tools

Watch the video to learn about some Stellarium tools that will help you in this activity.

Constellation Exploration

Explore the different constellations in the Stellarium sky.

  • Turn off the atmosphere and the ground.
  • Turn on the Deep-sky objects. 
  • Turn on the Constellation labels from the constellations buttons. 
  • Turn on Constellation lines and optional artwork. 
  • Go to the Starlore tab in the Sky and Viewing Options window. Check the "Show boundaries" box.

Which constellations interest you the most? Choose your favorite constellation to explore in more detail. 

  1. Look around your constellation and look for unique or interesting objects.
    • Which stars are part of the constellation?
       NOTE: Some stars have nicknames shown next to each star. If not, other names also show up in the top left corner of the screen when the object is selected. If there is no nickname, use the name starting with "HIP" when you talk or write about it. 
      • What other bright stars, nebulae, galaxies, or other cool objects can you find within its boundary lines?
      • Take screenshots of your constellation and some of your objects.
        • Windows Computer: Ctrl + S
        • Mac Computer: Command + S
        • The screenshots will automatically save to your desktop, unless you've changed that setting in Stellarium.
  2. Go online and look up some interesting facts about your constellation.
    • What is the constellation's history?
    • Are there any stories or myths written about it?

Constellation Presentation

Organize the information about your constellation and its objects. Create a presentation summarizing what you learned.

Include:

  • Screenshots
  • Facts and stories you researched
  • Your own sketch of the constellation, done by hand or on the computer
    (Draw its main stars, connecting lines, and any other details and labels you think are important.)
