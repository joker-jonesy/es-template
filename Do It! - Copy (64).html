Do It!

Your challenge is to build the Hungry Alligator robot and program it to open and close its mouth. Once you do that, change the program so the alligator learns a new trick.

Steps:

  1. Build the Hungry Alligator robot.
  2. Connect the sensors to the computer and program your Hungry Alligator robot.
  3. Program your Hungry Alligator to perform a new behavior.

If you have questions about gears, pulleys, or troubleshooting, be sure to explore the What You Should Know section.

Getting Started with WeDo

1. Open WeDo under “All Programs”.

2. Click the activity tab in the top left corner of your screen.

3. Click the Lego face in the top left corner.

4. Click the WeDo Set on the left.

Build Your Hungry Alligator

To build your Hungry Alligator, use the Lego bricks in your SmartLab and the instructions in the WeDo software. You can download a PDF version here. 

Once built, connect the sensor to the computer and test your Hungry Alligator. 

 

Test Your Alligator

It is important to understand the behavior of your robot. 

  • Test your alligator.
  • Record the behavior of your robot throughout the process.
  • Take photographs or videos for your ePortfolio.

Think about what you want your alligator to do. How do the pulleys and gears help to move your Hungry Alligator robot? Can you identify the gears used in this robot and why you used that gear to perform a job? 

Teach Your Alligator New Tricks

Now try this! Rebuild or re-program your alligator to perform a new behavior. What will your alligator do? 

Ideas for Re-Programming Your Alligator

  • Change the speed that the alligator opens and closes its mouth.
  • Get the alligator to keep its mouth open longer before closing it.
  • Have the alligator open and close its mouth a specific number of times.
  • Change the sound that the alligator makes.
  • Record your own sound for the alligator and use it.
  • Change both the movement of the mouth and the sound to create a completely different behavior.
  • Change the robot design so that it can do other things like move back and forth while chomping.

Keep a record of the changes you make to your alligator. Record your design and your program.
