What is Google Slides?

Google Slides is a free online website to create slideshows for presentations. Google Slides allows its users to edit and work with others on the same file at the same time.

This website allows users to create and design their own slides. Users can choose everything from the text, images, and background. It also lets users choose when certain objects on the page show up and when the next slide appears.

How Do You Use Google Slides?

Home Screen

When you pull up Google Slides, you will see a window like the one below. There are a few features that cover the basics for most presentations that are created.

  • On the left, you will see a “+” icon above the Overview column. This is where you add more slides to your presentation. Slides will be added to the bottom. If you want to move them, click and drag that slide in the overview to wherever you want it. To delete a slide, click the slide so it is highlighted yellow, and then press the Delete button on your keyboard.
  • On the toolbar, you will see a box with a “T” inside of it. This is for text boxes. When you click it and then click within your slide, Google Slides displays a box where you can type any text you want. You can also change the position of the text box.
  • Next, on the toolbar is an image icon. This is where you add images, either from the web or your computer, to your presentation.
  • The Background and Theme buttons are ways to change the background design on one slide or choose a design theme for the entire presentation.

Layouts

When adding more slides, you have the option to choose a premade layout. If you want a premade layout, you can click the drop-down arrow next to the + icon, or you can click the Layout button and choose a layout. To edit each premade text box, click inside the text box and edit the text. If you do not want a premade layout, you can select Blank and create your own.

Delete Textbox

Whether you use a premade layout or you create your own, you may make a text box, image, shape, or design that you might not like anymore. To delete it, click the outside border of the object. Make sure the border is highlighted with a straight blue line. Then, you can press Delete or Backspace on your keyboard to delete the object.

Making an Image a Shape

Imagine you have an image you want to use in your presentation. You may not always want it in the square shape.

Here are the steps to turn your image into any of the shapes provided in Google Slides:

  1. Have an image that you want to use already downloaded to the computer and saved to where you can find it.
  2. Insert the image onto the slide that you want it on.
  3. While the image is still selected (has the blue lines and squares around it), click the drop-down arrow next to the Crop Image button to find Mask Image. If your image gets unselected, click the image to select it again.
  4. The drop-down menu includes different shapes, arrows, callouts, and equations. Select any of them. Then, click your final shape.
  5. Your image should now be in the shape that you choose. You can resize the image by dragging any of the blue squares inward or outward. You can also move the image by selecting the image and dragging it to where you want in the slide.

Insert and Modify Shapes

  1. Go to the Insert button and click Shape.
  2. Choose the category of shape that you want: Shapes, arrows, callouts, or equations.
  3. Select the shape you want.
  4. Click and drag on the slide to create the shape.
  5. To modify a shape, follow these steps:
  • Select the shape you want to modify.
  • Click and drag the shape to move it.
  • Click and drag the squares on the sides and corners to resize, stretch, or shrink the shape.
  • Click and drag the circle attached to the shape to rotate it. 

Copy a Slide

If you want to make a slide that is very similar to a slide you already have, it may be easiest to copy the slide and then make the small changes. 

  1. Click the Slide button.
  2. Select Duplicate Slide.
  3. If you need to change the order of the slides, click on the slide thumbnail in the left-hand column and drag it where it needs to be.

Insert Fraction Equations

If you have the ability to install add-ons, then you may want to search for one that lets you type mathematical equations into Google Slides. If not, you can always use the symbols on your keyboard.  

You can find the plus, minus, and equals symbols to the right of 0 on the main section of your keyboard. Most keyboards also have these symbols included on a number pad on the right side of the keyboard.  

When it comes to fractions, you are probably most used to seeing them written with the numerator on top and the denominator on the bottom, like this:  34

However, fractions can also be written like this: 3/4 

In both cases, the 3 is the numerator and the 4 is the denominator. The forward slash (/) can be found on the same key as the question mark (?) and also above the 8 on the number pad to the right. 

Explore On Your Own

Practice using Google Slides by creating a short presentation about your favorite season. Include the following information in your presentation:

  • 1 cover slide that has your name
  • 3 or more slides that answer:
    • What is your favorite season?
    • Why do you like the season you picked?  
    • What activities do you like to do during that season?
  • Add images, backgrounds, and details to make the presentation uniquely yours
