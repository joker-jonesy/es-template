Do It!

Your challenge is to create your own animation with Scratch. Maybe you have used Scratch before. If not, follow the steps of the Getting Started tutorial before you start.

If you are new to Scratch, read What Is Scratch? in the What You Should Know section. If you are new to programming in general, read the entire What You Should Know section.

Getting Started Tutorial

If you are new to Scratch, or you want a refresher, complete this tutorial.

Do you have a Scratch account? Before you begin, ask your Facilitator if you need an account. If you need to save so you can work on your project later you will need an account.  

Go to the Scratch website Tips page. Click on the "Try it!" button.

Hint: You will need to have Flash enabled to use the link.

Click the Play button to complete the first step.

When you finish a step, click the right arrow to move on. Complete each step.

Create Your Own Animation 

Do you want to change an example animation or create your own?

To find example animations, check out the Animation Starter Projects.

If you are creating your own animation, write down your ideas. You could even draw your ideas.

Choose your favorite idea and think of the different animation steps that you will need to code. 

What sprite(s) will you use? What will they do? 

Share your animation with your classmates!
