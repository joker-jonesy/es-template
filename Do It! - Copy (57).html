Solar Power

Your challenge is to design, build, and test electrical circuits that use the solar cell. 

Can you even power lights, motors, and other resources when your machine is not collecting the rays of the sun?

To succeed in your challenge, you probably want to complete some introductory projects first. These experiences will give you the skills and experience needed to build and teach others about your final challenge projects.

For all of your projects, remember to:

  • Record your work
  • Take photographs
  • Label images
  • Write about what you did and what happened 

No Sun? No Problem! Try shining a lamp on your solar cell.

Introductory Projects

These introductory projects will give you the experience and knowledge needed to succeed in your challenge. You will experiment with the best angle for your light source, how different circuits generate different amounts of voltage, and what this means for the operation of devices.

Snap Circuits Green Energy Instruction Manual

  • #3 Best Charging Circuits - What is the best angle for your light source? 
  • #4 Solar Power - Why does the voltage drop when you connect the yellow LED?
  • #5 Solar Motor - In circuit #4, replace the yellow LED (D5) with the motor (M4, facing either direction) and place the wind fan on it. Now press the switch. How does the voltage changes as the solar cell runs the fan?

 

Advanced Projects

Now you are ready for your final challenge. Complete at least two advanced projects or try creating your own circuit design and running it with your solar cell.

Are you able to make your project work even when the solar cell is not collecting solar power? See the section on Stored Energy in What You Should Know.

LED Tip

LED bulbs only light up when electricity flows through the circuit in the right direction. Carefully build each circuit following the directions. If the LED does not light up try turning it around. Use the + sign to help you place your LED correctly.
