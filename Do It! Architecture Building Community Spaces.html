Do It! Arckit: Design and Measure Community Spaces

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to design a rectangular community space and build a model using the Arckit GO Colours 2.0 building set. You will need to take into consideration the purpose of the building and needs of the community when making your design.

Once you construct your model, imagine that your clients approve and ask you to build a larger model that they can put on display. You will need to measure the length and width of your model, calculate the measurements of this larger scale model, and write a multiplicative comparison statement to explain the relationship between the two. You will also express your multiplicative comparison as a multiplication equation and include all of this in a Google Slide displaying an image of your model.

 Project Steps

  1. Collect Information

  2. Design your Community Space

  3. Build your Community Space

  4. Upload to Google Slides

Collect Information

Imagine your clients and their needs. Before designing your community space, you should collect information about them.

  • Who will use the space? What are their specific needs?
  • What is the space going to be used for?
  • Where will it be built? How much space is available for the building?
  • How big does the space need to be for people to use it correctly?

Design Your Community Space

With the information you gathered, sketch the community space you want to build. The one design requirement is that the building’s footprint must be rectangular so you can measure and scale the dimensions later.

Many architects begin by sketching the basic layout of the walls and door openings.

You may want to use the Arckit building pieces to experiment with the building’s layout as you design.

Remember to think about what the community needs. Don't forget about any constraints or limits you may have, like how much money you can spend, how much space you have to build, the kind of materials you can use, etc.

Build Your Community Space

Using the Arckit GO Colours 2.0 pieces, build the community space you've designed.

  1. Begin by setting up the floor pieces.
  2. Use the yellow and clear wall, window, and door pieces to continue building up.
  3. Once the walls are in place, add an upper floor and/or the roofing.
  4. Use the tile pieces and texture sheets to give your model a professional look.
  5. Need more texture sheets? Go to the Architectexture page on Arckit's website. You can print, cut, and attach these texture sheets to your building to make it look like grass, stone, brick, tile, and wallpaper for the floors, walls, and roof of your model.
  6. Once you have completed your model, take a photograph.
  7. Next, measure your model in inches. You will only be measuring the footprint, or floor, of your model.
  8. Finally, decide on how much larger your display model should be, and solve for its measurements. Will you increase the size by a factor of 5? 8? 12? The factor you choose can be any number greater than 5 but less than 20.

Upload to Google Slides

Upload your work to Google Slides. Follow these steps:

  1. Open Google Slides.
  2. Select “new.”
  3. On the first slide, include the following:
  • Photo of your model
  • Scales as single row tables (tape diagrams) for both the length and the width of your design
  • Place the measurement of your design in the table.
  • Connect as many squares as needed to show the scale, or how many times larger you want the design.
  • The scale you choose can be any number  greater than 5 but less than 20.
  • Captions describing each tape diagram in words and as multiplication equations.
  • A textbox to show your math and the final dimensions.
  • Length and width of your small Arckit model footprint
  • Multiplicative comparison statement representing the relationship between the small model and the larger display model
  • Multiplicative equation representing the multiplicative comparison statement
