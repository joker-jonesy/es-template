Do It!

Your Challenge is to determine the wattage and lumen level of the bulbs in your classroom using a light sensor and decide if the bulbs maximize energy efficiency and support student learning. Then, create a presentation to share what you learned. 

Using the Light Probe

The activity in this Liftoff Challenge uses the Vernier Light Probe sensor. If you have not use the sensor before, start by practicing with the Learning to Use a Light Probe.

Explore Your Classroom

What bulbs are in your classroom? Make a list of the different kinds of bulbs. Do you think the bulbs are halogen, incandescent, fluorescent or light emitting diodes? Can you safely figure out their wattage or volts? Estimate how much the lights are used each day. Use the lumen table to help you figure out what would be the best light strength for your learning space. Do you think you have the best light bulbs for your classroom?

Measure the Lumens

Measure the light output in lumens for the different light bulbs you find around your classroom using the Vernier Light Sensor. Be sure to point to and measure as many bulbs as possible. Do you see any similarities or differences between the bulbs in the room?

Record your information.

Presentation

Decide if your classroom has the best bulbs to maximize student learning and energy efficiency.

Use a presentation tool like PowerPoint or Google Slides to create a presentation demonstrating what you learned about light bulbs, lumens, and watts.

Be sure to include:

  • Screenshots of your work 
  • Your data, graphs, and other experimental results
  • What you learned from the experiment 
  • Any recommendations you have for changing bulbs to increase efficiency and better light your classroom
