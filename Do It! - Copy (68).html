Do It!

Your challenge is to build and program a safe ride with Lego WeDo and explain how it works.

Steps to Complete Your Challenge:

  1. Make sure you have the Lego WeDo Add-on set configured.
  2. Use your Lego bricks to build your amusement park ride - the Ferris wheel or the carousel.
  3. Program your ride. 
  4. Answer the reflection question below and make the changes listed.
  5. Explain how your ride works. Review the What You Should Know section to help you to understand the concepts needed to explain how your amusement park ride works.

Getting Started with WeDo

  • Open WeDo under “All Programs”.

  • Click the activity tab in the top left corner of your screen.

  • Click the Lego face in the top left corner.

Build Your Amusement Park Ride

Choose either the Ferris Wheel or the Carousel.

To figure out the physics behind YOUR amusement park ride, start by building it, then programming it, testing it, and observing it in action.

Program Your Ride

Drag buttons from the bottom of the screen to the middle of the screen.

To test your machine, connect it to the computer and press the play button.

Reflect

Now that your ride is built, programmed, and tested, let’s run some special tests to figure out the physics.

  1. What is the velocity of the ride when using the program suggested by Lego? (If there is more than one speed setting, you can choose to measure one or both.)
  2. Adjust your ride so your riders can stay seated without getting snapped in. How fast can your ride go so the riders do not fly off?
  3. If a rider were to fly off, where do you think it would go? Depending on how you modified your ride, you may be able to test and see. Be sure to note the position of the rider on the ride, and the direction he or she flies.
  4. What else can you figure out about the physics of your ride?

Tip on Measuring Velocity

Remember, since velocity is the speed something moves in a particular direction, you need to include the direction and speed.

There are different ways to figure this out.

1. Measure rotations per minute.

2. Measure seconds per rotation.

Do not forget to include the direction the ride is turning -  clockwise or counter-clockwise.
