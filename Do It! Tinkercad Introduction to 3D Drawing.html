Do It! Tinkercad: 3D Design to Size

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to create your own 3D design with Tinkercad. It can be anything approved by your facilitator, but needs to include a rectangular prism in the design. At the same time, you will explore how scale factor affects the size of an object, and you will use powers of ten to convert metric measurements to enlarge or shrink. Then, you could print your design on a 3D printer.

 Project Steps

  1. Complete the 7 Direct Starters

  2. Explore

  3. Free Draw

  4. Convert Units of Measure

Complete the 7 Direct Starters

Start by doing the 7 Direct Starters activities. Once you complete them all, design your own 3D object. If you have a 3D printer, print the object you design.

To Complete the Direct Starters:

Select each item to learn more.

1. Sign in to Tinkercad.com.

2. Click on Learn.

3. Complete the Direct Starters to learn the basics of Tinkercad.

Explore

Once you learn the basics, take 15 minutes to explore on your own.

Try to create a new simple design.

Try looking at your objects from different sides.

Free Draw

When you are done exploring, design your own Tinkercad object that includes a rectangular prism in the design. Here are some ideas:

Write down the measurements of the rectangular prism part of your design. Remember to include length, width and height. Include these measurements in your Scale Factor Table. Take a screenshot of your original design. 

Convert Units of Measure

Now practice the skills needed to create two more of your 3D objects in different sizes: one larger than the original, and the other smaller. To keep things simple, you will practice these skills using the measurements of the rectangular prism portion of your design. To do so, apply a scale factor to each of the rectangular prism measurements you recorded in the table. Record the scale factor you used and apply it to the original measurements, recording your calculations and resulting new measurements in the appropriate column. A partial example has been done for you.

Plug the new calculated measurements into your Tinkercad designs. Take screenshots of your larger and smaller objects for your Final Project Submission.
