 Human Generated Energy

Explore these amazing gadgets that use humans to generate, or create, energy.

Choose a gadget to build and try out. After you build it, try to answer these questions:

  • How can you measure the amount of energy that is generated using the device?
  • What units will you use?
  • How long does it take to generate that much energy?

You may find the number in the article, or you may need to do additional research. For example, you could search “how much energy does human sweat generate?”

To describe the amount of energy, you will need to include how much time it takes to generate that much energy. For example, according to the website, the average human generates 100 watts of power a day. But what if you only have half a day to generate energy?

To find the amount of energy generated in half a day, you would divide 100 watts in half, or by 2.

100 \ 2 = 50

50 watts can be generated in half a day by a human at rest.

Dividing by 2 was easy to do because 100 is a composite number. 2 is one of its factors, so it’s easy to divide by 2.
