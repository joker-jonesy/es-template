Do It! Scratch: Program to Solve Word Problems

 The Challenge

Your challenge is to use Scratch to program a computer animation that teaches math. You can add to an existing program or plan and create your own animation "from scratch." You could have one character present a multiplication or division word problem and the other could solve it. Or, your characters could act out a word problem. Or perhaps you have another idea of how you could make an animation about math.

Plan Your Animation 

Now that you know how Scratch works, plan out your animation. 

  • Are you going to make an animation from “scratch”? Or will you go to the Explore page to find an animation that someone else has made and make changes to the code?  
  • How many characters do you want? 
  • What will they say? 
  • What will they do? 
  • Do you want the viewers to be able to interact with the animation? (For example, do you want them to be able to click on characters to make them talk?)
  • How do you plan to include multiplication or division? If you are unsure, look over your Plan and SMART Goal. 

You may want your characters to do something you have not learned yet. If so, you could always try another tutorial to learn those skills. Take the lesson a step further by making changes to the code.

Remember to keep your animation simple. Once you get the basics to work correctly, you can always add to it later.

Learn How to Use Green Operator Blocks to Include Math

Watch this tutorial  to help you use green Operator blocks in your script.

  • Watch from 1:00 to 1:37. The instructor uses an Operator block to tell his sprite how many steps to take. How can you use the multiplication block to do something similar?
  • From 1:38 to 2:00, the instructor gives examples of code that can be changed using the green Operator blocks. How can you use the multiplication or division Operator  blocks to change your sprite's other movements? How can you use the blocks to change how your sprite looks?
  • In 2:07 through 2:29, you learn how to make your sprite say the answer to math problems. Is there anything you would like your sprite to say that can be coded using the green Operator blocks? 

    • For example, a block that says "12/2" has been inserted into a character speaking code. The sprite then gives the answer: "6."

    • The instructor shows from 2:35 to 3:00 how inputting numbers in the multiplication and division green Operator blocks solves multiplication and division problems. How could you use this skill in your animation?

    Choose Your Math Problem

    This is your opportunity to design your own Scratch game that uses math. 

    Will one character share a word problem and another answer it? Will your characters act out a word problem while someone else narrates? Will one character teach another how to figure out an unknown whole number in a multiplication or division equation? Do you have another idea you would like to try?

    Once you decide on the math problem you want to animate, write down the problem and solve for the answer so you can program your animation with the correct numbers.

    Share Your Math Animation

    Follow the steps below to share your Scratch math animation with others:

    • Click on your username  in the upper right corner of the Scratch website 
    • Click on "MyStuff" in the dropdown menu
    • Click on the title of your game.
    • Click on the "Share" button towards the top right.
    • Fill out the "Instructions" field to tell people how to start your game (for example, what they should click on first).
    • Fill out the "Notes and Credits" section by typing one or two sentences that describe your game. Add another sentence to say who created the game (You!). In your description, explain how math is used in your program.
