Do It!

Create an Account 

Log into Tinkercad. If you do not already have an account, click the join now button and set up an email and password for your account. Create a login and password you will remember, this will be necessary to save your work and share your designs with others.

Tinker Tutorials

Tinkercad Circuits has tutorials you can use to help you start your circuit design. The tutorials are made to help you find tools to build your circuits and practice testing the circuits with the "Start Simulation" button. Work your way through the simulations. Each simulation will give you important directions and information on the left hand side of the screen. 

If you still need help designing a circuit, start with a pre-built circuit. You will find a bunch of different pre-built circuits under "Starters." You can practice copying these pre-built circuits or observing how the circuit works by pushing the "Start Simulation" button. 

Build and Test Your Circuit

Now it's time to design and build your own circuits! Use the "Start Simulation" button to test your circuit. If the resistors in your circuit such as the light or motor do not work during the simulation, try moving components around or refer to the What You Should Know section for some important background information on how circuits work. 

Start by building a basic complete circuit that includes a power source, a load or resistor, and something conductive connecting everything like copper wire. Then try to build a more complicated circuit or add additional components like multiple LED lights or a LED and motor. 

   

Tinkercad will automatically save your creations and when you log in you will be able to see all the circuits you have built. Tinkercad Circuits will also automatically name your circuit project. If you would like to give it your own name, just click on the name in the top left corner and change it.
