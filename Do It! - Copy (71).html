Do It!

Your challenge is to beat all three Ozobot Triple Crown Challenges.

Train for Speed Racing Success

Training is key to success in any race. Let’s do some training before trying the Triple Crown Challenge.

Design a maze-like path and create the code to guide Ozobot to the end.

STEPS:

1. Draw a path in pencil. Mark the start and finish.

2. Add at least one four-way intersection. 

3. Use each of the following OzoCodes at least once to guide Ozobot from the start to the finish:

Go Left       Go Right        Go straight     U-Turn (Line End)

4. Use markers to color in your path and your OzoCodes.

5. Place Ozobot at the start and run your program. See if Ozobot makes it to the finish. Test your path at least twice.

Training Exercise: Reflections

1. Did Ozobot go the way you thought it would? Why or why not?

2. What happens when Ozobot gets to an intersection and has no code to tell it which way to go?

3. How can you make sure that Ozobot uses the exact path you had in mind?

4. Review the Ozobot Tips.  Did you learn any of these tips through your own experimentation?

Record your work

  • Write down your reflections.
  • Take notes on lessons learned so they can help you with your Triple Crown Challenge.
  • Take a photo or video of Ozobot following your path.

Ready for the Triple Crown Challenge?

Challenge 1: Fun Run

1) Fun Run

Design a fun path. Then see if you can make it fast too.

2) Speed Test

See how fast you can get to the finish.

3) Time Trial

How many commands can Ozobot do in 60 seconds?

STEP 1: Design a path that would be FUN for you to follow. You can use different color lines but do not use OzoCodes.

Done? Great! Now make sure you have answers to the following:

1. What makes this path fun?

2. Time Ozobot as it follows the path. Complete three trials and record the times. Record the average of the times as well.

You can use this digital stopwatch or ask your facilitator for a stopwatch.

3. How much distance did Ozobot travel as it followed your path?

4. Watch Ozobot follow the path one more time and record your observations.

To observe is to pay attention and notice. So an observation is something you notice.

STEP 2: How could you change the path so Ozobot finishes faster? Come up with a hypothesis. Your hypothesis should include ideas for changing the path. Estimate how much time you could cut with each idea.

RULES: Your path still needs to be FUN for the same reasons that your first design was fun.

This time, you can use Ozocodes.

If you are done, that is awesome! Now answer these questions:

1. How much faster was Ozobot once you made your changes?

2. Were your estimates accurate? Why or why not?

3. Were there any changes made that did not help Ozobot finish faster?

4. What did you learn from this experience that could help you when designing other Ozobot paths? 

Challenge 2: Speed Test

See how fast you can get to the finish.

STEPS:

1. Draw a path in pencil that goes all across your paper (the long way). The path does not need to be straight.

2. Use three of the following OzoCodes at least once to guide Ozobot from the start to the finish. You can also use other codes that are not on this list.

Slow        Pause (3 sec.)        Turbo        Spin

3. Use markers to color in your path and your OzoCodes.

4. Time Ozobot as it follows the path. You can use this digital stopwatch or ask your facilitator for a stopwatch.

5. Follow steps 1-4 to create two more paths. See if you can design a faster path each time. 

Speed Test Reflections

1. What was your original plan for designing the fastest path, while following the rules in step 2?

2. Which OzoCodes did you use and why?

3.Were you able to make changes to your path that got Ozobot to the end faster?

4. Talk to other groups. Which group had the fastest path? How was that group’s fastest path similar or different from your fastest path?

Record your work

  • Write down your reflections.
  • Take a photo or video of your paths, and add comments to explain your changes and ideas.

How many commands can Ozobot do in 60 seconds?

Challenge 3: Time Trial

STEPS:

1. Using your observations from the other challenges, try to estimate the number of codes Ozobot can follow in 60 seconds. Create a path and include that number of OzoCodes in your design.

2. Run Ozobot to see how many of the codes it reads and properly follows in 60 seconds.

3. Follow steps 1-2 to create more paths. See if you can come up with more accurate estimates and get Ozobot to follow even more codes in 60 seconds.

Time Trial Reflections

1. What was the plan for designing your 1st path?

2. How many codes did Ozobot read and follow?

3. What changes did you make to your path or codes in order to get Ozobot to finish more commands?

4. Talk to other groups. Whose Ozobot followed the most codes? How was that group’s path similar or different from your best path?

Record your work in images and words.
