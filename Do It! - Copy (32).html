Do It!

Your challenge is to design, build, test, and evaluate machines that move. Then design your own moving machine, and compare its performance to the basic model.

Test the Basic Machine

Before you design a better car or crane, test the basic version that you just built.            

Pick one of the following tests:

  • Car Speed Test: How long does it take the basic car to travel 5  yards or meters?
  • Car Hill Climb Test: How steep a hill can the basic car climb without stopping? 
  • Crane Lift Test: How much weight can the basic crane lift without stopping?

Speed Test

How long does it take the basic car to travel five yards or meters? 

Decide if you are measuring in yards or meters.

  1. Set up a straight five-meter (or yard) course with both a starting and finish line. 
  2. Use a stop watch to time the car.
  3. Record your results.
  4. Repeat steps two and three several times. (NOTE: Keep the starting point for the car the same for each test. For example, if you start by placing the front wheels on the starting line, do this every time.)
  5. Save your results to compare to your own car design later on.

Hill Climb Test

What is the steepest ramp that the basic car can climb? 

  1. Build a ramp. 
  2. Record the steepness of the ramp. How might you do this? 
  3. Let the car drive up the ramp.
  4. Record the results. Was it able to climb without stopping?
  5. Change the ramp angle.
  6. Repeat your test. At what angle is the car no longer able to climb?
  7. Save your results to compare to your own car design later on.    

Crane Lift Test

How much weight can the basic crane lift? 

  1. Build a bucket for the crane.
  2. Add weight to the bucket (pennies make good weights).
  3. Record the total weight in the bucket. (Remember to include the units of measurement, e.g. grams, ounces, pennies)
  4. Turn the crane on to lift the weight.
  5. Repeat steps two, three, and four until the crane can no longer lift the weights.
  6. Record the maximum weight the crane lifts.
  7. Save your results. Compare them to the results for your own crane later on.

Design and Build a Better Machine

Now that you know how well your basic model works, you can make changes to improve it.

How will you know if your design works better than the basic design?

You already recorded how well the basic design works. You will then record how well your changed design works. You can compare the results to learn if your design works better, worse, or the same as the basic design.

Use the Engineering Design Process to guide you. Document your work for each step of the process. Go to the What You Should Know: Engineering Design Process for more information.

Pick one of the challenges below or come up with your own. 

  • Designed for Speed! Improve the basic car design to make the car move faster on a flat surface.
  • Hill Climber! Improve the basic car design to crawl up a steeper ramp.
  • Power Lifter! Improve the basic crane design to lift more weight.
