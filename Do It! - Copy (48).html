Do It!

Your challenge is to make an interactive Scratch computer game. You can add to an existing game, or plan and create your own game.

STEPS:

  1. Go through the Hide and Seek Game tutorial. (Link below.)
  2. Make creative changes to the original game.
  3. Pick another game example to change, or come up with your own game.
  4. Create a written plan of your idea.
  5. Start coding!

Hide and Seek Tutorial

To get started, complete all of the steps of the Hide and Seek Game tutorial. 

What changes can you make to the game? 

For example, consider the sprite's reaction after it is found. Or maybe more than one character can be hiding. Can you add a second sprite?

More Project Ideas

Now, find another game tutorial to practice with. Under the Explore menu option, find a game that you can learn from and modify. If you have your own game idea in mind, feel free to make a game from scratch.

If you make your own game, plan it out first. Then show it to your Facilitator.

  • What is the goal of the game?
  • What characters will it have?
  • What will each character do?
  • How will players interact with your game? 

Remember to keep your game simple. Once you get the basics to work properly, you can always add to it later.

Tips

If you are struggling to come up with ways to change or create a game, think about a computer game you enjoy playing.

  • What makes it a "good" game?
  • Is it an individual or a multi-player game?
    • How might you make a 2-player game in Scratch?
  • What type of game is it?
    • Adventure? Puzzle? Sports? Arcade? Interactive story? Educational?
  • How do you win? How can you lose?

Use your favorite game as a source of ideas for your own Scratch game.
