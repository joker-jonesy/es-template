What You Should Know

What Are Compilations?

A compilation is something made of multiple elements. In this case, your compilation will be made of multiple drawings. For example, you can add background images and move your characters around inside the frame.  When you add details to your drawings, you make the story more interesting. 

Compilation Tools and Techniques

Animation and Drawing by Do Ink uses the timeline and pathing tool to help you create your animation.

Timeline Tool

The timeline tool allows you to adjust when an element, or drawing, appears on the canvas and how long it is visible.

You can also use the playhead to see what your animation looks like at any point on the timeline.

You can see the timeline for each drawing in your compilation by clicking on the object in the canvas or by clicking on the blue triangle next to the time marker. 

Your background will take up the whole frame and should be added to your canvas first. Your other drawings will be layered on top of the background.

In a play, the actor at the front of the stage is in the foreground. The objects on the stage are in the background.

The photograph to the right has flowers in the foreground and a building in the background. 

Pathing Tool

The pathing tool lets you move a drawing around on the canvas as your animation plays.

Tap and drag the green pathing handle to create the motion path for your drawing.

When you let go, you will see the red anchor handle at the end of the path. This shows you where the drawing will stop.

If you need to edit the motion path of one element, or drawing, you can use the two editing tools in the bottom toolbar.

The first tool allows you to edit the motion path by clicking on a node (one of blue circles along the motion path) and moving it around.

The second tool allows you to add or delete nodes to the path to increase or decrease the number of curves.

Telling Stories: Parts of a Story

Before you create compilations to tell a story, consider the elements of all great stories.

  • Characters: The people, animals, or imaginary creatures in the story
  • Setting: The place where the story occurs such as a forest, city, 
  • distant planet
  • Plot: Events that happen to the characters of the story which usually includes a conflict/problem and a resolution.

The Wizard of Oz
Characters: Dorothy, Toto, Lion, Tin Man, Scarecrow, Munchkins, and The Wizard of Oz
Setting: The Magical Land of Oz
Plot: Dorothy finds herself in the magical Land of Oz and must get to the Emerald City to find a way back home to Kansas, the conflict/problem is that she needs to get back to Kansas and she has to find the Wizard of Oz to help her, the resolution is that she discovers she had the power to go home all along and clicks her magic ruby slippers together and wakes up back in Kansas.
