Do It! Kodu: Terrain Area

 The Challenge

Discover how to program as you create your first computer game with Kodu. Your challenge is to create terrain in Kodu and use multiplication and tiling to find its area.

When you make a computer game, you are writing a computer program.

Your program tells the computer how your game is played.

What will you program Kodu to do?

 Project Steps

  1. First Game

  2. Program Your Game to Keep Score

  3. Build Your Terrain

First Game

  1. Load Shooting Fish world.
  2. Add a Kodu object.
  3. When: spacebar pressed - Do: shoot a missile
  4. When: left arrow key is pressed - Do: turn left
  5. When: right arrow key is pressed - Do: turn right
  6. Save your game.

Program Your Game to Keep Score

Were you able to program Kodu to shoot fish?

If so, then you are ready to take your game to the next level. Most games like Shooting Fish keep score. You can program your game to keep score too. Watch the video on the right.

Score Tutorial

The Score Tutorial is another way to learn how to program your game to keep score. This lesson takes you step-by-step through programming a scoring rule.

STEPS:

  1. Go to the Home menu.
  2. Click Load World.
  3. Select Lessons.
  4. Find the Score Tutorial and follow along.

Now try it with your Shooting Fish game. You can keep score any way you want. You can choose how many points for each fish. You can set a point goal to win the game or a time limit when the game ends. You can assign different point values for different color fish or subtract points for hitting the wrong colors. It is up to you.

Build Your Terrain

Now it’s time for you to build your terrain in Kodu. Add land, mountains, hills, or sky to create your own world. 

After you have finished building your terrain, you need to find the area of the land.

  1. Step 1: Sketch out an aerial view of your world. This can be done either on paper or you can take a screenshot of your world in Kodu by rotating the camera view and zooming out.
  2. Step 2: Add labels to your aerial view showing the key features of your terrain. Outline the shapes that you will use to calculate the area of your world.
  3. Step 3: Then, estimate the area of land. Remember: to find the area, you can use tiling or multiplication. Be sure to include your area calculations.

When you are finished, take a screenshot of your land.
