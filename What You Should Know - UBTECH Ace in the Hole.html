What You Should Know - UKIT Beginner: Ace in the Hole

Important Vocabulary

  • Angle - a figure made when two lines originate from a single point which is measured in degrees
  • Protractor - a mathematical tool that is used to measure angles
  • Ray - a line drawn with a circle at one end and an arrow at the other that indicates its direction
  • Vertex -  the point where two rays meet
  • Claim- Evidence Reasoning (CER) - a written scientific explanation

Angles

When two rays start from one single point, they create an angle. The word angle comes from a Latin word that means corner and indeed, a corner is a great example - two walls meet at a single point and create an angle. The two rays that make up the angle are called the sides and the place where they meet is called the vertex.

The opening between the two sides is the angle that is measured. The size of the angle depends on how wide or narrow that space is.  All angles are measured in degrees (and use the symbol °).  23° would read, “twenty three degrees”.

Protractor

A protractor is a tool that measures an angle with reference to a circle. In the example below the angle measures at 130°. 

To use a protractor to measure angles:

  • Line up the vertex (where the rays meet) at the center dot on the protractor.
  • Line up one side of the angle with 0° on the protractor.
  • Read the protractor to see where the angle crosses the number scale.
  • The angle is 130°.

Protractors often have two number scales for measuring. If there are two number scales, how do you know which one to use? Look to see if the angle is lined up with the inner number scale or the outer number scale. In this case, the protractor is lined up with the 0 on the inner scale. The angle measures 50°.

Claims-Evidence-Reasoning

Claim- Evidence Reasoning (CER) is a way of writing a scientific explanation. A Claim is what you know, Evidence is how you know it and Reasoning is your thought process.  For example is you think the donuts at Donuts-R-Us are the best donuts in your town, your CER to defend this claim might look like this:

Claim: Donuts from Donuts-R-Us are the best donuts in my town.

Evidence: There is always a long line of people waiting to buy donuts. They sell out of their donuts within 2 hours of opening. 95% of the people who buy the donuts have bought the donuts before. The other store in town, Bargain Donuts, only sells donuts that are prepackaged and there is never a line to buy them. When surveyed, 97% of the people at Bargain Donuts said they had never bought them before and would not buy them again. Additionally, when checked at closing time, 50% of the donuts from the morning were still sitting on the shelves at Bargain Donuts. 

Reasoning: Because so many people are repeat buyers at Donuts R Us and since they sell out very quickly every morning, it indicates  that the donuts are very good. Since 97% of the donut buyers at Bargain Donuts would not buy there again, and since over 50% of their donuts are still sitting on the shelf at closing time, the donuts must not be very good.

While we won’t be comparing donuts in this activity, you will be answering the question: Does the angle of the robotic golf club swing make a difference as to how far the ping pong ball travels? You will make a claim that is specific and accurate, supporting that claim by giving evidence (facts) that you have collected through your experiment, and sharing your reasoning to explain how the evidence connects to the claim. You will upload your CER in your project submission.

Career Connection and Real-World Application

While computers have made it easier to create detailed drawings and building plans, it is important for many different occupations to have the skills to draw them by hand to better understand their jobs.

Athletes

Athletes need to understand angles to enhance their performance on the field to plan and improve their throws, kick a soccer ball, choose the angle you push or pull a weight, etc.

Surveyors

People who measure the plots of land for governments, builders, architects and engineers are called surveyors. Their job is to precisely measure the angles and area of the places they survey. They must provide very accurate calculations about the geography of the places they survey.

Architects

Architects use protractors to measure building designs and to make sure the buildings are safe and can withstand the strain the architect anticipates the building will undergo after it is built
