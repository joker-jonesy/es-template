 More Division

How could you divide whole numbers by fractions that are not unit fractions? Look for examples of fractions in other levels of the game. Create an equation that divides a whole number by a fraction.

Let’s look at an example: 4÷⅔. Look at the diagram below that represents 4 wholes. Each whole is split into thirds, with two of them shaded in. How many groups of ⅔ are in 4 wholes? Each of the wholes show 1 group of ⅔ (green shaded sections), for a total of 4 groups of ⅔. But if we look at the remaining pieces (gray shaded sections), we can make an additional 2 groups of ⅔. There are 6 total groups of ⅔, so 4÷⅔=6.
