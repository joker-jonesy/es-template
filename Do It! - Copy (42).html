Do It!

Your challenge is to build as many basic 2D and 3D shapes as you can with Zometool. Then, you will build a 3D model of a structure or object you can find in your environment. In the process, you will explore the properties of 2D and 3D shapes. 

Your Task

  • Start by exploring the different shapes found in your environment.
  • Build 2D shapes with Zometool.
  • Use your 2D shapes to build 3D shapes. 
  • Take a photograph of each shape you build for your ePortfolio.
  • Measure and describe your shapes. 

Exploring Shapes

Look around at the objects in your SmartLab.

Work with your partner to list some of the shapes you see. Which shapes can you find?

Find a 2D shape. How do you know it is a 2D shape?

Find a 3D shape. How do you know it is a 3D shape?

If you are unsure, read What Is a 2D Shape? and What Is a 3D Shape? in the What You Should Know section.

Take pictures and write about your objects in your ePortfolio. List any similarities and differences between the shapes.

 Build 2D Shapes

Now that you have explored shapes found in your SmartLab, you are ready for your first challenge. Use Zometool to build 2D polygons.

Here are some things to try:

  • Make shapes with different numbers of sides. What is the smallest number of sides you need to make a polygon?
  • Keep all the sides the same length. Which polygons can you make with the sides all the same length?
  • Use sides of different lengths. Which shapes can you make now?

  

Describe Your 2D Shapes

Describe some of your 2D shapes.

  • Take a photograph of each shape you build.
  • Make a list of the parts you used for each polygon. (Another group should be able to use your lists to collect the parts needed to create the same polygons.)  
  • Which struts work well for each shape?
  • Measure and record the height and width of your shape. (Remember your units.)
  • Name your shape.

Build 3D Shapes

Now it is time to turn your 2D shape into a 3D shape. 

Pick several of your 2D shapes and use them as a starting point for a new 3D shape. For example, you can use a 2D triangle and build a 3D pyramid.

Describe Your 3D Shapes

What 3D shapes did you make with Zometool? Describe some of your 3D shapes.

  • Take a photograph of each shape you build.
  • Make a list of the parts you used; include struts and nodes.
  • Measure and record the height, width, and depth of your shape.
  • Name your shape. 
    • Some 3D shapes have official names – like the cube you see here.
    • Use the Internet to see if there is a name for your shape. If not, name it yourself!

Create a Model from Your Environment

Look for 3D shapes around you for examples. Try copying shapes from the natural and designed world.

Use the Internet to look up building shapes, sculpture shapes, or other shapes found in man-made objects. You can also look up interesting rock formations, ice structures, or other shapes of nature.
