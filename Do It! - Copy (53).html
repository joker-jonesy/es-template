Do It!

Build a solar station and then a solar car. Then, see if you can improve your solar car design.

Solar Challenge

Before you build your car, build a solar station so you can see how photovoltaic (PV) panels collect energy, and how the energy meter measures and stores energy. Review the sections of What You Should Know that cover the Energy Meter so you know how to read the data. Download the solar station instructions here. 

Tip: You will need a light source. Direct sunlight is best, but if you need to stay indoors, ask your facilitator for the gooseneck lamp that comes with this kit.

Testing the Solar Station

Once you try out your solar station, predict what would happen to the meter readings if you make changes to the PV panel. Then, test the changes to see if you were right. You can use the tables in the Solar Station Worksheet for recording data. Through repeated testing, see if you can find the answers to these questions:

1. What happens to the meter readings when you set the PV panel to different angles? 

2. What happens to the meter readings when you move the solar station closer to the light source? Farther?

3. What happens when you use a different kind of light? (e.g. the sun, an overhead light, a lamp with different kinds of bulbs).

Build Your Solar Car

The Solar Station is an example of stored energy that can be used at a later time. Now you will get to explore how direct energy can be used to power a car.

Build your solar car using the Solar Car Instruction Manual Once it is built, test it under a light source. Try making changes like using different light sources, or driving on different surfaces (e.g. carpet, flat surface, incline). Remember to record your observations.

Record Your Observations

Use the questions below to guide yours data collection: 

1. When did your car work best? What were the conditions? (e.g. light source, brightness, floor surface)

2. Was your car ever slow or unable to move? If so, what were the conditions?

3. How could you improve your solar car? Write your ideas down in your ePortfolio so you can brainstorm design changes and solutions.

Time for Changes

Now that you…

  • Learned how to use the energy meter
  • Built your solar car
  • Made observations
  • Came up with ideas for improvement

Try out some of your improvements. Do your changes make your car faster, more reliable, more useful, or better in some other way?

If you need ideas for changes, you can use the Solar Car Worksheet. 

Do not forget to record your new observations. Take pictures and video and write a conclusion that reviews what you learned.
